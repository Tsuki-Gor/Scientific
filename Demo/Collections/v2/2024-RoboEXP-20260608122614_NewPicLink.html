                                                                                                                                Abstract: We introduce the novel task of interactive scene exploration, wherein robots autonomously explore environments and produce an action-conditioned scene graph (ACSG) that captures the structure of the underlying environment. The ACSG accounts for both low-level information (geometry and semantics) and high-level information (action-conditioned relationships between different entities) in the scene. To this end, we present the Robotic Exploration (RoboEXP) system, which incorporates the Large Multimodal Model (LMM) and an explicit memory design to enhance our system's capabilities. The robot reasons about what and how to explore an object, accumulating new information through the interaction process and incrementally constructing the ACSG. Leveraging the constructed ACSG, we illustrate the effectiveness and efficiency of our RoboEXP system in facilitating a wide range of real-world manipulation tasks involving rigid, articulated objects, nested objects, and deformable objects. Project Page: https://jianghanxiao.github.io/roboexp-web/ 摘要：我们提出交互式场景探索这一新任务，其中机器人自主探索环境，并生成动作条件场景图（ACSG），以刻画底层环境的结构。ACSG同时考虑场景中的低层信息（几何与语义）和高层信息（不同实体之间的动作条件关系）。为此，我们提出 RoboEXP（Robotic Exploration）系统，将大规模多模态模型（LMM）与显式记忆设计相结合，以增强系统能力。机器人推理应探索什么以及如何探索某个物体，通过交互过程积累新信息，并逐步构建 ACSG。借助所构建的 ACSG，我们展示了 RoboEXP 系统在支持广泛真实世界操作任务中的有效性与高效性，这些任务涉及刚性物体、可动铰接物体、嵌套物体和可变形物体。项目主页：https://jianghanxiao.github.io/roboexp-web/

                                                                                                                                1 Introduction

                                                                                                                                1 引言

                                                                                                                                Imagine a household robot designed to prepare breakfast. This robot must perform various tasks such as conducting inventory checks in cabinets, fetching food from the fridge, gathering utensils from drawers, and spotting leftovers under food covers. Key to its success is the ability to interact with and explore the environment, especially to find items that aren't immediately visible. Equipping it with such capabilities is crucial for the robot to effectively complete its everyday tasks. Robot exploration and active perception have long been challenging areas in robotics. Various techniques have been proposed, including information-theoretic approaches [1-7], frontier-based methods [8-10], imitation learning [11, 12] and reinforcement learning [13-17]. Nevertheless, previous research has primarily focused on exploring static environments by merely changing viewpoints in a navigation setting. 想象一台被设计用来准备早餐的家用机器人。它必须执行多种任务，例如检查橱柜中的库存、从冰箱取食物、从抽屉里收集餐具，以及发现食物罩下的剩余食物。其成功的关键在于能够与环境交互并进行探索，尤其是发现那些无法直接看到的物品。赋予它这种能力对机器人高效完成日常任务至关重要。机器人探索与主动感知长期以来都是机器人领域的挑战性研究方向。人们已提出多种技术，包括信息论方法[1-7]、前沿基方法[8-10]、模仿学习[11, 12]和强化学习[13-17]。然而，先前研究主要关注在导航场景下仅通过改变视角来探索静态环境。

                                                                                                                                In this work, we investigate the interactive scene exploration task, where the goal is to efficiently identify all objects, including those that are directly observable and those that can only be discovered through interaction between the robot and the environment (Fig. 1). Towards this goal, we present a novel scene representation called action-conditioned 3D scene graph (ACSG). Unlike conventional 3D scene graphs that focus on encoding static relations, ACSG encodes both spatial relationships and logical associations indicative of action effects (e.g., opening a fridge will reveal an apple inside). We then show that our task can be formulated as a problem of ACSG construction and traversal. 在这项工作中，我们研究交互式场景探索任务，其目标是高效识别所有物体，包括可直接观察到的物体，以及只能通过机器人与环境交互才能发现的物体（图1）。为实现这一目标，我们提出一种新型场景表示，称为动作条件三维场景图（ACSG）。不同于侧重编码静态关系的传统三维场景图，ACSG同时编码空间关系和表明动作效果的逻辑关联（例如，打开冰箱会露出里面的苹果）。随后我们表明，该任务可被表述为ACSG构建与遍历问题。

                                                                                                                                To tackle the interactive scene exploration task, we propose a novel, real-world robotic exploration framework, the RoboEXP system. At the core of our system is a large foundational model-powered instantiation of action-conditioned 3D scene graph. Specifically, our framework consists of four modules: perception, memory, decision-making, and action (Fig. 3). 为解决交互式场景探索任务，我们提出一种新颖的真实机器人探索框架RoboEXP系统。该系统的核心是由大基础模型驱动的动作条件三维场景图实例化。具体而言，我们的框架由四个模块组成：感知、记忆、决策和动作（图3）。

                                                                                                                                RoboEXP can handle diverse exploration tasks in a zero-shot manner, constructing complex action-conditioned 3D scene graph in various scenarios, including those involving obstructing objects and requiring multi-step reasoning (Fig. 2). We evaluate our system across various settings , demonstrating its adaptability and robustness. The system also effectively manages different human interventions. Moreover, we show that our reconstructed action-conditioned 3D scene graph demonstrates strong capacity in performing multiple complex downstream tasks. Action-conditioned 3D scene graph advances LLM/LMM-guided robotic manipulation and decision-making research [18, 19], extending their operation domain from environments with known or observable objects to complicated environments with unknown or unobserved ones. To our knowledge, this is the first of its kind. RoboEXP能够以零样本方式处理多样化的探索任务，在包括存在遮挡物和需要多步推理的各种场景中构建复杂的动作条件三维场景图（图2）。我们在多种设置下评估了该系统，展示了其适应性和鲁棒性。该系统也能有效应对不同的人类干预。此外，我们展示了所重建的动作条件三维场景图在执行多个复杂下游任务时具有强大能力。动作条件三维场景图推动了LLM/LMM引导的机器人操作与决策研究[18, 19]，将其运行范围从已知或可观测物体的环境扩展到包含未知或不可观测物体的复杂环境。据我们所知，这在同类工作中尚属首次。

                                                                                                                                2 Related Works

                                                                                                                                2 相关工作

                                                                                                                                Scene Graphs [20, 21] represent objects and their relations [22-24] in a scene via a graph structure. Previous studies generate scene graphs from images [21, 25, 26] or 3D scenes [27], and further with the assistance of large language models (LLMs) [28, 29]. While previous works model scene graphs in static 2D or 3D scenes, we generate action-conditioned scene graphs that integrate actions as core elements, depicting interactive relationships between objects and actions. Our work is also related to Neuro-Symbolic Representations that integrate neural networks with symbolic reasoning. Prior works explored understanding scenes and describing robotic skills in symbolic texts to interpret demonstrations [30, 31], ground abstract actions for robotic primitives [32] and generate action plans [33-38]. Our proposed framework also constructs symbolic representations of the environment, but in the form of action-conditioned scene graphs for robotic manipulation. 场景图[20,21]通过图结构表示场景中的对象及其关系[22-24]。先前研究从图像[21,25,26]或3D场景[27]生成场景图，并进一步借助大语言模型（LLMs）[28,29]。尽管以往工作建模的是静态2D或3D场景中的场景图，我们生成将动作作为核心要素的动作条件场景图，刻画对象与动作之间的交互关系。我们的工作也与神经符号表示相关，该方向将神经网络与符号推理相结合。此前研究探索了以符号文本理解场景并描述机器人技能，以解释示范[30,31]，将抽象动作落地为机器人原语[32]并生成动作计划[33-38]。我们提出的框架也构建了环境的符号表示，但其形式是用于机器人操作的动作条件场景图。

                                                                                                                                Robotic Exploration aims to autonomously navigate [9,11,39−45] ,interact with [12,46,46−50] , and gather information [51-53] from environments it has never encountered before. The primary guiding principle behind robotic exploration is to reduce the uncertainty of the environment [5- 7, 39, 54, 55], making uncertainty quantification key for robotic exploration tasks. Curiosity-driven exploration has recently emerged as a promising approach, showing effective results in various contexts [16, 56-58]. Recently, exploration has also been studied in the context of manipulation [59-66], aiming to better understand the scene by changing the state of the environment. Our work introduces a new active exploration strategy for manipulation, uniquely defining a novel scene graph-guided objective to guide the exploration process. Our work is also related to Active Perception, which actively selects actions for an agent to help it perceive and understand the environment [1, 67]. Unlike passive perception, actions offer more flexibility, such as control over viewpoints [2-4, 68], sensor configurations [15, 69], or adjustments to environmental configurations [70]. It can also reveal certain scene properties that cannot be perceived in a passive manner, such as dynamic parameters [16, 71] or articulation [62, 72, 73]. Our work falls into the category of actively exploring the environment to reveal what's inside or underneath objects. Differing from most previous active perception efforts, which are driven by handcrafted rules [74], information gain [75, 76], or reinforcement learning [16, 77], our approach is guided by grounding the commonsense knowledge encoded in a LLM/LMM into an explicit scene graph representation. 机器人探索旨在自主导航[9,11,39−45]、与[12,46,46−50]交互，并从其从未遇到过的环境中收集信息[51-53]。机器人探索的主要指导原则是降低环境的不确定性[5-7,39,54,55]，因此不确定性量化是机器人探索任务的关键。受好奇心驱动的探索近年来成为一种很有前景的方法，并在多种场景中展现出有效结果[16,56-58]。最近，探索也被研究用于操作任务[59-66]，旨在通过改变环境状态来更好地理解场景。我们的工作引入了一种新的主动探索策略用于操作，并独特地定义了一种新颖的场景图引导目标来指导探索过程。我们的工作也与主动感知相关，后者会主动为智能体选择动作，以帮助其感知并理解环境[1,67]。不同于被动感知，动作提供了更大的灵活性，例如对视角的控制[2-4,68]、传感器配置[15,69]，或对环境配置的调整[70]。它还可以揭示某些无法通过被动方式感知的场景属性，例如动态参数[16,71]或可动性[62,72,73]。我们的工作属于主动探索环境以揭示物体内部或下方内容的范畴。不同于大多数以手工规则[74]、信息增益[75,76]或强化学习[16,77]驱动的以往主动感知工作，我们的方法通过将LLM/LMM中编码的常识知识落地为显式场景图表示来进行引导。

                                                                                                                                Language Models for Robotics. Large language models (LLMs) [78-80] and large multimodality models (LMMs) [81, 82] are bringing overwhelming influence into the robotics field, for their strong capacity in common-sense knowledge and task-level planning. Previous studies have harnessed the common-sense knowledge of such large models to generate action candidates [83-85] and action sequences for task planning [80, 86-88], and generate code for robotic control and manipulation [18, 89, 90]. More recently, VILA [19] utilized GPT-4V [81, 82] for vision-language planning. In our RoboEXP system, we leverage GPT-4V for decision-making in two crucial roles to select and verify actions. Moreover, instead of memorizing everything using large models in a brute force way, our system employs explicit memory to enhance the decision-making process. 机器人领域的大语言模型。大语言模型（LLMs）[78-80]和大规模多模态模型（LMMs）[81,82]凭借其强大的常识知识和任务级规划能力，正对机器人领域产生巨大影响。此前研究已利用此类大模型的常识知识生成动作候选[83-85]和用于任务规划的动作序列[80,86-88]，以及生成用于机器人控制与操作的代码[18,89,90]。最近，VILA[19]利用GPT-4V[81,82]进行视觉-语言规划。在我们的RoboEXP系统中，我们利用GPT-4V进行决策，在选择和验证动作这两个关键角色中发挥作用。此外，我们的系统并非通过粗暴地用大模型记住一切，而是采用显式记忆来增强决策过程。

                                                                                                                                3 Action-Conditioned 3D Scene Graph

                                                                                                                                3 基于动作条件的三维场景图

                                                                                                                                An action-conditioned 3D scene graph (ACSG) is an actionable, spatial-topological representation that models objects and their interactive and spatial relations in a scene. Formally, ACSG is a directed acyclic graph G=(V,E) where each node represents either an object or an action,and edges E represent their interaction relations. The object node oi=(si,pi)∈V encodes the semantics si and geometry pi of each object,whereas the action node ak=(ak,Tk)∈V encodes high-level action type ak and low-level primitives Tk to perform the actions. Between the nodes are edges encoding their relations,which we categorize into four types: 1) between objects eo→o (e.g.,the door handle belongs to the fridge), 2) from objects to actions eo→a (e.g., toy can be picked up), 3) from action to objects ea→o (e.g.,a banana can be reached if we open the cabinet),or 4) from one action to another ea→a (e.g.,the cabinet can be opened only if we move away the condiment). Our action-conditioned 3D scene graph greatly enhances existing 3D scene graphs, as it explicitly models the action-conditioned relations between objects. Fig. 2 depicts a complete action-conditioned 3D scene graph of a tabletop scene. 基于动作条件的三维场景图（ACSG）是一种可执行、空间—拓扑的表示，用于在场景中建模物体及其交互与空间关系。形式化地，ACSG 是一张有向无环图 G=(V,E)，其中每个节点表示一个物体或一个动作，边 E 表示它们的交互关系。物体节点 oi=(si,pi)∈V 编码每个物体的语义 si 与几何 pi，而动作节点 ak=(ak,Tk)∈V 编码要执行的高层动作类型 ak 以及低层原语 Tk。节点之间的边编码它们之间的关系，我们将其归为四类：1）物体之间 eo→o（例如，门把手属于冰箱），2）从物体到动作 eo→a（例如，玩具可以被拿起），3）从动作到物体 ea→o（例如，若我们打开柜子，就能够够到香蕉），以及 4）从一个动作到另一个动作 ea→a（例如，只有在我们移开调味品之后，柜子才能被打开）。我们的基于动作条件的三维场景图极大增强了现有的三维场景图，因为它显式建模了物体之间的动作条件关系。图 2 展示了一个桌面场景的完整基于动作条件的三维场景图。

                                                                                                                                One advantage of our ACSG lies in its simplicity for retrieving and taking actions on an object. Regardless of how complicated the scene is, given our scene graph and a target object, an agent merely needs to sequentially execute all the actions on the paths from the root to the object node in a topological order to retrieve the object. 我们的 ACSG 的一个优势在于：对物体检索并执行动作更为简单。无论场景多么复杂，给定我们的场景图与目标物体，智能体只需按照拓扑顺序，依次在从根节点到物体节点的路径上执行所有动作，即可检索该物体。

                                                                                                                                Interactive Exploration. Constructing a complete ACSG of a real-world scene is challenging due to partial observability. To solve this task, we formulate the scene graph construction as an active perception and exploration problem using POMDP-inspired notations. Formally, at each time t , based on our past graph estimation Gt−1 ,and past sensor observations Ot−1 ,our agent takes an action At ,which causes the environment to transition to a new state,and the agent receives a new observation Ot ,which is used to update its current inferred graph Gt . This update might include adding new nodes to the graph or updating the state of an existing node. We will then continue with exploration and keep updating the set of remaining unexplored nodes U⊂V (See the Appendix for the full algorithm). The goal of the exploration is simple: discover and explore all the nodes of the scene graph in as little time as possible. We thus formulate a reward function with three terms: Rt=R graph t+R explore t+R time t ,where R graph t=|Vt|−|Vt−1| promotes our agent to discover as many nodes as possible to the graph, R explore t=max(0,|Ut−1|−|Ut|) gives a positive reward to actions that reduce the unexplored node set, which prioritizes the agent to explore previously unexplored nodes (note that an action can lead to the discovery of new unexplored nodes being added to the graph),and immediate reward R time t=−λ,0<λ<1 is a negative time reward that enhances efficiency and terminate the exploration when there is no more node to explore. 交互式探索。由于部分可观测性，构建真实场景的完整 AC SG 很困难。为解决该任务，我们将场景图构建表述为一个受 POMDP 启发的主动感知与探索问题。形式上，在每个时间 t ，基于我们过去的图估计 Gt−1 以及过去的传感器观测 Ot−1 ，我们的智能体采取一个动作 At ，从而使环境转移到新的状态，智能体获得新的观测 Ot ，并用它来更新其当前推断的图 Gt 。该更新可能包括向图中添加新节点，或更新现有节点的状态。随后我们将继续探索，并持续更新仍未被探索的节点集合 U⊂V （完整算法见附录）。探索的目标很简单：尽可能在最短时间内发现并探索场景图中的所有节点。因此，我们设计了一个包含三个部分的奖励函数：Rt=R graph t+R explore t+R time t ，其中 R graph t=|Vt|−|Vt−1| 促使智能体尽可能多地发现并加入图中的节点，R explore t=max(0,|Ut−1|−|Ut|) 对那些能够缩小未探索节点集合的动作给予正奖励，从而优先探索先前尚未探索的节点（注意，一个动作可能会导致发现新的未探索节点并将其加入图中），即时奖励 R time t=−λ,0<λ<1 是一个负的时间奖励，用于提升效率，并在不再有节点可探索时终止探索。

                                                                                                                                Intuitively, to maximize this reward at each discrete timestamp, we should prioritize exploring the unexplored nodes in the current scene graph that are likely to lead to the discovery of new nodes (e.g., opening a cabinet that has not been opened, or lifting a piece of clothing that might cover a small object). The key challenge lies in how we can perceive the objects in the scene, infer possible actions and their relations from the sensory data, and take actions with the current scene graph. In the next section, we will comprehensively describe our system implementation to achieve this goal. 直观地说，为了在每个离散时间戳最大化该奖励，我们应优先探索当前场景图中那些很可能带来新节点发现的未探索节点（例如，打开一个尚未打开的柜子，或抬起一件可能遮住小物体的衣物）。关键挑战在于：我们如何从场景中的感知信息中识别物体，从传感数据中推断可能的动作及其关系，并在当前场景图的基础上执行动作。下一节中，我们将全面描述我们的系统实现，以实现这一目标。

                                                                                                                                4 Interactive Robotic Exploration (RoboEXP) System

                                                                                                                                4 交互式机器人探索（RoboEXP）系统

                                                                                                                                In this section, we outline the structure of our RoboEXP system, including perception, memory, decision-making, and action modules (See the Appendix for full details of our implementation). 在本节中，我们将概述 RoboEXP 系统的结构，包括感知、记忆、决策和行动模块（实现的完整细节请参见附录）。

                                                                                                                                Perception Module. Given multiple RGBD observations from different viewpoints, the objective of the perception module (Fig. 3a) is to detect and segment objects while extracting their semantic features. To enhance generality, we opt for the open-vocabulary detector GroundingDINO [91] and the Segment Anything in High Quality (SAM-HQ) [92], with a predefined list of tags. For the extraction of semantic features used in subsequent instance merging, we employ per-instance CLIP [93], following a similar strategy to the one proposed by Jatavallabhula et al. [94]. 感知模块。给定来自不同视角的多路 RGBD 观测，感知模块（图 3a）的目标是检测并分割物体，同时提取其语义特征。为增强通用性，我们采用开放词汇检测器 GroundingDINO [91] 和高质量分割（SAM-HQ）[92]，并使用预定义的标签列表。用于后续实例融合的语义特征提取，我们为每个实例使用 CLIP [93]，遵循与 Jatavallabhula 等人 [94] 所提出的类似策略。

                                                                                                                                Memory Module. The memory module (Fig. 3b) handles merging across different viewpoints and time steps. To merge across different viewpoints, we project 2D information to 3D and leverage the instance merging strategy similar to Lu et al. [95] to attain consistent 3D information. Addressing memory updates across time steps presents a challenge due to dynamic changes in the environment. For instance, a closed door in the previous time step may be opened by our robot in the current time step. To accurately reflect such changes, our algorithm evaluates whether elements within our memory have become outdated, primarily through depth tests based on the most recent observations. This process ensures that the low-level memory accurately represents the environment's current state, effectively managing scenarios where objects may change positions or states across different time steps. For the high-level graph of our ACSG, the memory module analyzes the relationships between objects and the logical associations between actions and objects. Depending on changes in low-level memory and relationships, the memory module is tasked with updating the graph. This involves adding, deleting, or modifying nodes and edges within our graph. 记忆模块。记忆模块（图 3b）负责跨不同视角与时间步长的融合。为实现跨视角融合，我们将二维信息投影到三维，并借鉴 Lu 等人 [95] 类似的实例融合策略，以获得一致的三维信息。处理跨时间步长的记忆更新带来挑战，因为环境会动态变化。例如，上一时间步中的关闭门可能会在当前时间步被我们的机器人打开。为准确反映这些变化，我们的算法会评估记忆中的元素是否已过时，主要通过基于最新观测的深度测试来完成。该过程确保底层记忆能够准确表征环境的当前状态，从而有效应对物体在不同时间步中可能出现位置或状态变化的情形。对于我们 ACSG 的高层图，记忆模块分析物体之间的关系，以及动作与物体之间的逻辑关联。根据底层记忆与关系的变化，记忆模块负责更新图结构。这包括在图中添加、删除或修改节点与边。

                                                                                                                                Decision-Making Module. The primary goal of the decision module (Fig. 3c) is to identify the appropriate object and corresponding skill to enhance the effectiveness and efficiency of interactive scene exploration. In the context of our task, distinct objects may necessitate distinct exploration strategies. While humans can easily discern the most suitable skill to apply (e.g., picking up the top Matryoshka doll to inspect its contents), achieving such decisions through heuristic-based methods is challenging. The LMM brings commonsense knowledge to our decision-making process and serves in two pivotal roles. Firstly, it functions as an action proposer. Given the current digital environment from the memory module, GPT-4V is tasked with selecting the appropriate skill for unexplored objects in our system. Secondly, the LMM also serves as the action verifier. For the proposer role, it analyzes the object-centric attributes but ignore surrounding information when choosing the proper skill. To address this, we use another LMM program to verify the feasibility of the action and identify any objects in the scene that may impede the action based on information from our ACSG. 决策模块。决策模块（图 3c）的主要目标，是识别合适的物体及其对应的技能，以提升交互式场景探索的有效性与效率。在我们的任务中，不同的物体可能需要不同的探索策略。人类可以轻松判断最合适的技能（例如拿起最上层的套娃来检查其内容），但通过基于启发式的方法实现此类决策很困难。LMM 为我们的决策过程引入常识知识，并承担两项关键作用。首先，它充当动作提议者。给定来自记忆模块的当前数字环境，GPT-4V 被要求为系统中尚未探索的物体选择合适的技能。其次，LMM 也充当动作验证者。对于提议者角色，它会分析以物体为中心的属性，但在选择合适技能时会忽略周围信息。为解决这一问题，我们使用另一个 LMM 程序来验证动作的可行性，并基于我们 ACSG 中的信息找出场景中可能阻碍该动作的任何物体。

                                                                                                                                Action Module. In the action module (Fig. 3d), our primary focus is on autonomously constructing the ACSG through effective and efficient interaction. We design a set of action primitives that operate on the low-level geometric information embedded within our ACSG. These primitives encompass seven categories: "open the door", "open the drawer", "close the door", "close the drawer", "pick object to idle space", "pick back object", "move wrist camera to position". Strategic utilization of these skills plays a pivotal role in accomplishing intricate tasks seamlessly within our system. 行动模块。在行动模块（图 3d）中，我们的主要关注点是通过有效且高效的交互，自主构建 ACSG。我们设计了一组动作原语，它们在嵌入于 ACSG 的底层几何信息上运行。这些原语涵盖七个类别：“打开门”“打开抽屉”“关闭门”“关闭抽屉”“把物体拿到空闲空间”“把物体拿回去”“移动腕部相机到指定位置”。在系统中对这些技能的策略性使用，是顺利完成复杂任务的关键。

                                                                                                                                6 Limitations and Future Work

                                                                                                                                6 局限性与未来工作

                                                                                                                                Although our system has proven effective, there is room for improvement. The breakdown of the failure rate in Fig. 4.b suggests that failures primarily arise from detection and segmentation errors within the perception module. To address this issue, we envision two future directions: 1) enhancing the capabilities of visual foundation models for open-world semantic understanding, and 2) utilizing temporal cues and semantic fusion techniques to improve perception robustness through continuous observations. Furthermore, our system would benefit from enhanced LMM capacities and the integration of more sophisticated and generalizable skill modules. Such improvements would enhance both the decision-making and the action execution, thereby further reducing failure cases. 尽管我们的系统已被证明有效，仍有改进空间。图4.b中的失败率分解表明，失败主要源于感知模块中的检测和分割错误。为解决这一问题，我们设想两个未来方向：1）提升视觉基础模型在开放世界语义理解方面的能力；2）利用时间线索和语义融合技术，通过连续观测提高感知鲁棒性。此外，我们的系统还可受益于更强的LMM能力，以及更复杂且更具泛化性的技能模块的集成。这些改进将同时增强决策和动作执行，从而进一步减少失败案例。

                                                                                                                                7 Conclusion

                                                                                                                                7 结论

                                                                                                                                We introduced RoboEXP, a foundation-model-driven robotic exploration framework capable of effectively identifying all objects in a complex scene, both directly observable and those revealed through interaction. Central to our system is action-conditioned 3D scene graph, an advanced 3D scene graph that goes beyond traditional models by explicitly modeling interactive relations between objects. Experiments have shown RoboEXP's superior performance in interactive scene exploration across various challenging scenarios, significantly outperforming a strong GPT4V-based baseline. Notably, the reconstructed action-conditioned 3D scene graph is crucial for guiding complex downstream manipulation tasks, like setting up the table in a mock environment with fridges, cabinets, and drawer sets. Our system and its action-conditioned scene graph lay the groundwork for practical robotic deployment in complex settings, especially in environments like households and offices, facilitating their integration into everyday human activities. 我们提出了 RoboEXP，这是一种基于基础模型的机器人探索框架，能够有效识别复杂场景中的所有物体，包括直接可见的物体以及通过交互所揭示的物体。我们的系统核心是动作条件化的三维场景图，这是一种先进的三维场景图：它通过明确建模物体之间的交互关系，超越了传统模型。实验表明，在多种具有挑战性的场景中，RoboEXP 在交互式场景探索方面表现出色，显著优于一个强大的基于 GPT4V 的基线。尤其是，重建得到的动作条件化三维场景图对于引导复杂的下游机械操作任务至关重要，例如在一个模拟环境中布置餐桌，环境中包含冰箱、橱柜和抽屉组。我们的系统及其动作条件化场景图为在复杂环境中进行实际机器人部署奠定了基础，尤其是在家庭和办公等场景中，从而便于它们融入日常的人类活动。

                                                                                                                                B.1.1 Perception Module

                                                                                                                                B.1.1 感知模块

                                                                                                                                Raw RGBD images are captured through the wrist camera in different viewpoints and processed by the perception modules to extract scene semantics, including object labels, 2D bounding boxes, segmentations, and semantic features. As mentioned in the main paper, to obtain per-instance CLIP features, we implement a strategy similar to the one proposed by Jatavallabhula et al. [94]. Specifically, we extend the local-global image feature merging approach by incorporating additional label text features to augment the semantic CLIP feature for each instance. Furthermore, we exclusively focus on instance-level features, disregarding pixel-level features, thereby accelerating the entire semantic feature extraction process. 通过腕部相机在不同视角采集原始 RGBD 图像，并由感知模块对其进行处理，以提取场景语义，包括物体标签、2D 边界框、分割结果和语义特征。如主论文所述，为获得逐实例的 CLIP 特征，我们实现了一种类似于 Jatavallabhula 等人 [94] 提出的策略。具体而言，我们在局部-全局图像特征融合方法的基础上，结合额外的标签文本特征，从而为每个实例增强语义 CLIP 特征。此外，我们只关注实例级特征，忽略像素级特征，以加速整个语义特征提取流程。

                                                                                                                                B.1.2 Memory Module

                                                                                                                                B.1.2 记忆模块

                                                                                                                                The memory module is designed to construct our ACSG of the environment by assimilating observations over time. For the low-level memory, to ensure stable instance merging from 2D to 3D, we employ a similar instance merging strategy as presented in Lu et al. [95], consolidating observations from diverse RGBD sources across various viewpoints and time steps. In contrast to the original algorithm, which considers only 3D IoU and semantic feature similarity we additionally incorporate label similarity and instance confidence. To enhance algorithm efficiency, we represent low-level memory using a voxel-based representation with filtering designs, which allows for more efficient computation and cleaner memory updates. Meanwhile, given the crowded nature of objects in our tabletop setting, we have implemented voxel-based filtering designs to obtain a cleaner and more complete representation of the objects for storage in our memory. 记忆模块旨在通过随时间整合观测来构建我们环境的 ACSG。对于底层记忆，为确保从 2D 到 3D 的稳定实例融合，我们采用与 Lu 等人 [95] 相同的实例融合策略：将来自不同视角与时间步的多种 RGBD 观测进行整合。与原始算法仅考虑 3D IoU 以及语义特征相似度不同，我们额外引入标签相似度和实例置信度。为提升算法效率，我们使用带过滤设计的体素化表示来表示底层记忆，从而实现更高效的计算与更清晰的记忆更新。与此同时，考虑到我们桌面场景中物体较为拥挤，我们还实现了体素化过滤设计，以获得更干净且更完整的物体表示，用于存储在记忆中。

                                                                                                                                B.1.4 Action Module

                                                                                                                                B.1.4 动作模块

                                                                                                                                The action module focuses on providing useful action primitives to aid in constructing our ACSG. We have designed seven action primitives: "open the [door]", "open the [drawer]", "close the [door]", "close the [drawer]", "pick [object] to idle space", "pick back [object]", "move wrist camera to [position]". To fully support autonomous actions, we employ a heuristic-based algorithm leveraging geometric cues. The input to each action primitive is an object node of our ACSG, from which we can extract all necessary semantic, state, and low-level geometry information of the object. The integrated information can help us determine the specific grasping pose and path planning for opening and picking actions, which generalize to different instances in various positions and poses. 动作模块专注于提供有用的动作原语，以辅助构建我们的 ACSG。我们设计了七个动作原语：“打开[门]”、“打开[抽屉]”、“关闭[门]”、“关闭[抽屉]”、“将[物体]拾取到空闲空间”、“将[物体]拾回”、“将腕部相机移动到[位置]”。为全面支持自主动作，我们采用了基于启发式、利用几何线索的算法。每个动作原语的输入都是我们 ACSG 中的一个对象节点，我们可以从中提取该对象所需的全部语义、状态和底层几何信息。整合后的信息有助于我们确定开门和拾取动作的具体抓取位姿与路径规划，并可泛化到不同位置和姿态下的不同实例。

                                                                                                                                For opening action primitives related to doors or drawers, engagement with handles is required. In our implementation, we exploit the handle's position and geometry to discern its motion type (prismatic or revolute) and motion parameters (motion axis and motion origin). Executing this action involves utilizing the detected handle and its geometry to adeptly open doors or drawers. Upon identifying the specific handle to be operated, our system retrieves the point cloud converted from our voxel-based representation corresponding to that handle from our memory module. Subsequently, we employ Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to determine the principal direction of the handle, aiding in aligning the gripper for optimal engagement. Additionally, understanding the opening direction is pivotal for effectively handling doors or drawers. To ascertain this, we analyze neighboring points and deduce the most common normal as the opening direction. The combined information of the handle direction and the opening direction provides sufficient guidance for our robot arm to grasp the handle and open the prismatic part. However, in the case of a revolute joint, the motion becomes more intricate. Therefore, we further utilize the motion parameters inferred from the geometry to simulate the evolving opening direction based on the revolute joint's opening process. This well-designed heuristic empowers our system to reliably open drawers or doors in our tabletop setting. 对于与门或抽屉相关的开启动作原语，需要与把手交互。在我们的实现中，我们利用把手的位置和几何形状来辨识其运动类型（棱柱型或转动型）及运动参数（运动轴和运动原点）。执行该动作时，会利用检测到的把手及其几何信息来灵活地打开门或抽屉。一旦识别出需要操作的具体把手，我们的系统就会从记忆模块中检索与该把手对应、由体素表示转换而来的点云。随后，我们采用主成分分析（PCA）来确定把手的主方向，从而帮助夹爪对齐以实现最佳接触。此外，理解开启方向对于有效处理门或抽屉至关重要。为此，我们分析邻近点并推断最常见的法向作为开启方向。把手方向与开启方向的组合信息足以为机器人手臂提供指引，使其抓取把手并打开棱柱关节。然而，在转动关节的情况下，运动会更加复杂。因此，我们进一步利用从几何形状中推断出的运动参数，根据转动关节的开启过程来模拟变化中的开启方向。这一精心设计的启发式方法使我们的系统能够在桌面环境中可靠地打开抽屉或门。

                                                                                                                                For the pickup-related primitives, we simplify the pickup logic to exclusively consider a top-down direction. Consequently, our focus narrows down to acquiring essential information such as the object's height and xy location. We achieve this by extracting the object's point cloud from its associated voxel-based representation. Subsequently, we pinpoint the highest points within the cloud, calculating their mean to determine the optimal pickup point. This calculated point serves as a precise reference for our gripping mechanism, facilitating the successful grasping of objects in the specified direction. 对于拾取相关的原语，我们将拾取逻辑简化为仅考虑自上而下的方向。因此，我们的重点缩小到获取物体高度和 xy 位置等关键信息。我们通过从其关联的体素表示中提取物体点云来实现这一点。随后，我们找出点云中的最高点，并计算其均值以确定最佳拾取点。该计算得到的点可作为抓取机制的精确参考，从而便于在指定方向上成功抓取物体。

                                                                                                                                Regarding viewpoint change, the primitive is parameterized with the expected pose. For example, after opening the door/drawer, to see inside, we develop the heuristic to choose the proper viewpoint from the open direction as the parameter for the primitive, allowing for the implementation of the action primitive. 对于视角变化，该原语以期望位姿作为参数。例如，在打开门/抽屉后，为了看清内部，我们设计了启发式方法，从开启方向中选择合适的视角作为该原语的参数，从而实现该动作原语的执行。

                                                                                                                                B.2 交互式探索中的其他设计

                                                                                                                                One desiderata for robot exploration is the ability to handle scenarios that necessitate multi-step or recursive reasoning. An example of this is the Matryoshka doll case, which cannot be addressed using previous one-step LLM-based code generation approaches [18, 19]. In contrast, our modular design allows agents to dynamically plan and adapt in a closed-loop manner, enabling continuous LLM-based exploration based on environmental feedback. 机器人探索的一项理想目标，是能够应对需要多步或递归推理的场景。例如，套娃问题无法通过以往基于单步的 LLM 代码生成方法来解决 [18, 19]。相较之下，我们的模块化设计使得智能体能够以闭环方式动态规划并自适应，从而基于环境反馈实现持续的基于 LLM 的探索。

                                                                                                                                To manage multi-step reasoning, our system incorporates an action stack as a simple but effective "planning" module. Guided by decisions from the decision module, the stack structure adeptly organizes the order of actions. For instance, upon picking up the top Matryoshka doll, if the perception and memory modules identify another smaller Matryoshka doll in the environment, the decision module determines to pick it up. Our action stack dynamically adds this pickup action to the top of the stack, prioritizing the new action over picking back the previous, larger Matryoshka doll. This stack structure facilitates multi-step reasoning and constructs the system's logic in a deep and coherent structure. 为管理多步推理，我们系统引入动作栈，作为一个简单但有效的“规划”模块。在决策模块的引导下，栈结构能巧妙组织动作的执行顺序。例如，当拿起最上层的套娃后，如果感知与记忆模块在环境中识别出另一个更小的套娃，决策模块将决定将其拿起。我们的动作栈会将这次取物动作动态压入栈顶，并将新动作优先于将先前更大的套娃重新拿起。该栈结构有助于多步推理，并以深层且连贯的结构构建系统逻辑。

                                                                                                                                Moreover, for the interactive scene exploration task, maintaining scene consistency is crucial in practice (e.g., the agent should close the fridge after exploring it). We employ a greedy strategy returning objects to their original states. This approach keeps the environment close to its pre-exploration state, making RoboEXP more practical for real-world applications. 此外，在交互式场景探索任务中，维持场景一致性在实践中至关重要（例如，智能体在探索冰箱后应将其关闭）。我们采用贪心策略，将物体恢复到原始状态。该做法使环境尽可能接近探索前的状态，使 RoboEXP 更适用于真实世界应用。

                                                                                                                                B.3 Usage of ACSG

                                                                                                                                B.3 ACSG的使用

                                                                                                                                The ACSG constructed during the exploration stage shows beneficial for scenarios that require a comprehensive understanding of scene content and structure, such as household environments like kitchens and living rooms, office environments, etc. We list several examples illustrating the potential usage of the scene graph in various tasks. 探索阶段构建的ACSG有助于需要全面理解场景内容与结构的场景，如厨房、客厅等家庭环境以及办公室环境等。我们列举若干示例，以说明场景图在各种任务中的潜在用途。

                                                                                                                                Judging Object Existence. A direct application of our ACSG is to determine the presence or absence of specific objects in the current environment. For instance, during the exploitation stage of the scenario (App. D) to set the dining table, if the spoon is missing, the robot can further seek human assistance. 判断物体存在。ACSG的一个直接应用是判断当前环境中是否存在特定物体。例如，在布置餐桌的利用阶段（App. D）中，如果勺子缺失，机器人可以进一步寻求人类帮助。

                                                                                                                                Object Retrieval. One notable advantage of our ACSG is its ability to capture all actions and their preconditions. Utilizing this information, retrieving any object becomes straightforward by following the graph structure and executing actions in topological order along the paths from the root to the target object node. For example, in the obstruction scenario (App. D), the ACSG can provide the sequence of actions required to fetch the tape: 1) removing the condiment blocking the cabinet door, 2) opening the cabinet via the door handle, and 3) retrieving the tape. Such insights are crucial for tasks like cooking. 物体取回。我们的ACSG的一个显著优势是能够捕捉所有动作及其前置条件。借助这些信息，只需沿图结构，从根节点到目标物体节点的路径按拓扑顺序执行动作，即可轻松取回任意物体。例如，在阻塞场景中（App. D），ACSG可以提供取回胶带所需的动作序列：1）移除阻挡柜门的调味品，2）通过门把手打开柜子，3）取回胶带。这类见解对于烹饪等任务至关重要。

                                                                                                                                Advanced Usage. The high-level representation of the environment provided by our ACSG serves as a simplified yet effective model. Similar to the approach proposed by Gu et al. [96], integrating the scene graph with Large Language Models (LLM) or Large Multi-modal Models (LMM) offers enhanced capabilities, including natural language interaction. This enables the robot to respond to human preferences expressed in natural language (e.g., fetching a coke when the person is thirsty) or through visual cues (e.g., fetching a mug when the table is dirty). 高级用法。我们的ACSG提供的环境高层表示是一个简化但有效的模型。与Gu等人[96]提出的方法类似，将场景图与大型语言模型（LLM）或大型多模态模型（LMM）结合，可提升能力，包括自然语言交互。这使机器人能够响应人类以自然语言表达的偏好（例如，人在口渴时取来可乐）或通过视觉线索表达的偏好（例如，桌子脏了时取来杯子）。

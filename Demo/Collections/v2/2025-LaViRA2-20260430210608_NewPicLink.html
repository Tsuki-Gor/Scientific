                                                                              Abstract-Zero-shot Vision-and-Language Navigation in Continuous Environments (VLN-CE) requires an agent to navigate unseen environments based on natural language instructions without any prior training. Current methods face a critical trade-off: either rely on environment-specific waypoint predictors that limit scene generalization, or underutilize the reasoning capabilities of large models during navigation. We introduce LaViRA, a simple yet effective zero-shot framework that addresses this dilemma by decomposing action into a coarse-to-fine hierarchy: Language Action for high-level planning, Vision Action for middle-level perceptual grounding, and Robot Action for low-level control. This modular decomposition allows us to leverage the distinct strengths of different scales of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) at each stage, creating a system that is powerful in its reasoning, grounding and practical control. LaViRA significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on the VLN-CE benchmark, demonstrating superior generalization capabilities in unseen environments, while maintaining transparency and efficiency for real-world deployment.                                                                               I. INTRODUCTION

                                                                              I. 引言

                                                                              Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN) presents the challenge of grounding natural language instructions within visual observations to enable an embodied agent to navigate through previously unseen environments [1]. Early VLN research was primarily conducted in discrete, graph-based settings where navigation is simplified to selecting paths between predefined nodes. To bridge the gap to the real world, Vision-and-Language Navigation in Continuous Environments (VLN-CE) [2] was introduced, removing the reliance on connectivity graphs and forcing agents to contend with realistic challenges like continuous visual perception and fine-grained motor control. 视觉语言导航（VLN）提出了将自然语言指令与视觉观察相结合的挑战，以使具身智能体能够在先前未见过的环境中进行导航 [1]。早期的 VLN 研究主要在离散的、基于图的环境中进行，其中导航被简化为在预定义节点之间选择路径。为了弥合与现实世界的差距，研究者引入了连续环境中的视觉语言导航（VLN-CE）[2]，消除了对连通性图的依赖，并迫使智能体应对诸如连续视觉感知和精细运动控制等现实挑战。

Two main paradigms have emerged. Waypoint prediction with large model reasoning. These methods combine a pre-trained waypoint predictor with an LLM or MLLM [3], [4]. The predictor proposes navigable waypoints, and the large model reasons over these candidates to select the next step. This leverages high-level planning but requires separate pre-training and struggles to generalize to unseen environments. Value mapping with vision language models. An alternative discards the waypoint predictor and uses a vision-language model (e.g., BLIP [7]) to generate a semantic relevance heatmap over the scene. The agent navigates toward the highest-scoring region. While this avoids predictor training, it typically limits powerful large models to offline instruction parsing, underutilizing their reasoning capabilities during online navigation.                                                                               These two paradigms reveal a fundamental trade-off. Waypoint-based methods excel at high-level reasoning but are constrained by a separate, inflexible waypoint prediction module. Value-mapping methods are more perceptually grounded but lack dynamic, high-level reasoning during navigation.                                                                               Can we design a purely zero-shot VLN-CE framework that (1) removes the dependency on a pre-trained waypoint predictor and (2) fully harnesses the reasoning abilities of MLLMs for navigation decision making? 我们能否设计一个纯零样本（zero-shot）的 VLN-CE 框架，使其能够（1）摆脱对预训练路点预测器的依赖，并（2）充分利用多模态大模型（MLLM）的推理能力进行导航决策？

                                                                              We answer this question with LaViRA: Language-Vision-Robot Action Translation. Our key insight is to decompose navigation into a coarse-to-fine, multi-stage action space, progressively refined from language to vision to robot control. Grounded in the divide-and-conquer principle for long-horizon decision making under partial observability [8] and hierarchical reinforcement learning theory [9], this decomposition exponentially reduces the search space at each stage, improving decision efficiency and zero-shot generalization in unseen continuous environments. Rather than requiring a single model to produce low-level controls directly, we allocate each stage to a model scale that best matches its reasoning or perceptual demands, allowing the system to exploit the complementary strengths of different models. 我们用 LaViRA（语言 - 视觉 - 机器人动作转换）来回答这个问题。我们的关键见解是将导航分解为一个从粗到细的多阶段动作空间，逐步从语言过渡到视觉再到机器人控制。基于部分可观测条件下的长视野决策分治原则 [8] 和分层强化学习理论 [9]，这种分解在每一阶段都以指数级方式缩小搜索空间，提高了决策效率，并增强了在未见连续环境中的零样本泛化能力。我们并非要求单个模型直接生成低级控制指令，而是将每个阶段分配给最能满足其推理或感知需求的模型规模，使系统能够充分利用不同模型的互补优势。

                                                                              1. Language Action: A powerful MLLM acts as a high-level planner, analyzing the instruction, history, and current observation to produce a coarse strategic decision, such as which general direction to head, whether to backtrack, or when to stop.

                                                                              2. 语言动作：强大的 MLLM 作为高层规划器，通过分析指令、历史记录和当前观测，做出粗略的战略决策，例如确定大致的前进方向、是否需要回溯或何时停止。

                                                                              1. Vision Action: A smaller, efficient MLLM takes this high-level decision and grounds it in the visual scene, identifying a specific object or region to move towards.

                                                                              2. 视觉动作：一个更小、更高效的 MLLM 接收该高层决策，并将其落实到视觉场景中，识别出具体的移动目标或区域。

                                                                              1. Robot Action: A simple, rule-based controller executes the low-level movement to the identified target.

                                                                              2. 机器人动作：一个简单的规则驱动控制器执行底层移动，到达识别出的目标点。

                                                                              This hierarchical decomposition offers three key advantages. First, it is purely zero-shot, eliminating any need for pre-trained waypoint predictors. Second, it fully engages MLLM reasoning across multiple granularities: high-level planning, mid-level perceptual grounding, and low-level control. Third, its modular design provides transparency and practicality, allowing seamless adaptation to both simulated and real-world robots. 这种分层分解具有三大关键优势。首先，它是纯零样本的，无需任何预训练的路点预测器。其次，它在多个粒度上充分调用了 MLLM 的推理能力：高层规划、中层感知定位和底层控制。第三，其模块化设计提供了透明度和实用性，能够无缝适配模拟环境和真实世界的机器人。

                                                                              II. 相关工作

                                                                              A. Vision-and-Language Navigation

                                                                              A. 视觉语言导航

                                                                              Early research in Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN) [1], where an agent follows instructions to navigate unseen environments, predominantly focused on discrete, graph-based settings [10]-[12]. Such environments enable high-level decision-making but fail to capture the continuous control demands of real-world scenarios. To address this, VLN in Continuous Environments (VLN-CE) [2] removes reliance on connectivity graphs and requires agents to perform fine-grained control actions like moving forward, rotating, and avoiding obstacles. This transition introduces new challenges in scene analysis, generalization, and low-level control. 视觉与语言导航（VLN）的早期研究 [1] 主要聚焦于离散的基于图的环境 [10]-[12]，在这些研究中，智能体需遵循指令在未知环境中导航。此类环境虽有助于进行高层次决策，但无法满足现实场景中的连续控制需求。为解决这一问题，连续环境下的视觉与语言导航（VLN - CE） [2] 不再依赖连通图，要求智能体执行诸如前进、旋转和避障等细粒度控制动作。这一转变给场景分析、泛化能力和低层次控制带来了新的挑战。

                                                                              Numerous VLN methods in both discrete and continuous environments have improved performance through enhanced cross-modal alignment [13], [14], reinforcement learning [15], data augmentation [16]-[19], and map-based representations [20]-[22]. However, these learning-based approaches require substantial environment-specific training, which limits their applicability for zero-shot deployment-motivating recent interest in training-free VLN-CE solutions. 离散和连续环境中的众多视觉语言导航（VLN）方法通过增强跨模态对齐[13]、[14]、强化学习[15]、数据增强[16]-[19]和基于地图的表示[20]-[22]提高了性能。然而，这些基于学习的方法需要大量特定环境的训练，这限制了它们在零样本部署中的适用性，从而引发了近期对无训练视觉语言导航 - 连续环境（VLN - CE）解决方案的关注。

                                                                              B. Zero-Shot VLN with Foundation Models

                                                                              B. 基于基础模型的零样本 VLN

                                                                              The rise of foundation models such as LLMs [23], [24] and MLLMs [7], [25], [26] has driven a new wave of zero-shot VLN-CE research, categorized by how they integrate with robot control. One dominant paradigm is waypoint-based navigation [3], [4]. These methods use an LLM/MLLM to select waypoints proposed by a pre-trained predictor [27]. However, this creates a critical dependency on the predictor, which often fails to generalize to unseen scenes and limits backtracking flexibility [4]. Another approach is heuristic value-mapping [5], [6], where a Vision-Language Model (VLM) generates a semantic heatmap to guide the agent. In these frameworks, powerful LLMs are restricted to offline instruction parsing, underutilizing their dynamic reasoning during navigation. Reliance on hard constraints for progress estimation also introduces rigidity in complex scenarios [5]. Recently, GC-VLN [28] models instructions as explicit graph constraints for training-free navigation. While effective for structured reasoning, it still requires offline parsing and lacks flexible backtracking. LLM [23], [24] 和 MLLM [7], [25], [26] 等基础模型的兴起，推动了零样本 VLN-CE 研究的新浪潮，其分类依据在于它们与机器人控制的集成方式。一种主流范式是基于路点的导航 [3], [4]。这些方法利用 LLM/MLLM 来选择由预训练预测器提出的路点 [27]。然而，这导致了对预测器的严重依赖，而预测器往往难以泛化到未见过的场景，且限制了回溯的灵活性 [4]。另一种方法是启发式价值映射 [5], [6]，即视觉语言模型 (VLM) 生成语义热力图来引导智能体。在这些框架中，强大的 LLM 仅限于离线指令解析，未能充分利用其在导航过程中的动态推理能力。对进度估计硬约束的依赖也在复杂场景中引入了僵化性 [5]。最近，GC-VLN [28] 将指令建模为显式的图约束，以实现无需训练的导航。尽管在结构化推理方面有效，但它仍需离线解析，且缺乏灵活的回溯能力。

                                                                              Our LaViRA framework bridges the gap between these approaches, introducing a Language-Vision-Robot Action Translation that progressively refines action from language-level planning to vision-level grounding to robot-level execution. This design allows MLLMs reasoning to directly influence decisions at multiple granularities, enabling fully zero-shot, interpretable, and adaptable navigation in continuous environments. 我们的 LaViRA 框架弥合了这些方法之间的差距，引入了“语言-视觉-机器人”动作转换，将动作从语言级规划逐步细化到视觉级定位，再到机器人级执行。这种设计使 MLLM 的推理能够直接影响多个粒度的决策，从而在连续环境中实现完全零样本、可解释且适应性强的导航。

                                                                              C. Hierarchical Structure in VLN

                                                                              C. VLN 中的层次化结构

                                                                              Hierarchical architectures have emerged as a powerful paradigm for addressing complex, long-horizon navigation tasks. This strategy effectively decomposes the problem into high-level planning and low-level execution, allowing different modules to specialize. Current hierarchical approaches often combine high-level semantic planning with low-level motion control. One common strategy involves dividing the environment into distinct topological regions and having a high-level planner select a sequence of zones to traverse, while a low-level controller navigates within each zone [29], [30]. Other methods learn policies for both the manager and worker through techniques like imitation learning [31] or reinforcement learning [32]. A recent example, NavA3 [33], uses a global policy to identify target regions and a pre-trained model for fine-grained navigation. 层次化架构已成为解决复杂、长时程导航任务的强大范式。该策略有效地将问题分解为高层规划和底层执行，使不同模块能够各司其职。当前的层次化方法通常将高层语义规划与底层运动控制相结合。一种常见策略是将环境划分为不同的拓扑区域，由高层规划器选择要穿过的区域序列，而底层控制器在每个区域内进行导航 [29], [30]。其他方法则通过模仿学习 [31] 或强化学习 [32] 等技术为管理者和执行者学习策略。最近的一个例子 NavA3 [33] 使用全局策略来识别目标区域，并利用预训练模型进行细粒度导航。

                                                                               

                                                                              Fig. 2: The LaViRA Pipeline. Our framework decomposes navigation into three sequential stages. (1) Language Action: A large MLLM processes the instruction, history, and current observation to generate a high-level plan, deciding whether to move forward, turn, backtrack, or stop. (2) Vision Action: A smaller, more efficient MLLM takes the high-level plan and progress estimation to identify a specific visual target in the chosen direction, outputting its bounding box and description. (3) Robot Action: The target's pixel coordinates are projected onto a global map, and a rule-based controller navigates the robot to the destination. This hierarchical process enables generalized and robust zero-shot vision-language navigation. 图 2：LaViRA 流水线。我们的框架将导航分解为三个连续阶段。(1) 语言动作：大型 MLLM 处理指令、历史记录和当前观测，生成高层计划，决定是前进、转向、回溯还是停止。(2) 视觉动作：一个更小、更高效的 MLLM 接收高层计划和进度估计，在选定方向上识别特定的视觉目标，并输出其边界框和描述。(3) 机器人动作：目标的像素坐标被投影到全局地图上，由基于规则的控制器引导机器人到达目的地。这种层次化过程实现了通用且稳健的零样本视觉语言导航。

                                                                              However, a common limitation of these methods is their reliance on extensive, environment-specific training, which restricts generalization and hinders zero-shot deployment. In contrast, LaViRA's Language-Vision-Robot action decomposition is fully zero-shot. By leveraging MLLM reasoning and a rule-based controller, it eliminates the need for any training, which simplifies deployment and enhances modularity. 然而，这些方法的一个共同局限是依赖于大量特定于环境的训练，这限制了泛化能力并阻碍了零样本部署。相比之下，LaViRA 的“语言-视觉-机器人”动作分解是完全零样本的。通过利用 MLLM 推理和基于规则的控制器，它消除了对任何训练的需求，从而简化了部署并增强了模块化程度。

                                                                              III. PROPOSED METHOD

                                                                              III. 拟议方法

                                                                              Our approach, LaViRA, introduces a novel framework for zero-shot Vision-and-Language Navigation in Continuous Environments (VLN-CE). The core idea is simple yet effective: we decompose the complex navigation task into a coarse-to-fine hierarchy of actions: Language Action, Vision Action, and Robot Action. This modular decomposition allows us to leverage the distinct strengths of different scales of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) at each stage, creating a system that is powerful in its reasoning, grounding and practical for real-world deployment, all without requiring any environment-specific training. 我们的方法 LaViRA 引入了一种用于连续环境视觉语言导航 (VLN-CE) 的新型零样本框架。其核心思想简单而有效：我们将复杂的导航任务分解为从粗到细的动作层次：语言动作、视觉动作和机器人动作。这种模块化分解使我们能够在每个阶段利用不同规模多模态大语言模型 (MLLM) 的独特优势，构建出一个既具备强大推理和定位能力，又适用于实际部署的系统，且无需任何特定于环境的训练。

                                                                              The overall pipeline is illustrated in Figure 2 In the VLN-CE task, an agent must follow a natural language instruction I through an unseen environment. At each timestep t ,it uses an egocentric observation It to choose its next action At from a continuous space. To address this,our method decomposes the navigation process into a sequence of three hierarchical actions: a high-level directional plan (Language Action), the grounding of this plan into a specific visual target (Vision Action), and finally, the low-level movement to reach it (Robot Action). We detail each stage below. 整体流水线如图 2 所示。在 VLN-CE 任务中，智能体必须遵循自然语言指令 I 在未见过的环境中导航。在每个时间步 t，它利用自我中心观测 It 从连续空间中选择下一个动作 At。为了解决这一问题，我们的方法将导航过程分解为三个层次化动作序列：高层方向规划（语言动作）、将该计划定位为特定视觉目标（视觉动作），以及最终到达目标的底层移动（机器人动作）。我们在下文详细说明每个阶段。

                                                                              A. Language Action: High-Level Planning

                                                                              A. 语言动作：高层规划

                                                                              The first stage of our framework addresses the question: Where should I generally go next? To answer this, we employ a powerful, large-scale MLLM (e.g., GPT-40) that functions as a high-level planner. This model is responsible for interpreting the full context of the navigation task and producing a coarse, directional command. 我们框架的第一阶段旨在解决“下一步大体该往哪走？”这一问题。为此，我们采用了一个功能强大的大规模多模态大模型（如 GPT-4o）作为高层规划器。该模型负责解读导航任务的完整上下文，并生成粗略的导向指令。

                                                                              Specifically, the model receives three types of input: 具体而言，模型接收三种类型的输入：

                                                                              • Language Instruction I : The given natural language instruction provided at the start of the task.

                                                                              • 语言指令 I：任务开始时给定的自然语言指令。

                                                                              • Current Observation Ot : A set of four images corresponding to the agent's front, left, right, and back views {I front ,I left ,I right ,I back } ,providing an informative understanding of the current location.

                                                                              • 当前观测 Ot：一组对应智能体前、左、右、后四个视角的图像 {I front ,I left ,I right ,I back }，为理解当前位置提供信息。

                                                                              • Navigation History Ht : A structured summary of past observations and actions, formatted as a sequence like Ht={(O0,A0),(O1,A1),…,(Ot−1,At−1)} . This provides crucial context on what has already been accomplished.

                                                                              • 导航历史 Ht：对过去观测和动作的结构化总结，格式为序列 Ht={(O0,A0),(O1,A1),…,(Ot−1,At−1)}。这为已完成的任务提供了关键的上下文信息。

                                                                              Given this rich multimodal context, the MLLM performs two tasks simultaneously. First, it generates a Progress Estimation Pt ,a natural language assessment of how much of the instruction has been completed. This explicit reasoning step forces the model to track its progress against the overall instruction. Second, based on its analysis, the model selects a Language Action At lang  from a discrete set: 基于这些丰富的多模态上下文，MLLM 同时执行两项任务。首先，它生成进度评估 Pt，即对指令完成程度的自然语言评估。这一显式的推理步骤迫使模型根据总体指令跟踪其进度。其次，基于分析结果，模型从离散集合中选择一个语言动作 At lang ：

                                                                              • navigate to : Move forward, left, right, or behind.

                                                                              • 导航至 <方向>：向前、向左、向右或向后移动。

                                                                              • backtrack to : Return to a previously visited waypoint location.

                                                                              • 回溯至 <路点>：返回之前访问过的路点位置。

                                                                              • stop: Terminate the navigation.

                                                                              • 停止：终止导航。

                                                                              This process can be formulated as: 此过程可表述为：

                                                                              (1)(At lang ,Pt)=MLLM large (I,Ht,Ot)

                                                                               

                                                                              This stage effectively abstracts the continuous environment into a few high-level choices, allowing the large MLLM to focus its powerful reasoning capabilities on strategic, long-term planning. 该阶段有效地将连续环境抽象为少数几个高层选择，使大型 MLLM 能够将其强大的推理能力集中于战略性的长期规划上。

(Left) The prompt for the Language Action model, which takes in full context to decide on a high-level direction. (Right) The prompt for the Vision Action model, which uses the output from the first stage to ground the decision in a specific visual target. 图 3：语言动作与视觉动作的提示词。（左）语言动作模型的提示词，利用完整上下文决定高层方向。（右）视觉动作模型的提示词，利用第一阶段的输出将决策落实到具体的视觉目标上。

                                                                              B. Vision Action: Perceptual Grounding

                                                                              B. 视觉动作：感知定位

                                                                              Once a high-level Language Action is determined, the next stage must answer: What specific thing should I move towards in that direction? This is the role of the Vision Action stage, where we ground the abstract plan into a concrete perceptual target. 一旦确定了高层语言动作，下一阶段必须回答：“在该方向上，我具体应该朝什么目标移动？”这就是视觉动作阶段的作用，我们将抽象计划落实为具体的感知目标。

                                                                              For this task, we use a smaller, more efficient MLLM (e.g., Qwen2.5-VL-32B). This choice is deliberate: grounding is a more focused perception task that does not require the same extensive world knowledge as high-level planning, making a smaller model more suitable and computationally efficient. As our ablation studies in Section IV-C confirm, pairing a powerful planner with a specialized, efficient grounding model yields optimal performance. The model is prompted with: 为此，我们使用了一个更小、更高效的 MLLM（如 Qwen2.5-VL-32B）。这一选择是有意为之：定位是一个更聚焦的感知任务，不需要像高层规划那样具备广泛的世界知识，因此较小的模型更合适且计算效率更高。正如我们在 IV-C 节的消融实验所证实的，将强大的规划器与专门的高效定位模型相结合可获得最佳性能。模型接收的提示词包括：

                                                                              • Language Instruction I : The original instruction.

                                                                              • 语言指令 I：原始指令。

                                                                              • Progress Estimation Pt : The text generated by the Language Action model.

                                                                              • 进度估计 Pt：由语言动作模型生成的文本。

                                                                              • Chosen Direction Image Idir : The single image corresponding to the direction chosen in the previous stage At lang  .

                                                                              • 所选方向图像 Idir：对应于上一阶段 At lang  所选方向的单张图像。

                                                                              The model's task is to identify the most relevant object or region in the image that aligns with the next step of the instruction. It outputs a Vision Action At vis  in a structured format containing a bounding box and its description. This can be expressed as: 该模型的任务是识别图像中与指令下一步最相关的物体或区域。它以包含边界框及其描述的结构化格式输出视觉动作 At vis 。这可以表示为：

                                                                              (2)At vis =MLLM small (I,Pt,I dir )

                                                                               

                                                                              The output At vis  is a dictionary containing: 输出 At vis  是一个包含以下内容的字典：

                                                                              • Bounding Box bbox2d : The 2D coordinates [x1,y1, x2, y2] localizing the target.

                                                                              • 边界框 bbox2d：定位目标的二维坐标 [x1, y1, x2, y2]。

                                                                              • Target Description: A textual description of the identified object or region.

                                                                              • 目标描述：对所识别物体或区域的文本描述。

                                                                              As shown in Figure 3, the prompt instructs the model to select a target that is not too close, encouraging meaningful progress. This stage effectively translates the high-level plan into a tangible, visually verifiable goal. 如图 3 所示，提示词引导模型选择一个不太近的目标，以促进有效的进展。该阶段有效地将高层规划转化为具体且视觉可验证的目标。

                                                                              C. Robot Action: Low-Level Control

                                                                              C. 机器人动作：底层控制

                                                                              The final Robot Action stage answers: How do I physically get there? It translates the identified visual target into low-level motor commands using a robust, rule-based controller. Pixel-to-World Projection. First, we select the bottom-center pixel of the target's bounding box and project it into a 3D point in the world frame. This involves unprojecting the 2D pixel to the camera's 3D coordinate system using the intrinsic matrix K : 最后的机器人动作阶段回答了：我如何物理到达那里？它使用稳健的基于规则的控制器，将识别出的视觉目标转化为底层运动指令。像素到世界的投影。首先，我们选择目标边界框的底中心像素，并将其投影到世界坐标系中的 3D 点。这涉及使用内参矩阵 K 将 2D 像素反投影到相机的 3D 坐标系：

                                                                              (3)[X cam ,Y cam ,Z cam ]T=d⋅K−1⋅[u target ,v target ,1]T

                                                                               

                                                                              where d is the depth. This 3D point is then transformed from the camera frame to the world frame using the agent's current pose T agent =(x agent ,z agent ,θ agent ) to yield a target position p world =(x world ,z world ) on the 2D map: 其中 d 是深度。然后，使用智能体当前的位姿 T agent =(x agent ,z agent ,θ agent ) 将该 3D 点从相机坐标系转换到世界坐标系，从而在 2D 地图上得出目标位置 p world =(x world ,z world )：

                                                                              (4)[x world z world ]=[x agent z agent ]+[cos⁡θ−sin⁡θsin⁡θcos⁡θ][Z cam −X cam ]

                                                                               

                                                                              Path Planning and Control. Given the target position p world  ,the agent computes a short-term path using the Fast Marching Method (FMM). A low-level controller then executes this path with local obstacle avoidance. This deterministic final step grounds the reasoning chain in physical action, ensuring interpretability and making the system adaptable to different robot platforms by simply swapping the controller. 路径规划与控制。给定目标位置 p world ，智能体使用快速行进法 (FMM) 计算短期路径。随后，底层控制器执行该路径并进行局部避障。这一确定性的最后一步将推理链落实到物理动作中，确保了可解释性，并通过简单地更换控制器使系统能够适应不同的机器人平台。

The agent's observation is composed of posed 640×480 RGB-D images, low-level path planning is executed using the Fast Marching Method (FMM) on a global map constructed from depth observations. All experiments were conducted on 8 NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPUs for parallel evaluation. 实现细节。我们的零样本框架无需针对特定环境进行训练。在高层语言动作阶段，我们评估了两种领先的 MLLM：Gemini-2.5-Pro 和 GPT-4o。在视觉动作阶段，我们主要使用高效的 Qwen2.5-VL-32B，并在消融实验中探索了其他模型。智能体的观测由带位姿的 640×480 RGB-D 图像组成，底层路径规划使用快速行进法（FMM）在基于深度观测构建的全局地图上执行。所有实验均在 8 张 NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPU 上进行并行评估。

                                                                              C. Ablation Studies

                                                                              C. 消融研究

                                                                              We performed a series of ablation studies to analyze LaViRA's performance and quantify the contribution of its core components.                                                                               Framework Design Choices. We conducted further ablations on key design choices, as shown in Table III First, we ablated the hierarchical structure. An end-to-end baseline ("w/o LA+VA") fails with 0% SPL. Removing the high-level planner ("w/o LA") yields 4.4% SPL, while removing the perceptual grounding module ("w/o VA") achieves 13.9% SPL. Our full framework outperforms the latter by 14.4 points, confirming the effectiveness of coarse-to-fine decomposition. Next, we analyzed history representation. Replacing visual history with textual descriptions drops SPL by 5.3 points to 23.0%. Using only past actions (25.1%) or only textual observations (21.8%) also underperforms, showing that combining raw visual observations and actions is most effective. We then evaluated the backtracking mechanism. Disabling it causes a 6.1-point SPL drop. Restricting backtracking to the last waypoint only remains 4.8 points worse than our flexible strategy. Finally, we ablated pixel point selection. Directly outputting pixel coordinates achieves 19.4% SPL, which is 8.9 points lower than our bounding-box + bottom-center heuristic approach. 框架设计选择。我们针对关键设计选择进行了进一步消融实验，如表III所示。首先，我们消融了分层结构。端到端基线（“w/o LA+VA”）失败，SPL为0%。移除高层规划器（“w/o LA”）后SPL为4.4%，而移除感知定位模块（“w/o VA”）后SPL为13.9%。我们的完整框架比后者高出14.4个百分点，证实了从粗到细分解的有效性。其次，我们分析了历史表示。用文本描述替换视觉历史使SPL下降了5.3个百分点至23.0%。仅使用过去动作（25.1%）或仅使用文本观察（21.8%）的效果也较差，表明结合原始视觉观察和动作最为有效。随后，我们评估了回溯机制。禁用该机制导致SPL下降6.1个百分点。仅将回溯限制在最后一个路径点，效果仍比我们的灵活策略差4.8个百分点。最后，我们消融了像素点选择。直接输出像素坐标的SPL为19.4%，比我们的“边界框+底部中心”启发式方法低8.9个百分点。

                                                                              D. Qualitative Analysis

                                                                              D. 定性分析

                                                                              To offer qualitative insights into LaViRA's decision-making, Figure 4 shows a successful navigation run and common failures. In the success case, LaViRA demonstrates its coarse-to-fine approach: it first makes a high-level directional choice ("navigate left"), then grounds this in a visual landmark ("Black door with glass panels"), and finally selects a precise waypoint. This hierarchical process validates our design. 为了提供对LaViRA决策过程的定性见解，图4展示了一次成功的导航运行和常见失败案例。在成功案例中，LaViRA展示了其从粗到细的方法：首先做出高层方向选择（“向左导航”），然后将其定位在视觉地标（“带有玻璃面板的黑色门”）上，最后选择精确的路径点。这一分层过程验证了我们的设计。

                                                                              The failure cases illustrate three common errors: (1) A Language Action error from ambiguous instructions, e.g., failing to identify the correct door when multiple doors are visible. This can be mitigated by explicit sub-instruction decomposition during prompting. (2) A Vision Action error where the correct object is grounded to the wrong image region. Although VAM excels at bounding-box grounding, it sometimes fails on larger areas like "hallway" or "living room". Integrating open-vocabulary segmentation (e.g., SAM 2) as post-processing can address this. (3) A simulation-induced depth error where transparent objects (e.g., windows in Habitat) lack depth values, leading to incorrect 3D projection. In real-world deployment, this can be resolved via LiDAR or stereo depth fusion. 失败案例说明了三种常见错误：（1）语言动作错误，源于指令歧义，例如在多个门可见时无法识别正确的门。这可以通过在提示过程中进行明确的子指令分解来缓解。（2）视觉动作错误，即正确的目标被定位到错误的图像区域。尽管VAM在边界框定位方面表现出色，但在“走廊”或“客厅”等较大区域上偶尔会失败。将开放词汇分割（如SAM 2）作为后处理步骤可以解决此问题。（3）模拟导致的深度错误，即透明物体（如Habitat中的窗户）缺乏深度值，导致错误的3D投影。在实际部署中，这可以通过激光雷达或立体深度融合来解决。

                                                                              V. REAL-WORLD EXPERIMENTS

                                                                              V. 真实世界实验

                                                                              To validate LaViRA's practicality beyond simulation, we deployed it on two distinct real-world robots: a Unitree Go1 quadruped and an Agilex Cobot Magic wheeled platform. These experiments tested the framework's sim-to-real transferability, requiring only the replacement of the low-level robot controller. The Unitree Go1 was equipped with a Jetson Orin NX and an Intel RealSense D435i camera, using its native velocity controller to navigate. The Agilex Cobot Magic platform used a chest-mounted Orbbec Dabai camera and a 2-DOF velocity controller for its mobile base. In both deployments, the onboard computers called the respective MLLM APIs for Language and Vision Actions, transmitting the resulting target pixel coordinates to the robot's navigation system. 为了验证LaViRA在模拟之外的实用性，我们将其部署在两种不同的真实机器人上：Unitree Go1四足机器人和Agilex Cobot Magic轮式平台。这些实验测试了该框架的模拟到现实迁移能力，仅需替换底层机器人控制器。Unitree Go1配备了Jetson Orin NX和Intel RealSense D435i相机，使用其原生速度控制器进行导航。Agilex Cobot Magic平台使用胸部安装的Orbbec Dabai相机和用于移动底盘的2自由度速度控制器。在两次部署中，机载计算机均调用相应的MLLM API进行语言和视觉动作，并将生成的最终目标像素坐标传输给机器人的导航系统。

                                                                              As shown in Figure 5 both platforms successfully executed navigation tasks in complex indoor office environments. The supplementary video further demonstrates consistent performance across multiple cases on each robot, highlighting the framework's strong sim-to-real transferability with only platform-specific low-level controller replacement. These qualitative results confirm that LaViRA's hierarchical reasoning generalizes effectively from simulation to physical hardware without any training. 如图5所示，两个平台均在复杂的室内办公环境中成功执行了导航任务。补充视频进一步展示了每个机器人在多个案例中的一致表现，突显了该框架在仅需替换平台特定底层控制器的情况下，具有强大的模拟到现实迁移能力。这些定性结果证实，LaViRA的分层推理无需任何训练即可有效地从模拟泛化到物理硬件。

                                                                              VI. CONCLUSION

                                                                              VI. 结论

                                                                              We introduce LaViRA, a hierarchical framework for zero-shot Vision-and-Language Navigation in continuous environments. By decomposing navigation into a coarse-to-fine action hierarchy, LaViRA eliminates pre-trained waypoint predictors and fully leverages multi-granularity MLLM reasoning. Experiments show that pairing a powerful MLLM for high-level planning with an efficient one for perceptual grounding achieves state-of-the-art performance on the VLN-CE benchmark, significantly outperforming existing zero-shot methods. 我们介绍了LaViRA，这是一个用于连续环境中零样本视觉语言导航的分层框架。通过将导航分解为从粗到细的动作层级，LaViRA消除了预训练路径点预测器的需求，并充分利用了多粒度MLLM推理。实验表明，将强大的MLLM用于高层规划，并结合高效的MLLM进行感知定位，在VLN-CE基准测试上达到了最先进的性能，显著优于现有的零样本方法。

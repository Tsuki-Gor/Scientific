ABSTRACT
摘要

Despite being studied for over twenty years, Recommender Systems (RSs) still suffer from important issues that limit their applicability in real-world scenarios. Data sparsity, cold start, and explainability are some of the most impacting problems. Intuitively, these historical limitations can be mitigated by injecting prior knowledge into recommendation models. Neuro-Symbolic (NeSy) approaches are suitable candidates for achieving this goal. Specifically, they aim to integrate learning (e.g., neural networks) with symbolic reasoning (e.g., logical reasoning). Generally, the integration lets a neural model interact with a logical knowledge base, enabling reasoning capabilities. In particular, NeSy approaches have been shown to deal well with poor training data, and their symbolic component could enhance model transparency. This gives insights that NeSy systems could potentially mitigate the aforementioned RSs limitations. However, the application of such systems to RSs is still in its early stages, and most of the proposed architectures do not really exploit the advantages of a NeSy approach. To this end, we conducted preliminary experiments with a Logic Tensor Network (LTN), a novel NeSy framework. We used the LTN to train a vanilla Matrix Factorization model using a First-Order Logic knowledge base as an objective. In particular, we encoded facts to enable the regularization of the latent factors using content information, obtaining promising results. In this paper, we review existing NeSy recommenders, argue about their limitations, show our preliminary results with the LTN, and propose interesting future works in this novel research area. In particular, we show how the LTN can be intuitively used to regularize models, perform cross-domain recommendation, ensemble learning, and explainable recommendation, reduce popularity bias, and easily define the loss function of a model.

1 INTRODUCTION
1 引言

Recommender Systems (RSs) are nowadays essential tools for e-services. Specifically, they aim to mitigate information overload by suggesting novel items to users based on their preferences [34]. Since the beginning of the RSs literature, Collaborative Filtering (CF) [4,24,36] has been affirmed to be the de facto recommendation approach. CF is based on the principle that similar users tend to rate items similarly. Following this intuition, CF methods find similarity patterns [36] across user histories and leverage them to perform recommendations. Latent Factor Models [25, 38], particularly Matrix Factorization (MF) [21, 22, 30, 32], have been proven to be the most successful CF technique. These models map users and items in joint latent space and model their interactions as inner products in such space.
推荐系统（RS）如今是电子服务的重要工具。具体而言，它们旨在通过根据用户的偏好向用户建议新项目来减轻信息过载 [34]。自推荐系统文献开始以来，协同过滤（CF）[4,24,36] 被确认为事实上的推荐方法。CF 基于相似用户倾向于以相似方式评价项目的原则。遵循这一直觉，CF 方法在用户历史中寻找相似性模式 [36] 并利用这些模式进行推荐。潜在因子模型 [25, 38]，特别是矩阵分解（MF） [21, 22, 30, 32]，已被证明是最成功的 CF 技术。这些模型将用户和项目映射到联合潜在空间，并将它们的交互建模为该空间中的内积。
Recently, many deep learning models have raised the bar regarding recommendation accuracy [11, 20, 28]. However, in a recent study [17], it has been shown that most deep learning methods can be outperformed by a basic Matrix Factorization baseline when properly tuned. This finding suggests that neural networks struggle with the sparse structure of recommendation datasets, as a basic linear model can perform better.
最近，许多深度学习模型在推荐准确性方面提高了标准 [11, 20, 28]。然而，在一项最近的研究中 [17]，已显示大多数深度学习方法在适当调整时可以被基本的矩阵分解基线超越。这个发现表明，神经网络在处理推荐数据集的稀疏结构时存在困难，因为基本的线性模型可以表现得更好。
Furthermore, despite being studied for over twenty years, state-of-the-art recommendation models still suffer from historical limitations that limit their applicability in real-world scenarios [2]. Specifically, (i) the long-tail distribution of user-item interactions causes the datasets to be very sparse. A minority of items are very popular and hence full of ratings, while most items are unknown to the users and lack ratings. This leads to a drastic number of missing ratings. Then, (ii) the dynamic nature of the recommendation scenario leads to cold-start cases. New users and/or items can be added to the platform during a recommendation engine lifecycle. Dealing with such cases is challenging since no data is available at the beginning to perform accurate recommendations. Finally, (iii) users tend to be wary of the system if it cannot convince them about the provided recommendation [10,43] .
此外，尽管研究已有二十多年，最先进的推荐模型仍然受到历史限制的影响，这限制了它们在现实场景中的适用性 [2]。具体而言，(i) 用户-项目交互的长尾分布导致数据集非常稀疏。少数项目非常受欢迎，因此评分数量众多，而大多数项目对用户来说是未知的，缺乏评分。这导致了大量缺失评分的情况。然后，(ii) 推荐场景的动态特性导致了冷启动问题。在推荐引擎的生命周期内，新的用户和/或项目可以被添加到平台中。处理这种情况具有挑战性，因为在开始时没有可用的数据来进行准确的推荐。最后，(iii) 如果系统无法让用户信服所提供的推荐，用户往往会对系统持谨慎态度 [10,43]。
Intuitively, these issues can be mitigated by providing additional knowledge to the model. Such knowledge should be carefully designed to compensate for data scarcity, provide additional information when no ratings are available, or enhance model transparency. Following this intuition, methods based on Factorization Machines [31,33] have been proposed. These models extend the user-item matrix by adding new dimensions containing content (e.g., movie genres, demographic information) and/or contextual side information (e.g., location, time). Despite being able to increase performance and mitigate sparsity-related issues, these systems still lack explainability. The research field of Knowledge-Aware Recommender Systems has better investigated this latter problem [3, 29].
直观上，这些问题可以通过向模型提供额外知识来缓解。这种知识应经过精心设计，以弥补数据稀缺，在没有评分可用时提供额外信息，或增强模型的透明度。基于这一直觉，提出了基于因子分解机的方法 [31,33]。这些模型通过添加包含内容（例如，电影类型、人口统计信息）和/或上下文侧信息（例如，位置、时间）的新维度来扩展用户-项目矩阵。尽管能够提高性能并缓解与稀疏性相关的问题，但这些系统仍然缺乏可解释性。知识感知推荐系统的研究领域对此后一个问题进行了更深入的研究 [3, 29]。
We believe Neuro-Symbolic (NeSy) [14] approaches are better candidates for injecting knowledge inside models. Specifically, they aim to integrate learning and (symbolic) reasoning to obtain the best from both worlds. In particular,(i)symbolic methods typically work well with poor training data, while neural networks struggle. Then, (ii) symbolic methods are usually explainable by design, while neural networks are black-boxes. Finally, (iii) symbolic methods manage to work in the absence of data (i.e., zero-shot learning), while for neural networks, it is impossible. Ideally, by integrating these two paradigms, it could be possible to obtain a recommendation engine that can deal well with sparsity, address cold-start cases, and make its predictions less opaque.
我们认为神经符号（NeSy）[14] 方法是将知识注入模型的更好候选者。具体而言，它们旨在整合学习和（符号）推理，以从两者中获得最佳效果。特别是，（i）符号方法通常在训练数据稀缺的情况下表现良好，而神经网络则面临困难。然后，（ii）符号方法通常在设计上是可解释的，而神经网络则是黑箱。最后，（iii）符号方法能够在没有数据的情况下工作（即零样本学习），而对于神经网络来说，这是不可能的。理想情况下，通过整合这两种范式，可以获得一个能够很好处理稀疏性、解决冷启动问题并使预测结果不那么不透明的推荐引擎。
Many Neuro-Symbolic recommenders have been proposed recently [9,12,13,23,26,37,41,42] . However,these methods do not totally exploit the advantages of the NeSy system, or they have not been applied to overcome the aforementioned limitations, where we believe they are particularly suited. To this end, in a preliminary experiment, we used a Logic Tensor Network (LTN) [5] to encode logical axioms for enabling regularization of a vanilla Matrix Factorization (MF) based on logical reasoning. We obtained promising results, showing that the benefits of the encoded knowledge increase with the sparsity of the user-item ratings. In the next sections, we review the existing literature for NeSy recommenders, argue about their limitations, show our preliminary experiments with LTN, and propose possible interesting directions for research in this field.
最近提出了许多神经符号推荐系统 [9,12,13,23,26,37,41,42]。然而，这些方法并没有完全利用NeSy系统的优势，或者它们尚未被应用于克服上述限制，而我们认为它们特别适合。为此，在一项初步实验中，我们使用逻辑张量网络（LTN）[5] 编码逻辑公理，以便基于逻辑推理对普通矩阵分解（MF）进行正则化。我们获得了有希望的结果，显示编码知识的好处随着用户-项目评分的稀疏性增加而增加。在接下来的章节中，我们回顾了现有的NeSy推荐系统文献，讨论它们的局限性，展示我们与LTN的初步实验，并提出该领域可能有趣的研究方向。

2 REVIEW OF THE LITERATURE
2 文献综述

In the last two years, the RS community has seen the emergence of many NeSy approaches. The seminal approach that applied NeSy to RSs has been Neural Collaborative Reasoning (NCR) [13]. NCR formalizes the sequential recommendation task [16] as a logical reasoning problem. In particular, the user's ratings are represented using propositional variables, and logical connectives are used to construct formulas that express sequential patterns between them. For example,the propositional formula eui1∧eui2∧eui3⇒eui4 is used in NCR to state that user u liked item i4 after she liked items i1,i2 ,and i3 . Then,NCR maps the variables (e.g., eui1 ) to logical embeddings and the logical connectives (e.g., ∧,⇒ ) to neural networks which act on those embeddings. The neural networks are forced to behave like classic logic connectives using logical regularization. Doing so allows each logical expression to be equivalently organized as a neural network to conduct logical reasoning and prediction in a continuous space. The learning is carried on by feeding the model with these propositional formulas in such a way it learns the sequential patterns in the user-item ratings. They compared NCR with many linear and deep baselines, showing it can reach state-of-the-art performance. However, this approach is not properly NeSy as neural networks implement the symbolic part (i.e., logical connectives). LTN uses fuzzy logic semantics instead, making the framework theoretically sound and possibly more explainable. Moreover, NCR uses propositional logic, which makes it impossible to encode complex and expressive knowledge due to the simplicity of the language syntax.
在过去两年中，推荐系统（RS）社区出现了许多神经符号（NeSy）方法。将NeSy应用于推荐系统的开创性方法是神经协作推理（NCR）[13]。NCR将顺序推荐任务[16]形式化为一个逻辑推理问题。特别地，用户的评分使用命题变量表示，逻辑连接词用于构建表达它们之间顺序模式的公式。例如，命题公式eui1∧eui2∧eui3⇒eui4在NCR中用于表述用户u在喜欢了项目i1,i2和i3之后喜欢了项目i4。然后，NCR将变量（例如，eui1）映射到逻辑嵌入，并将逻辑连接词（例如，∧,⇒）映射到作用于这些嵌入的神经网络。通过逻辑正则化，神经网络被迫表现得像经典逻辑连接词。这使得每个逻辑表达式可以等效地组织为一个神经网络，以在连续空间中进行逻辑推理和预测。学习通过以这样的方式将这些命题公式输入模型进行，模型学习用户-项目评分中的顺序模式。他们将NCR与许多线性和深度基线进行了比较，显示其可以达到最先进的性能。然而，这种方法并不真正属于NeSy，因为神经网络实现了符号部分（即逻辑连接词）。LTN则使用模糊逻辑语义，使该框架在理论上合理，并可能更具可解释性。此外，NCR使用命题逻辑，这使得由于语言语法的简单性，无法编码复杂和富有表现力的知识。
In Graph Collaborative Reasoning (GCR) [12], NCR is extended to work with knowledge graphs. In particular, they provide a simple approach for translating the graph structure into logical expressions to convert the link prediction task into a logical reasoning problem. As in NCR, they use logically constrained neural modules to build the network architecture according to the logical expression. Then, they use back-propagation to efficiently learn the model parameters, bridging differentiable learning and symbolic reasoning in a unified architecture. They conducted experiments similar to NCR, showing that GCR can improve NCR on knowledge graphs. However, since GCR is an extension of NCR applied to knowledge graphs, the two methods share the same drawbacks.
在图协作推理（GCR）[12]中，NCR 被扩展为与知识图谱一起工作。特别地，他们提供了一种简单的方法，将图结构转换为逻辑表达式，从而将链接预测任务转化为逻辑推理问题。与 NCR 一样，他们使用逻辑约束神经模块根据逻辑表达式构建网络架构。然后，他们使用反向传播有效地学习模型参数，在统一架构中桥接可微学习和符号推理。他们进行了类似于 NCR 的实验，表明 GCR 可以在知识图谱上改善 NCR。然而，由于 GCR 是应用于知识图谱的 NCR 的扩展，这两种方法共享相同的缺点。
In Counterfactual Collaborative Reasoning (CCR) [23], NCR is used to perform data augmentation based on logical reasoning for sequential recommendation. Specifically, counterfactual logic reasoning is exploited to generate counterfactual examples for data augmentation. The examples are generated by discovering slight changes in users' explicit feedback (i.e., the sequence of purchases) by solving a counterfactual optimization problem. They showed that these new examples, together with the original examples, can alleviate scarcity and enhance the performance of sequential recommendations. Furthermore, the CCR framework can generate explicit counterfactual explanations to understand the user behavior sequence.
在反事实协作推理（CCR）[23]中，NCR 被用于基于逻辑推理进行数据增强，以实现序列推荐。具体而言，利用反事实逻辑推理生成反事实示例以进行数据增强。这些示例是通过解决反事实优化问题，发现用户明确反馈（即购买序列）中的细微变化而生成的。他们表明，这些新示例与原始示例结合，可以缓解稀缺性并增强序列推荐的性能。此外，CCR 框架可以生成明确的反事实解释，以理解用户行为序列。
Another approach that successfully integrated logical reasoning and learning has been HYbrid Probabilistic Extensible Recommender (HyPER) [26], which is based on Probabilistic Soft Logic. In particular, HyPER exploits the expressiveness of First-Order Logic (FOL) to encode knowledge from a wide range of information sources, such as multiple user and item similarity measures, content, and social information. For example, the FOL formula SimilarUser ssim (u1,u2)∧Rating(u1,i)⇒Rating(u2,i) is used to express that if users u1 and u2 are similar according to similarity measure sim,and u1 likes item i ,then u2 should also like i . Similar formulas are used to express other kinds of facts. Then, Hinge-Loss Markov Random Fields are used to learn how to balance the different information types. The main concern of HyPER is scalability due to the usage of Markov Logic Networks [35]. HyPER is highly related to our work with LTN since the logical formulas we use resemble the ones used in HyPER.
另一种成功整合逻辑推理和学习的方法是混合概率可扩展推荐系统（HyPER）[26]，它基于概率软逻辑。特别是，HyPER 利用一阶逻辑（FOL）的表达能力，从多种信息源中编码知识，例如多个用户和项目的相似性度量、内容和社交信息。例如，FOL 公式 SimilarUser ssim (u1,u2)∧Rating(u1,i)⇒Rating(u2,i) 用于表达如果用户 u1 和 u2 根据相似性度量 sim 相似，并且 u1 喜欢项目 i，那么 u2 也应该喜欢 i。类似的公式用于表达其他类型的事实。然后，使用铰链损失马尔可夫随机场来学习如何平衡不同的信息类型。HyPER 的主要关注点是由于使用马尔可夫逻辑网络 [35] 而导致的可扩展性。HyPER 与我们的 LTN 工作高度相关，因为我们使用的逻辑公式与 HyPER 中使用的公式相似。
To conclude the review, [37] proposed using a NeSy approach to encode FOL formulas to enhance knowledge graph embeddings [40] and provide accurate knowledge-aware recommendations. Their approach consists of three steps: (i) the FOL formulas are automatically extracted from a recommendation knowledge graph, then (ii) the knowledge graph embeddings are learned jointly with the extracted formulas using a NeSy approach. Finally, (iii) the user-item embeddings are fed to a neural architecture to get predictions. Specifically, in the second step, they used KALE [19], a NeSy approach that allows learning knowledge graph embeddings jointly with FOL formulas. In particular, the learning can be unified as the graph triples can be interpreted as FOL atoms. As KALE uses fuzzy semantics to learn graph embeddings, this approach can be considered more theoretically sound compared to previous deep approaches, as the symbolic component remains symbolic during learning, as it happens with LTN and HyPER. One limitation of the proposed method is that they automatically extract the rules from the same graph where the learning is carried on, meaning the rules could potentially encode redundant information, as already present in the graph structure. We believe a NeSy approach is completely exploited when the formulas encode novel knowledge that is complementary w.r.t. the knowledge the model can learn autonomously. These intuitions can be observed in an ablation study they conducted, where it is evident that the logical formulas do not provide a drastic contribution to the evaluation metrics. Despite that, a comparison with many baselines shows that the proposed approach can reach state-of-the-art performance.
为了总结这项评审，[37] 提出了使用 NeSy 方法对 FOL 公式进行编码，以增强知识图谱嵌入 [40] 并提供准确的知识感知推荐。他们的方法包括三个步骤：（i）从推荐知识图谱中自动提取 FOL 公式，然后（ii）使用 NeSy 方法与提取的公式共同学习知识图谱嵌入。最后，（iii）将用户-项目嵌入输入到神经架构中以获得预测。具体而言，在第二步中，他们使用了 KALE [19]，这是一种允许与 FOL 公式共同学习知识图谱嵌入的 NeSy 方法。特别地，学习可以统一，因为图三元组可以被解释为 FOL 原子。由于 KALE 使用模糊语义来学习图嵌入，因此与之前的深度方法相比，这种方法在理论上被认为更为合理，因为符号组件在学习过程中保持符号性，这与 LTN 和 HyPER 的情况相同。所提出方法的一个限制是，他们从同一图中自动提取规则，而学习也在该图中进行，这意味着规则可能会编码冗余信息，因为这些信息已经存在于图结构中。我们认为，当公式编码与模型能够自主学习的知识互补的新知识时，NeSy 方法才被完全利用。这些直觉可以在他们进行的消融研究中观察到，显然逻辑公式对评估指标没有显著贡献。尽管如此，与许多基线的比较表明，所提出的方法可以达到最先进的性能。
Note that none of the presented works designed experiments to understand if the model can deal with sparsity and/or cold-start, so it is unclear if the literature really exploits the aforementioned advantages of a NeSy system to overcome such historical recommendation limitations.

3 PRELIMINARY EXPERIMENTS
3 初步实验

In our preliminary experiments on Neuro-Symbolic Integration, we selected Logic Tensor Networks (LTN) [5, 8] as the NeSy framework to perform recommendations. LTN allows learning a neural model using the satisfaction of a FOL knowledge base as an objective. Specifically, it defines a FOL language, called Real Logic, that allows mapping every symbolic expression to the domain of real numbers. By doing so, the logical formulas in the knowledge base form a computational graph that can be used for gradient-based optimization. In other words, as the framework uses fuzzy logic, the logical formulas become differentiable and hence can be used as the loss function of the neural network. To this end, Real Logic defines the grounding function G ,which defines the mapping between the symbolic and real domains. Specifically, individuals are mapped to tensors of real values, variable symbols to sequences of individuals, functional symbols to real functions, and predicate symbols to real functions with output in [0.,1.] . In the research field of recommendation systems, choosing the right loss function is critical since it may significantly improve the model's performance [27]. However, designing a loss is challenging due to the complexity of the problem. A NeSy framework like LTN makes this task easier as the loss function can be intuitively expressed as a set of logical axioms. Thanks to the expressiveness of FOL, one can express fine-grained constraints that can represent complex loss functions. Intuitively, u+ and i+ are variable symbols denoting positive user-item pairs,while u− and i− denote negative user-item pairs. Positive pairs are user-item pairs associated with a positive rating, while negative pairs are user-item pairs associated with a negative rating. Clearly, this formalization assumes we are working with explicit feedback, but modifications can be applied to make the knowledge base work with implicit feedback too. In other words, by satisfying this knowledge base, the LTN learns how to train the MF to factorize the user-item matrix using the ground truth (i.e., the target ratings). Note this loss function can be seen as a binary cross-entropy defined in a fuzzy logic way. In fact, this formalization assumes the recommendation problem is treated as a binary classification task, where one has to predict whether a user u likes (i.e.,high truth value) or dislikes (i.e.,low truth value) an item i . In the next section,we present how this simple knowledge base can be enlarged to account for side information that can be used to regularize the MF model by logical reasoning.

4.1 Hybrid recommendation
4.1 混合推荐

In Section 3.1, we showed how LTN could be used to implement a recommendation model based on Collaborative Filtering. It is wellknown that CF cannot deal with cold-start cases, as collaborative information for newly added users and items is unavailable. For this reason, hybrid recommendation models have been proposed [7]. The idea is to merge CF with content-based recommendations in order to deal with cold-start while maintaining all the advantages of CF. Implementing this idea in LTN is straightforward. It involves adding the following axiom to the knowledge base.
在第 3.1 节中，我们展示了如何使用 LTN 实现基于协同过滤的推荐模型。众所周知，协同过滤无法处理冷启动情况，因为新添加的用户和项目缺乏协作信息。因此，提出了混合推荐模型 [7]。其思想是将协同过滤与基于内容的推荐相结合，以应对冷启动，同时保持协同过滤的所有优势。在 LTN 中实现这一想法是直接的。这涉及将以下公理添加到知识库中。

Note the predicate Sim can be implemented as a similarity measure based on content (e.g., movie genres in common) or latent (i.e., pre-trained embeddings) information. The only constraint is the output has to be in the range [0.,1.] as it has to be interpreted as a logical predicate by LTN. Clearly, this formula will help the model deal with cold-start item cases, as when no information is available about some ratings, we are using content information to compensate for this. The LTN will perform hybrid recommendations by finding a trade-off between Axiom (4), Axiom (1), and Axiom (2) in the objective. 4.2 Cross-domain recommendation
4.2 跨领域推荐

Cross-domain recommendation [44] aims at mitigating data sparsity by transferring knowledge acquired from other domains (e.g., books, songs) to the target domain (e.g., movies). The source domain is usually denser than the target domain, as the objective is to improve the performance of the latter one by using high-quality pre-trained knowledge. All the proposed methods share one challenge: finding a way to transfer knowledge between domains. This task, called domain adaptation, requires learning a mapping function between domains. To this end, LTN can be used as an interface for transferring knowledge between domains. To do that, the following axiom can be added to the knowledge base.
跨领域推荐 [44] 旨在通过将从其他领域（例如，书籍、歌曲）获得的知识转移到目标领域（例如，电影）来缓解数据稀疏性。源领域通常比目标领域更密集，因为目标是通过使用高质量的预训练知识来提高后者的性能。所有提出的方法都面临一个挑战：找到在领域之间转移知识的方法。这个任务称为领域适应，要求学习领域之间的映射函数。为此，可以使用 LTN 作为在领域之间转移知识的接口。为此，可以将以下公理添加到知识库中。
∀(u,b,m)Likessource (u,b)∧Sim(b,m)⇒Likestarget (u,m)(6)
In the formalization, u,b ,and m are variable symbols for denoting users,books,and movies,respectively. Then,Likessource is a pre-trained recommendation model on book ratings (i.e., the source domain), while Likestarget represents the recommendation model we want to train on the target domain (i.e., movie ratings in this example). Again, Sim is a predicate that can be implemented using a similarity measure based on content information (e.g., storyline) or latent structure. Intuitively, Axiom (6) states that every time a user u likes a book b in the source domain,if the book is similar to a movie m in the target domain,then u should like m . Clearly, this axiom allows the transfer of information between domains by logical reasoning. We conducted some preliminary experiments based on this idea and obtained promising results. Note one could apply Axiom (6) only when user-movie ratings are missing on the target domain. In such cases, knowing something about the source domain acts as a kind of data augmentation for the target domain and hence helps mitigate data sparsity.
在形式化中，u,b、m 是表示用户、书籍和电影的变量符号。然后，Likessource 是一个在书籍评分（即源领域）上预训练的推荐模型，而 Likestarget 代表我们希望在目标领域（即本示例中的电影评分）上训练的推荐模型。同样，Sim 是一个可以使用基于内容信息（例如，情节）或潜在结构的相似性度量来实现的谓词。直观地说，公理（6）声明每当用户 u 在源领域中喜欢一本书 b 时，如果这本书与目标领域中的一部电影 m 相似，那么 u 应该喜欢 m。显然，这个公理通过逻辑推理允许在领域之间转移信息。我们基于这个想法进行了初步实验，并获得了令人鼓舞的结果。请注意，只有在目标领域缺少用户-电影评分时，才能应用公理（6）。在这种情况下，了解源领域的某些信息充当目标领域的数据增强，从而有助于缓解数据稀疏性。

4.3 Ensemble learning
4.3 集成学习

Ensemble models [15] are machine learning models constructed by aggregating the predictions of multiple models in such a way as to improve performance. In many research areas, ensemble learning has been shown to work better than a single model. We believe LTN can be leveraged to bring the power of ensemble learning to recommender systems. The idea is to encode an axiom for each possible model one wants to include in the ensemble. Then, LTN has to find a trade-off between the predictions of the different models. When LTN aggregates the satisfaction levels of the axioms through SatAgg, they can be interpreted as different types of signals in the loss function. Clearly, one model is usually better than other models in specific situations. For example, in providing novel recommendations or when computing rankings. By aggregating decisions from multiple models, obtaining a more flexible recommendation model is possible. In LTN, it is possible to obtain such an ensemble by encoding the following axioms.
集成模型 [15] 是通过聚合多个模型的预测来构建的机器学习模型，以提高性能。在许多研究领域，集成学习的效果通常优于单一模型。我们相信 LTN 可以被利用，将集成学习的力量引入推荐系统。其思想是为每个希望包含在集成中的可能模型编码一个公理。然后，LTN 必须在不同模型的预测之间找到一个权衡。当 LTN 通过 SatAgg 聚合公理的满意度时，它们可以被解释为损失函数中的不同类型信号。显然，在特定情况下，一个模型通常优于其他模型。例如，在提供新颖推荐或计算排名时。通过聚合多个模型的决策，可以获得一个更灵活的推荐模型。在 LTN 中，可以通过编码以下公理来获得这样的集成。

This holds even if Likesmodel 1 states user u dislikes item i ,as the equivalence operator (i.e., ⇔ ) is used. Intuitively,LTN constructs the ensemble by finding a trade-off between all the selected models.
在形式化中， Likesmodel  . 与 ∗∈{1,2,…,n} 可以是任何最先进的推荐模型。相反，Likes 代表我们试图学习的作为其他模型集成的模型。直观上，这些公理迫使 Likes 像所有指定模型 Likesmodel*  一样表现。举例来说，如果 Likesmodel 1 表示用户 u 喜欢项目 i，那么 Likes 被迫表现得一样。这种情况即使在 Likesmodel 1 表示用户 u 不喜欢项目 i 时也成立，因为使用了等价运算符（即 ⇔）。直观上，LTN 通过在所有选定模型之间找到权衡来构建集成。

4.4 Reduce popularity bias
4.4 减少流行度偏见

Due to the long-tail distribution of user-item ratings, recommendation datasets are usually biased towards popular items [1]. Many recommender systems struggle to remove this bias. Usually, the most popular items must be removed from the test set to evaluate performance on non-trivial recommendations [6]. LTN can be used to decrease the popularity bias by adding this axiom to the knowledge base.
由于用户-项目评分的长尾分布，推荐数据集通常偏向于热门项目 [1]。许多推荐系统在消除这种偏见方面面临困难。通常，必须从测试集中移除最受欢迎的项目，以评估在非平凡推荐上的性能 [6]。LTN 可以通过将此公理添加到知识库中来减少流行度偏见。
(7)∀u,iPopular(i)⇒¬Likes(u,i)
In the formalization, Popular is a predicate symbol denoting whether an item i is popular or not. Note the popularity of an item can be computed or learned. Intuitively, Axiom (7) states that every time an item i is popular,user u should not like i . By forcing the satisfaction of Axiom (7) together with Axiom (1) and Axiom (2), the MF model learns to recommend using the ground truth while automatically decreasing the ratings for popular items, giving more weight to unknown (long-tail) items. This helps generate recommendations with a higher novelty [18].
在形式化中，Popular 是一个谓词符号，表示一个项目 i 是否受欢迎。注意，项目的流行度可以被计算或学习。直观地说，公理 (7) 表示每当一个项目 i 受欢迎时，用户 u 就不应该喜欢 i。通过强制满足公理 (7) 以及公理 (1) 和公理 (2)，MF 模型学习使用真实数据进行推荐，同时自动降低对热门项目的评分，给予未知（长尾）项目更多的权重。这有助于生成更具新颖性的推荐 [18]。

4.5 Explainable recommendation
4.5 可解释的推荐

In the introduction, we argued that one advantage of the NeSy approaches is that they could enable model transparency. In the literature,just a few of them can explain their predictions [23,41,42] . One interesting aspect of LTN is that the framework has not been designed just for learning. In particular, it also provides the possibility to query the knowledge base after the training phase. Querying is the process of grounding the knowledge base with novel data and checking the satisfaction level of its formulas. We believe this feature can be used to provide explanations for recommendations. For example, after we train a model with Axiom (1), Axiom (2), and Axiom (3), we could check the satisfaction level of the following formula for a test user user test to which we recommended the movie Avatar.
在引言中，我们论证了 NeSy 方法的一个优势是它们能够实现模型透明度。在文献中，只有少数方法能够解释它们的预测 [23,41,42]。LTN 的一个有趣方面是该框架并不仅仅是为了学习而设计的。特别是，它还提供了在训练阶段之后查询知识库的可能性。查询是将新数据与知识库进行结合并检查其公式的满足程度的过程。我们相信这个特性可以用来为推荐提供解释。例如，在我们用公理 (1)、公理 (2) 和公理 (3) 训练模型后，我们可以检查以下公式在我们推荐电影《阿凡达》的测试用户 user test 的满足程度。

∃g LikesGenre (usertest ,g)∧ HasGenre(Avatar,g)

Intuitively, if the formula is evaluated with a high truth value, it means our test user likes at least one movie genre of Avatar. Note one can go through the computational graph of the formula to understand exactly which is this genre and provide this finding as an explanation. Finally, thanks to the expressiveness of FOL, it is possible to provide more elaborated explanations based on multiple formulas.
直观地说，如果该公式的评估结果为高真值，这意味着我们的测试用户至少喜欢《阿凡达》的一个电影类型。值得注意的是，可以通过公式的计算图来理解这个类型究竟是什么，并将这一发现作为解释提供。最后，得益于一阶逻辑的表达能力，可以基于多个公式提供更详细的解释。

5 CONCLUSIONS
5 结论

In this paper, we proposed different ways to use a Neuro-Symbolic approach to mitigate important recommender systems' limitations and solve interesting recommendation tasks. In particular, we showed the flexibility of LTN in designing heterogeneous recommendation objectives. This is possible thanks to the expressiveness of FOL.
在本文中，我们提出了不同的方法来使用神经符号方法来缓解重要推荐系统的局限性并解决有趣的推荐任务。特别是，我们展示了逻辑张量网络在设计异构推荐目标方面的灵活性。这得益于一阶逻辑的表达能力。

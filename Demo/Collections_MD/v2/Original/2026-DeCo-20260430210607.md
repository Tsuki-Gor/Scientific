# DeCo: Task Decomposition and Skill Composition for Zero-Shot Generalization in Long-Horizon 3D Manipulation
# DeCo：用于长程 3D 操作零样本泛化的任务分解与技能组合


Zixuan Chen ${}^{\circledR }$ , Junhui Yin, Yangtao Chen, Jing Huo ${}^{\circledR }$ , Member, IEEE, Pinzhuo Tian ${}^{\circledR }$ , Member, IEEE, Jieqi Shi ${}^{\circledR }$ , Member, IEEE, Yiwen Hou ${}^{\circledR }$ , Yinchuan Li ${}^{\circledR }$ , Member, IEEE, and Yang Gao ${}^{\circledR }$
Zixuan Chen ${}^{\circledR }$ , Junhui Yin, Yangtao Chen, Jing Huo ${}^{\circledR }$ , IEEE 会员, Pinzhuo Tian ${}^{\circledR }$ , IEEE 会员, Jieqi Shi ${}^{\circledR }$ , IEEE 会员, Yiwen Hou ${}^{\circledR }$ , Yinchuan Li ${}^{\circledR }$ , IEEE 会员, 以及 Yang Gao ${}^{\circledR }$


Abstract-Generalizing language-conditioned multi-task imitation learning (IL) models to novel long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks is challenging. To address this, we propose DeCo (Task De composition and Skill Co mposition), a model-agnostic framework that enhances zero-shot generalization to compositional long-horizon manipulation tasks. DeCo decomposes IL demonstrations into modular atomic tasks based on gripper-object interactions, creating a dataset that enables models to learn reusable skills. At inference, DeCo uses a vision-language model (VLM) to parse high-level instructions, retrieve relevant skills, and dynamically schedule their execution. A spatially-aware skill-chaining module ensures smooth, collision-free transitions between skills. We introduce De-CoBench, a benchmark designed to evaluate compositional generalization in long-horizon manipulation tasks. DeCo improves the success rate of three IL models—RVT-2, 3DDA, and ARP—by 66.67%, 21.53%, and 57.92%, respectively, on 12 novel tasks. In real-world experiments, the DeCo-enhanced model, trained on only 6 atomic tasks, completes 9 novel tasks in zero-shot, with a 53.33% improvement over the baseline model.
摘要——将语言条件下的多任务模仿学习（IL）模型泛化到新颖的长程 3D 操作任务中极具挑战性。为解决这一问题，我们提出了 DeCo（任务分解与技能组合），这是一个与模型无关的框架，旨在增强模型对组合式长程操作任务的零样本泛化能力。DeCo 基于抓取器与物体的交互将 IL 演示分解为模块化的原子任务，从而构建出一个使模型能够学习可重用技能的数据集。在推理阶段，DeCo 利用视觉语言模型（VLM）解析高层指令，检索相关技能，并动态调度其执行过程。空间感知技能链模块确保了技能间平滑且无碰撞的转换。我们引入了 DeCoBench，这是一个旨在评估长程操作任务中组合泛化能力的基准。DeCo 在 12 个新颖任务上将 RVT-2、3DDA 和 ARP 三种 IL 模型的成功率分别提高了 66.67%、21.53% 和 57.92%。在真实世界实验中，仅在 6 个原子任务上训练的 DeCo 增强模型以零样本方式完成了 9 个新颖任务，较基准模型提升了 53.33%。

## I. INTRODUCTION
## I. 引言


IN RECENT years, imitation learning (IL) has emerged as a mainstream way for robotic manipulation. By leveraging visual demonstrations and language instructions, IL trains language-conditioned multi-task control policies, enabling robots to acquire diverse skills and perform complex tasks in unstructured 3D environments. However, current multi-task IL models still suffer from limited generalization [1], [2], [3], [4], [5], particularly when facing novel long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks [6]-even when such tasks are merely sequential compositions of previously learned skills. For instance, a model may have learned to follow individual instructions such as "open drawer", "put block in opened drawer", and "close drawer", yet still fail to execute the novel instruction "put block into the closed drawer and then close drawer". This failure stems from the model's inability to decompose novel tasks and to retrieve, schedule, and perform the correct composition of its learned skills-failing to recognize that the task can be completed by sequentially executing three known skills: opening the drawer, placing the block, and then closing the drawer. Such limitations in task decomposition and skill composition severely undermine the real-world applicability and scalability of current multi-task IL models. Although vision-language models (VLMs) have been used to generate subtasks for long-horizon tasks via instruction plans [6], [7], [8], executable code [9], [10], spatial keypoints [11], [12], or affordance maps [13], [14], they often fail to align high-level semantic plans with low-level execution. Low-level tasks are typically limited to simple motion planning or pretrained skills, and the semantic decomposition does not directly map to the physical skill space. This gap limits the effective composition of low-level skills, ultimately hindering zero-shot performance on long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks [15], [16], [17].
近年来，模仿学习（IL）已成为机器人操作的主流方式。通过利用视觉演示和语言指令，IL 训练出语言条件下的多任务控制策略，使机器人能够获取多种技能并在非结构化 3D 环境中执行复杂任务。然而，当前的多任务 IL 模型仍存在泛化能力有限的问题 [1], [2], [3], [4], [5]，特别是在面对新颖的长程 3D 操作任务时 [6]——即使这些任务仅仅是先前学习技能的顺序组合。例如，模型可能已经学会了遵循“打开抽屉”、“将积木放入打开的抽屉”和“关闭抽屉”等独立指令，但在执行“将积木放入关闭的抽屉并关闭抽屉”这一新颖指令时仍会失败。这种失败源于模型无法分解新颖任务，也无法检索、调度并执行其已学技能的正确组合——未能识别出该任务可以通过依次执行三个已知技能来完成：打开抽屉、放置积木，然后关闭抽屉。这种在任务分解和技能组合方面的局限性严重削弱了当前多任务 IL 模型的实际应用价值和可扩展性。尽管视觉语言模型（VLM）已被用于通过指令规划 [6], [7], [8]、可执行代码 [9], [10]、空间关键点 [11], [12] 或可供性图 [13], [14] 为长程任务生成子任务，但它们往往难以将高层语义规划与底层执行对齐。底层任务通常局限于简单的运动规划或预训练技能，且语义分解无法直接映射到物理技能空间。这种差距限制了底层技能的有效组合，最终阻碍了长程 3D 操作任务的零样本性能 [15], [16], [17]。


In this paper, we address the following core question: How can long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks be decomposed into learned skills such that multi-task imitation learning (IL) models can interpret their structure, plan accordingly, and successfully complete novel, compositional tasks without additional training? To this end, we propose DeCo (Task De composition and Skill Co mpositon), a model-agnostic framework compatible with a wide range of multi-task IL models. DeCo enhances the zero-shot generalization of multi-task IL models to novel, compositional long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks-tasks that are unseen during training but can be solved by composing previously learned skills through visual and semantic reasoning, as illustrated in Fig. 1. Specifically, DeCo enables IL models to decompose novel tasks into reusable atomic skills, flexibly schedule them, and execute skill sequences without additional training.
在本文中，我们探讨了以下核心问题：如何将长程 3D 操作任务分解为已学技能，使得多任务模仿学习（IL）模型能够理解其结构、进行相应规划，并在无需额外训练的情况下成功完成新颖的组合式任务？为此，我们提出了 DeCo（任务分解与技能组合），这是一个与模型无关的框架，兼容多种多任务 IL 模型。DeCo 增强了多任务 IL 模型对新颖组合式长程 3D 操作任务的零样本泛化能力——这些任务在训练期间未曾见过，但可以通过视觉和语义推理组合先前学习的技能来解决，如图 1 所示。具体而言，DeCo 使 IL 模型能够将新颖任务分解为可重用的原子技能，灵活调度它们，并在无需额外训练的情况下执行技能序列。

DeCo consists of three key components. First, inspired by how humans decompose long-horizon tasks through hand-object interactions, DeCo proposes a new perspective on training datasets for multi-task IL, building upon prior methods of sub-task discovery in manipulation [14], [18]. It preprocesses original IL demonstrations by analyzing the physical interactions between the gripper and objects, decomposing them into a set of modular and reusable atomic tasks. Each task is paired with a natural language instruction and a goal pose, forming a training dataset of atomic tasks for training multi-task IL models to acquire diverse skills. Second, during testing, DeCo uses vision-language models (VLMs) to parse novel language instructions and visual inputs, retrieve relevant atomic instructions from the training dataset, and generate an execution plan. The multi-task IL model sequentially executes the skills, while DeCo monitors task progress via gripper interactions, enabling dynamic scheduling and flexible skill composition. Finally, to ensure smooth transitions between skills, DeCo builds a spatially aware cost map for the scene to calculate collision-free chaining poses, guiding the robot between sequential skills and ensuring motion continuity and safety. Extensive evaluations utilize DeCoBench, a benchmark built upon RLBench [19] to systematically evaluate zero-shot compositional generalization. Three representative models—RVT-2 [3], 3DDA [20], and ARP [5]—are integrated with DeCo, showing significantly improved generalization performance. In real-world settings, 6 atomic training tasks enable the zero-shot execution of 9 novel long-horizon tasks, validating practical applicability.
DeCo由三个关键部分组成。首先，受人类通过手-物交互分解长程任务的启发，DeCo在借鉴现有操作子任务发现方法[14]、[18]的基础上，为多任务模仿学习（IL）的训练数据集提出了新视角。它通过分析夹爪与物体间的物理交互来预处理原始IL演示数据，将其分解为一系列模块化且可复用的原子任务。每个任务均配有自然语言指令和目标位姿，从而构建出用于训练多任务IL模型以获取多样化技能的原子任务数据集。其次，在测试阶段，DeCo利用视觉语言模型（VLM）解析新颖的语言指令和视觉输入，从训练数据集中检索相关的原子指令并生成执行计划。多任务IL模型按顺序执行这些技能，同时DeCo通过夹爪交互监控任务进度，实现动态调度和灵活的技能组合。最后，为确保技能间的平滑过渡，DeCo构建了场景的空间感知代价地图，以计算无碰撞的衔接位姿，引导机器人在连续技能间转换，确保运动的连续性和安全性。广泛的评估使用了DeCoBench，这是一个基于RLBench [19]构建的基准测试，旨在系统地评估零样本组合泛化能力。我们将三种代表性模型——RVT-2 [3]、3DDA [20]和ARP [5]——与DeCo集成，结果显示其泛化性能得到了显著提升。在真实世界场景中，仅需6个原子训练任务即可实现9个新颖长程任务的零样本执行，验证了其实际应用价值。


In summary, our main contributions are as follows: (1) The proposal of DeCo, a model-agnostic framework designed to equip multi-task IL models with zero-shot generalization capabilities for novel yet compositional long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks. (2) The development of DeCoBench to systematically evaluate compositional generalization, complemented by extensive real-world validation. Results demonstrate that DeCo enhances the performance of representative multitask IL models and achieves robust zero-shot generalization on novel long-horizon tasks.
总之，我们的主要贡献如下：（1）提出了DeCo，这是一个模型无关的框架，旨在赋予多任务IL模型针对新颖且可组合的长程3D操作任务的零样本泛化能力。（2）开发了DeCoBench以系统地评估组合泛化能力，并辅以广泛的真实世界验证。结果表明，DeCo增强了代表性多任务IL模型的性能，并在新颖的长程任务上实现了稳健的零样本泛化。


## II. RELATED WORK
## II. 相关工作


This section reviews recent advancements in learning manipulation policies from demonstrations and strategies for long-horizon manipulation, highlighting the persistent challenges in achieving zero-shot compositional generalization.
本节回顾了从演示中学习操作策略及长程操作策略的最新进展，并强调了在实现零样本组合泛化方面所面临的持续挑战。


Learning Manipulation Policies from Demonstrations: Learning manipulation policies from offline visual demonstrations has garnered significant attention, fueled by advances in visual perception [21], [22]. Early 2D-based approaches [15], [23], [24], [25], [26], [27], [28] have demonstrated success in simple pick-and-place tasks, benefiting from fast training, low hardware requirements, and modest computational demands. However, their reliance on pretrained image encoders and limited spatial understanding makes them less effective for tasks requiring high-precision and robust 3D interactions. To address this, works such as C2F-ARM [29] and PerAct [1] extend learning to 6-DoF actions in 3D environments, but they still require training separate task-specific policies. More recent efforts [2], [3], [5], [6], [14], [20], [30], [31], [32] aim to develop unified multi-task imitation learning (IL) models that can perform diverse tasks from heterogeneous demonstrations. This shift is crucial for building general-purpose robotic agents. However, most of these models are limited to tasks observed during training, and particularly struggle to generalize to novel long-horizon scenarios, which hinders their deployment in real-world applications [6]. To address this, we propose a model-agnostic framework compatible with multi-task IL models for zero-shot generalization to novel long-horizon tasks.
从演示中学习操作策略：得益于视觉感知领域的进展 [21], [22]，从离线视觉演示中学习操作策略已引起广泛关注。早期的基于 2D 的方法 [15], [23], [24], [25], [26], [27], [28] 在简单的抓取放置任务中表现出色，具有训练速度快、硬件要求低及计算需求适中等优势。然而，它们对预训练图像编码器的依赖以及有限的空间理解能力，使其在需要高精度和稳健 3D 交互的任务中表现欠佳。为解决这一问题，C2F-ARM [29] 和 PerAct [1] 等研究将学习扩展至 3D 环境中的 6-DoF 动作，但仍需针对不同任务训练独立的策略。近期研究 [2], [3], [5], [6], [14], [20], [30], [31], [32] 致力于开发统一的多任务模仿学习 (IL) 模型，以通过异构演示执行多样化任务。这种转变对于构建通用机器人智能体至关重要。然而，大多数此类模型仅限于执行训练中见过的任务，在泛化至新颖的长程场景时尤为困难，这阻碍了其在现实世界中的部署 [6]。为此，我们提出了一种与多任务 IL 模型兼容的模型无关框架，旨在实现对新颖长程任务的零样本泛化。


Methods for Long-Horizon Manipulation: A common strategy for long-horizon manipulation is to decompose complex tasks into sequential subtasks using predefined action primitives (e.g., grasp, place, pull) [33], [34], [35] or environment-specific cues [15], [36], [37], [38], [39], [40], [41]. While effective in structured settings, these approaches lack compositional flexibility and generalization, making them fragile to goal shifts and environmental changes, and limiting scalability in multi-task IL. Meanwhile, VLMs have shown promise in high-level planning by decomposing instructions into natural language, executable code, spatial keypoints, or affordance maps [7], [8], [9], [10], [13], [14]. However, bridging high-level semantic plans with flexible low-level execution remains challenging. For example, Points2Plans [42] grounds LLM plans via relational dynamics, but still relies on parameterized action primitives rather than adaptive policies. As a result, low-level behaviors remain constrained to predefined skills, and semantic decomposition often fails to align with the physical skill space, limiting composition and generalization in long-horizon 3D manipulation [15], [16], [17]. To address these limitations, we propose a modular and reusable atomic task construction that enables consistent decomposition across diverse scenarios and compatibility with multi-task IL models. We further introduce a spatially-aware skill chaining module with collision avoidance. Combined with VLM-guided planning, our framework improves generalization and robustness in compositional long-horizon manipulation.
长时程操作方法：长时程操作的常见策略是使用预定义的动作基元（例如抓取、放置、拉动）[33]、[34]、[35]或特定于环境的线索[15]、[36]、[37]、[38]、[39]、[40]、[41]将复杂任务分解为顺序子任务。虽然这些方法在结构化环境中有效，但它们缺乏组合灵活性和泛化能力，使其在目标变化和环境改变时较为脆弱，并限制了多任务模仿学习（IL）的可扩展性。同时，视觉语言模型（VLMs）通过将指令分解为自然语言、可执行代码、空间关键点或可供性地图，在高级规划方面展现出了潜力[7]、[8]、[9]、[10]、[13]、[14]。然而，将高级语义规划与灵活的低级执行相衔接仍然具有挑战性。例如，Points2Plans [42]通过关系动力学来落实大语言模型（LLM）的规划，但仍然依赖于参数化的动作基元，而非自适应策略。因此，低级行为仍然局限于预定义的技能，并且语义分解往往无法与物理技能空间对齐，从而限制了长时程三维操作中的组合和泛化能力[15]、[16]、[17]。为解决这些局限性，我们提出了一种模块化且可复用的原子任务构建方法，该方法能够在不同场景下实现一致的分解，并与多任务IL模型兼容。我们进一步引入了一个具有避障功能的空间感知技能链模块。结合VLM引导的规划，我们的框架提高了组合式长时程操作的泛化能力和鲁棒性。

## III. METHOD
## III. 方法


## A. Problem Formulation
## A. 问题定义


We describe the end-effector pose with the position vector and orientation unit quaternion of the gripper, which fully characterize its spatial state and serve as the key action parameter in manipulation tasks. We define the physical interaction as the contact event between a robotic gripper and an object, identified by changes in the gripper's openness. A single change in the gripper (open to closed or vice versa) is a cycle. A full interaction, denoted as ${p}^{\text{ full }}$ ,consists of two cycles: open $\rightarrow$ closed $\rightarrow$ open. A half interaction, ${p}^{\text{ half }}$ ,represents a single change from open to closed or vice versa: ${p}^{\text{ full }} = {p}_{o \rightarrow  c}^{\text{ half }} \rightarrow  {p}_{c \rightarrow  o}^{\text{ half }}$ ,where ${p}_{o \rightarrow  c}^{\text{ half }}$ and ${p}_{c \rightarrow  o}^{\text{ half }}$ represent the two sub-phases of the gripper transition. A visual illustration of full and half interactions is shown in Fig. 3. We assume access to a training task set ${\mathcal{T}}^{o} = \left\{  {{T}_{1}^{o},{T}_{2}^{o},\ldots ,{T}_{n}^{o}}\right\}$ (subscript $o$ denotes original tasks),each paired with a natural language instruction ${\ell }_{i}^{o}$ . However,the physical interaction phases within each ${T}_{i}^{o}$ are often inconsistent. By decomposing tasks using predefined interaction boundaries, we construct an atomic task set ${\mathcal{T}}^{a} = \left\{  {{T}_{1}^{a},{T}_{2}^{a},\ldots ,{T}_{m}^{a}}\right\}$ (subscript $a$ denotes atomic tasks,typically $m > n$ ) and a corresponding instruction library ${\mathcal{L}}^{a} = \left\{  {{\ell }_{1}^{a},{\ell }_{2}^{a},\ldots ,{\ell }_{m}^{a}}\right\}$ . Each atomic task contains a consistent interaction cycle,either ${p}^{\text{ full }}$ or ${p}^{\text{ half }}$ . We frame 3D manipulation as keyframe prediction [1], [2], [14], [29], [43].A language-conditioned multi-task IL model $\mathcal{M}$ takes as input the observation ${o}_{t}$ (RGB-D) and instruction $\ell$ ,and predicts a 6-DoF end-effector pose and a 1-DoF gripper state [1], [2]. Trained on ${\mathcal{T}}^{a}$ ,the model $\mathcal{M}$ learns a policy $\pi$ to solve any ${T}_{i}^{a} \in  {\mathcal{T}}^{a}$ . Our objective is for the enhanced model $\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo to generalize zero-shot to a novel compositional long-horizon task ${T}^{\text{ new }}$ ,decomposable into atomic steps: ${T}^{\text{ new }} = {T}_{x}^{a} \rightarrow  {T}_{y}^{a} \rightarrow  \cdots  \rightarrow \; {T}_{z}^{a}$ . At inference, $\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo retrieves,schedules,and executes atomic skills corresponding to $\left\{  {{\ell }_{x}^{a},\ldots ,{\ell }_{z}^{a}}\right\}$ ,enabling zero-shot generalization.
我们用末端执行器的位置向量和方向单位四元数来描述其位姿，这完整刻画了其空间状态，并作为操作任务中的关键动作参数。我们将物理交互定义为机器人夹爪与物体之间的接触事件，通过夹爪开合状态的变化来识别。夹爪的一次状态改变（从开到闭或反之）为一个周期。一次完整交互记为 ${p}^{\text{ full }}$，包含两个周期：开 $\rightarrow$ 闭 $\rightarrow$ 开。一次半交互 ${p}^{\text{ half }}$ 表示从开到闭或反之的单次改变：${p}^{\text{ full }} = {p}_{o \rightarrow  c}^{\text{ half }} \rightarrow  {p}_{c \rightarrow  o}^{\text{ half }}$，其中 ${p}_{o \rightarrow  c}^{\text{ half }}$ 和 ${p}_{c \rightarrow  o}^{\text{ half }}$ 代表夹爪转换的两个子阶段。完整交互和半交互的视觉示意图见图 3。我们假设可以访问训练任务集 ${\mathcal{T}}^{o} = \left\{  {{T}_{1}^{o},{T}_{2}^{o},\ldots ,{T}_{n}^{o}}\right\}$（下标 $o$ 表示原始任务），每个任务都配有自然语言指令 ${\ell }_{i}^{o}$。然而，每个 ${T}_{i}^{o}$ 内的物理交互阶段往往不一致。通过利用预定义的交互边界分解任务，我们构建了一个原子任务集 ${\mathcal{T}}^{a} = \left\{  {{T}_{1}^{a},{T}_{2}^{a},\ldots ,{T}_{m}^{a}}\right\}$（下标 $a$ 表示原子任务，通常为 $m > n$）及相应的指令库 ${\mathcal{L}}^{a} = \left\{  {{\ell }_{1}^{a},{\ell }_{2}^{a},\ldots ,{\ell }_{m}^{a}}\right\}$。每个原子任务包含一个一致的交互周期，即 ${p}^{\text{ full }}$ 或 ${p}^{\text{ half }}$。我们将 3D 操作建模为关键帧预测 [1], [2], [14], [29], [43]。语言条件多任务模仿学习模型 $\mathcal{M}$ 以观测 ${o}_{t}$ (RGB-D) 和指令 $\ell$ 为输入，预测 6 自由度末端执行器位姿和 1 自由度夹爪状态 [1], [2]。在 ${\mathcal{T}}^{a}$ 上训练后，模型 $\mathcal{M}$ 学习到策略 $\pi$ 以解决任意 ${T}_{i}^{a} \in  {\mathcal{T}}^{a}$。我们的目标是使增强模型 $\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo 能够零样本泛化到新的组合式长程任务 ${T}^{\text{ new }}$，该任务可分解为原子步骤：${T}^{\text{ new }} = {T}_{x}^{a} \rightarrow  {T}_{y}^{a} \rightarrow  \cdots  \rightarrow \; {T}_{z}^{a}$。在推理时，$\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo 检索、调度并执行对应于 $\left\{  {{\ell }_{x}^{a},\ldots ,{\ell }_{z}^{a}}\right\}$ 的原子技能，从而实现零样本泛化。


## B. Physical Interaction-Based Task Decomposition and Skill Learning
## B. 基于物理交互的任务分解与技能学习


To construct modular and reusable atomic skills, DeCo proposes a novel task decomposition strategy inspired by human hand-object interactions and prior work [14], [18]. This decomposition is based on the physical interactions of the robotic gripper, as described in Section III-A. For the original demonstrations, ${\mathcal{T}}^{o}$ ,used to train the multi-task IL model $\mathcal{M}$ ,DeCo decomposes tasks based on full physical interactions ${p}^{\text{ full }}$ . For instance, a demonstration for the instruction "put item in a closed drawer without closing the drawer" can be divided into two atomic tasks: "open drawer" and "place item into open drawer", each aligned with a full gripper interaction. After decomposition, DeCo reformats the atomic demonstrations for skill learning. Each atomic task ${T}_{i}^{a}$ is paired with a language instruction ${\ell }_{i}^{a}$ ,forming the instruction library ${\mathcal{L}}^{a}$ . Demonstrations are processed using a keyframe discovery method [43] that identifies keyframes based on gripper state transitions or near-zero joint velocities. Each demonstration concludes with a full physical interaction ${p}^{\text{ full }}$ ,and the end-effector pose in the final keyframe is marked as the goal pose defined in the robot base frame. Optionally, demonstrations may include temporal data (e.g., time steps) to support task progression modeling. Finally, $\mathcal{M} + \mathrm{{DeCo}}$ is trained with these physically consistent atomic datasets, enabling it to effectively acquire multiple atomic skills. The objective is to learn a language-conditioned policy ${\pi }_{{\theta }^{ * }}^{a}$ that maps observation-instruction pairs to actions: ${\theta }^{ * } = \arg \mathop{\min }\limits_{\theta }{\mathbb{E}}_{i,\left( {o,a}\right) }\left\lbrack  {{\mathcal{L}}_{\text{ MT-IL }}\left( {{\pi }_{\theta }^{a}\left( {o,{\ell }_{i}^{a}}\right) ,a}\right) }\right\rbrack$ ,where ${\pi }_{{\theta }^{ * }}^{a}\left( {o,{\ell }_{i}^{a}}\right)  = {\mathcal{M}}_{{\theta }^{ * }}\left( {o,{\ell }_{i}^{a}}\right)$ . Unless otherwise stated,all training datasets of atomic tasks and experimental results of $\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo presented in the main paper are based on ${p}^{\text{ full }}$ . To explore the suitable granularity of physical interaction, we also implement a DeCo variant based on ${p}^{\text{ half }}$ . Ablation study results are discussed in Section V-D.
为了构建模块化且可复用的原子技能，DeCo 提出了一种受人类手-物交互及前人工作 [14], [18] 启发的创新任务分解策略。该分解基于机器人夹爪的物理交互，如第 III-A 节所述。对于用于训练多任务模仿学习（IL）模型 $\mathcal{M}$ 的原始演示 ${\mathcal{T}}^{o}$，DeCo 基于完整物理交互 ${p}^{\text{ full }}$ 对任务进行分解。例如，针对“将物品放入抽屉但不关闭抽屉”这一指令的演示，可被划分为两个原子任务：“打开抽屉”和“将物品放入打开的抽屉”，每个任务均与一次完整的夹爪交互对齐。分解后，DeCo 会重新格式化原子演示以进行技能学习。每个原子任务 ${T}_{i}^{a}$ 均配有一个语言指令 ${\ell }_{i}^{a}$，从而构成指令库 ${\mathcal{L}}^{a}$。演示数据通过关键帧发现方法 [43] 进行处理，该方法基于夹爪状态转换或接近零的关节速度来识别关键帧。每个演示均以一次完整的物理交互 ${p}^{\text{ full }}$ 结束，且最终关键帧中的末端执行器位姿被标记为在机器人基座坐标系下定义的目标位姿。演示数据可选择性地包含时间数据（如时间步长）以支持任务进度建模。最后，$\mathcal{M} + \mathrm{{DeCo}}$ 使用这些物理一致的原子数据集进行训练，使其能够有效地习得多种原子技能。其目标是学习一种语言条件策略 ${\pi }_{{\theta }^{ * }}^{a}$，将观测-指令对映射为动作：${\theta }^{ * } = \arg \mathop{\min }\limits_{\theta }{\mathbb{E}}_{i,\left( {o,a}\right) }\left\lbrack  {{\mathcal{L}}_{\text{ MT-IL }}\left( {{\pi }_{\theta }^{a}\left( {o,{\ell }_{i}^{a}}\right) ,a}\right) }\right\rbrack$，其中 ${\pi }_{{\theta }^{ * }}^{a}\left( {o,{\ell }_{i}^{a}}\right)  = {\mathcal{M}}_{{\theta }^{ * }}\left( {o,{\ell }_{i}^{a}}\right)$。除非另有说明，正文中呈现的所有原子任务训练数据集及 $\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo 的实验结果均基于 ${p}^{\text{ full }}$。为探索物理交互的合适粒度，我们还实现了一个基于 ${p}^{\text{ half }}$ 的 DeCo 变体。消融研究结果将在第 V-D 节中讨论。


## C. VLM-Guided Planning and Skill Scheduling
## C. VLM 引导的规划与技能调度


After a multi-task imitation learning (IL) model $\mathcal{M}$ has mastered multiple atomic skills, DeCo enables it to generalize to novel long-horizon tasks through skill composition. Given a novel yet compositional task ${T}^{\text{ new }},\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo first invokes the vision-language model (VLM) GPT-40 [44] with the task instructions, observed RGB images, and a pre-built atomic instruction library. Leveraging the VLM's reasoning and planning capabilities, the system retrieves relevant atomic instructions and produces an ordered skill sequence to accomplish the task. The low-level execution of each atomic skill is carried out entirely by the IL policy $\mathcal{M}$ ,while DeCo provides only goal-pose supervision and skill-level scheduling. To connect consecutive skills, DeCo does not rely on a conventional global motion planner; instead, it employs a short-horizon, spatially constrained motion-bridging module. This module generates collision-aware transitional motions between the previous skill's goal pose and the next skill's start pose, while keeping the trajectory close to the demonstrated state distribution, thereby mitigating policy distribution shift. During execution, the system continuously monitors the robot pose and checks it against the goal pose of the current skill. A successful match indicates completion of the atomic skill—corresponding to a full gripper interaction cycle—and triggers the execution of the next skill; otherwise, the current skill continues until the goal pose is reached. This pose-based closed-loop monitoring mechanism ensures reliable skill termination without modifying the underlying policy. Additional implementation details, including VLM prompts, are provided in the supplementary materials on our project website.
在多任务模仿学习（IL）模型 $\mathcal{M}$ 掌握多种原子技能后，DeCo 使其能够通过技能组合泛化至新颖的长程任务。给定一个新颖但可组合的任务 ${T}^{\text{ new }},\mathcal{M} +$，DeCo 首先调用视觉语言模型（VLM）GPT-4o [44]，输入任务指令、观测到的 RGB 图像以及预先构建的原子指令库。利用 VLM 的推理和规划能力，系统检索相关的原子指令并生成有序的技能序列以完成任务。每个原子技能的底层执行完全由 IL 策略 $\mathcal{M}$ 完成，而 DeCo 仅提供目标位姿监督和技能级调度。为了连接连续的技能，DeCo 不依赖传统的全局运动规划器，而是采用了一个短程、空间受限的运动桥接模块。该模块在上一技能的目标位姿与下一技能的起始位姿之间生成具有避障意识的过渡运动，同时使轨迹保持在演示状态分布附近，从而减轻策略分布偏移。在执行过程中，系统持续监测机器人位姿并将其与当前技能的目标位姿进行比对。匹配成功即表示原子技能完成（对应一个完整的夹爪交互周期），并触发下一技能的执行；否则，当前技能将持续运行直至达到目标位姿。这种基于位姿的闭环监测机制确保了可靠的技能终止，且无需修改底层策略。包括 VLM 提示词在内的更多实现细节，请参阅我们项目网站上的补充材料。


## D. Spatially-Aware Skill Chaining
## D. 空间感知技能链


Although $\mathcal{M} + \mathrm{{DeCo}}$ can semantically combine atomic skills to accomplish long-horizon tasks via VLM-guided planning and scheduling (see Section III-C), challenges remain in executing them sequentially. A key issue is achieving smooth transitions between atomic skills in 3D space. Each skill learned by $\mathcal{M}$ has distinct start and goal poses, often causing large spatial discontinuities between successive skills. Traditional motion planners, while avoiding collisions, lack semantic-spatial awareness of policy compatibility. They may select geometrically feasible transitions that lie in low-confidence regions of the next policy, leading to failures. To address this, DeCo introduces a spatially-aware skill chaining module that enables seamless transitions without modifying pose distributions. Once the current skill completes—i.e., the robot reaches the goal pose—the system schedules the next instruction and predicts its start pose. The current goal pose, predicted next start pose, and scene point cloud are passed to a spatially-aware cost map module adapted from Voxposer [13]. This module generates collision-free chaining poses bridging the two skills, as shown in the third part of Fig. 2. The robot then performs RRT-based [45] motion planning over these poses to ensure safe transitions. Operating during skill handoff in $\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo,this module enables smooth composition and reliable execution of long-horizon 3D manipulation.
尽管 $\mathcal{M} + \mathrm{{DeCo}}$ 可以通过 VLM 引导的规划与调度（见第 III-C 节）在语义上组合原子技能以完成长程任务，但在顺序执行这些技能时仍存在挑战。一个关键问题是如何在 3D 空间中实现原子技能间的平滑过渡。由 $\mathcal{M}$ 学习到的每个技能都有明确的起始位姿和目标位姿，这往往会导致连续技能之间出现巨大的空间不连续性。传统的运动规划器虽然能避开碰撞，但缺乏对策略兼容性的语义-空间感知。它们可能会选择几何上可行但处于下一策略低置信度区域的过渡路径，从而导致失败。为解决这一问题，DeCo 引入了一个空间感知技能链模块，能够在不修改位姿分布的情况下实现无缝过渡。当当前技能完成（即机器人到达目标位姿）后，系统会调度下一个指令并预测其起始位姿。当前目标位姿、预测的下一起始位姿以及场景点云会被输入到改编自 Voxposer [13] 的空间感知代价地图模块中。如图 2 第三部分所示，该模块生成连接两个技能的无碰撞链式位姿。随后，机器人基于这些位姿执行 RRT [45] 运动规划，以确保过渡安全。该模块在 $\mathcal{M} +$ DeCo 的技能交接过程中运行，实现了长程 3D 操作的平滑组合与可靠执行。

## IV. DeCoBench: BENCHMARK OVERVIEW
## IV. DeCoBench：基准测试概览


DeCoBench is presented as a benchmark for evaluating zero-shot generalization in novel compositional long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks, as shown in Fig. 4. While DeCoBench is heavily inspired by RLBench [19], it is specifically designed to focus on the compositionality of tasks and their ability to generalize across different task compositions. DeCoBench covers three domains: Object Rearrangement with Drawer, Object Rearrangement with Cupboard, and Rubbish Cleanup. In the Object Rearrangement with Drawer domain, original tasks-put item in drawer without close and take item out of drawer and close-are decomposed into four atomic tasks based on the gripper's interaction cycle: open drawer, close drawer, put item in drawer, and take item out of drawer. A variant atomic task, take box out of drawer, is created via object substitution. This domain includes two 4-cycle tasks, five 6-cycle tasks, and two 10-cycle tasks. In the Object Rearrangement with Cupboard domain, exchange boxes is decomposed into box in cupboard and box out of cupboard, with take broom out of cupboard introduced as an additional task. The exchange boxes task serves as a 4-cycle compositional long-horizon task. In the Rubbish Cleanup domain, sweep and drop rubbish is decomposed into sweep rubbish to dustpan and drop rubbish in dustpan, with the original task serving as a 4-cycle compositional task. The benchmark further incorporates two cross-domain compositional tasks: transfer box (6 cycles) across Drawer and Cupboard, and retrieve and sweep (4 cycles) across Cupboard and Cleanup. ## V. EXPERIMENTS
## V. 实验


DeCo is evaluated in both simulated and real-world environments. Specifically, the following research questions are addressed: 1) To what extent does DeCo enhance the generalization of multi-task IL models on long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks (Section V-B)? 2) Can the framework achieve robust generalization in real-world long-horizon manipulation tasks (Section V-C)? 3) How do heuristic settings in DeCo influence its generalization performance (Section V-D)?
DeCo 在模拟和真实世界环境中均进行了评估。具体而言，本文探讨了以下研究问题：1) DeCo 在多大程度上增强了多任务模仿学习（IL）模型在长程 3D 操作任务上的泛化能力（第 V-B 节）？2) 该框架能否在真实世界的长程操作任务中实现稳健的泛化（第 V-C 节）？3) DeCo 中的启发式设置如何影响其泛化性能（第 V-D 节）？


## A. Experimental Setup
## A. 实验设置


Baseline Multi-task IL Models: DeCo is applied to three representative multi-task IL models—RVT-2 [3], 3DDA [20], and ARP [5]—to validate its model-agnostic design and demonstrate its generalization benefits. RVT-2 is a multi-view robotic transformer using a coarse-to-fine strategy on point clouds to predict the next-best action heatmap. 3DDA combines 3D scene representations with a diffusion-based policy for manipulation. ARP employs a Chunking Causal Transformer [5] to autoregres-sively generate action sequences for manipulation tasks.
基准多任务模仿学习模型：我们将 DeCo 应用于三种代表性的多任务模仿学习模型——RVT-2 [3]、3DDA [20] 和 ARP [5]，以验证其模型无关的设计并展示其在泛化方面的优势。RVT-2 是一种多视图机器人 Transformer，利用点云上的由粗到细策略来预测最优动作热图。3DDA 将 3D 场景表示与基于扩散的策略相结合以进行操作。ARP 采用分块因果 Transformer [5] 来自回归生成操作任务的动作序列。


Simulation Setup: Simulation experiments are conducted on the proposed DeCoBench benchmark. Demonstrations are generated using scripted policies, with goal poses defined in the robot base frame. Observations are collected from four RGB-D cameras at the front, left shoulder, right shoulder, and wrist. RVT-2 and ARP use ${128} \times  {128}$ image inputs,while 3DDA uses ${256} \times  {256}$ ,following original settings. Each baseline and its DeCo-enhanced variant are trained on atomic tasks (50 demonstrations per task) and evaluated on compositional tasks (20 test demonstrations per task). All policies are evaluated with three random seeds. For fair comparison, original training configurations are used: batch size 24 for RVT-2, 48 for ARP, and 8 for 3DDA. All models are trained on 8 NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4090 GPUs.
仿真设置：仿真实验在提出的 DeCoBench 基准上进行。演示数据由脚本策略生成，目标位姿定义在机器人基座坐标系中。观测数据采集自前、左肩、右肩和手腕处的四个 RGB-D 相机。遵循原始设置，RVT-2 和 ARP 使用 ${128} \times  {128}$ 图像输入，而 3DDA 使用 ${256} \times  {256}$ 。每个基准模型及其 DeCo 增强版本均在原子任务上进行训练（每个任务 50 条演示），并在组合任务上进行评估（每个任务 20 条测试演示）。所有策略均使用三个随机种子进行评估。为确保公平比较，采用了原始训练配置：RVT-2 的批大小为 24，ARP 为 48，3DDA 为 8。所有模型均在 8 张 NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4090 GPU 上进行训练。


Real-robot Setup: DeCo is validated on a Franka Emika Panda robot equipped with an exocentric Intel RealSense D435i camera, as shown in Fig. 6 (left). RVT-2 and RVT-2+DeCo are compared on an object rearrangement task involving a drawer. Training uses 6 atomic tasks (16 variations) collected via kinesthetic teaching ( $\approx  5$ mins per task),while testing covers 9 long-horizon tasks (30 variations) for zero-shot generalization. ## B. Generalization Performance on DeCoBench
## B. DeCoBench 上的泛化性能


Table I shows the performance of various models on 10 atomic tasks in DeCoBench. While 3DDA achieves the highest overall success rate, ARP and RVT-2 also demonstrate competitive performance. However, when DeCo is integrated into these models, some tasks show a decrease in performance. This can be attributed to the instability introduced by the VLM during atomic task planning, which negatively affects the accuracy of instruction-following in multi-task IL models. This drop primarily stems from VLM visual-semantic grounding errors: 1) state estimation hallucinations (e.g., falsely detecting occlusions) trigger unnecessary prerequisite skills (over-planning), disrupting the execution flow; and 2) instruction distribution shifts, where VLM-generated instructions differ from training data, causing the IL policy to estimate suboptimal goal poses. Notably, baseline models rely on ground-truth instructions they encountered during training, which enables them to perform better on these tasks. In contrast, Table II highlights DeCo's effectiveness in novel task generalization. It presents the performance of RVT-2, 3DDA, and ARP on 12 long-horizon tasks after being trained on 10 atomic tasks, alongside their DeCo-enhanced counterparts. While the baseline models excel at atomic tasks (as shown in Table I), they struggle significantly with long-horizon tasks, failing to generalize atomic skills to more complex scenarios. This failure stems from two fundamental limitations: the inability to reason about unseen temporal dependencies and perceptual aliasing in circular loops. Without explicit task decomposition, baselines cannot infer necessary pre-conditions (e.g., opening before placing) from high-level instructions. Moreover, in tasks with repeated states, these reactive policies struggle to differentiate identical observations across different stages. DeCo, however, substantially boosts their performance, improving the success rates from 0.00% to 66.67% for RVT- 2+DeCo, from 0.00% to 21.53% for 3DDA+DeCo, from 0.14% to 58.06% for ARP+DeCo. These results underscore the power of DeCo's model-agnostic design and its capacity for zero-shot generalization, efficiently scheduling and composing learned atomic skills to perform well in new, long-horizon tasks. It is important to emphasize that the upper bound of DeCo's enhancement on model generalization is limited by: (1) the inherent capability of the base IL model, and (2) the task reasoning ability of the VLM. For example, although 3DDA+DeCo and ARP+DeCo perform well on training atomic tasks, they fail on certain compositional long-horizon tasks such as Sweep and Drop (sweep rubbish + drop rubbish) and Retrieve and Sweep (broom out of cupboard + sweep rubbish). While DeCo can accurately plan and schedule the corresponding atomic skills, both 3DDA and ARP face challenges in visual processing within these unseen compositional contexts.
表 I 展示了各模型在 DeCoBench 10 个原子任务上的表现。虽然 3DDA 取得了最高的总体成功率，但 ARP 和 RVT-2 也表现出较强的竞争力。然而，当这些模型集成 DeCo 后，部分任务的性能有所下降。这归因于 VLM 在原子任务规划中引入的不稳定性，对多任务 IL 模型遵循指令的准确性产生了负面影响。这种性能下降主要源于 VLM 的视觉语义对齐错误：1) 状态估计幻觉（例如错误地检测到遮挡）触发了不必要的先决技能（过度规划），从而干扰了执行流程；2) 指令分布偏移，即 VLM 生成的指令与训练数据存在差异，导致 IL 策略估计出次优的目标位姿。值得注意的是，基线模型依赖于训练期间遇到的真值指令，这使其在这些任务上表现更好。相比之下，表 II 突显了 DeCo 在新任务泛化方面的有效性。它展示了 RVT-2、3DDA 和 ARP 在 10 个原子任务上训练后，在 12 个长程任务上的表现，以及它们经 DeCo 增强后的对比结果。虽然基线模型在原子任务上表现出色（如表 I 所示），但在长程任务上却表现吃力，无法将原子技能泛化到更复杂的场景中。这种失败源于两个根本局限：无法推理未见的临时依赖关系，以及循环回路中的感知混淆。在没有显式任务分解的情况下，基线模型无法从高层指令中推断出必要的先决条件（例如，放置前先打开）。此外，在具有重复状态的任务中，这些反应式策略难以区分不同阶段中相同的观测结果。然而，DeCo 大幅提升了它们的性能，将 RVT-2+DeCo 的成功率从 0.00% 提升至 66.67%，3DDA+DeCo 从 0.00% 提升至 21.53%，ARP+DeCo 从 0.14% 提升至 58.06%。这些结果强调了 DeCo 模型无关设计的强大之处，及其在零样本泛化方面的能力，能够高效地调度和组合已学习的原子技能，从而在新的长程任务中表现良好。需要强调的是，DeCo 模型泛化能力的上限受限于：(1) 基础 IL 模型固有的能力，以及 (2) VLM 的任务推理能力。例如，尽管 3DDA+DeCo 和 ARP+DeCo 在训练原子任务上表现良好，但在某些组合式长程任务（如“清扫并丢弃”和“取回并清扫”）上却表现不佳。虽然 DeCo 可以准确规划和调度相应的原子技能，但 3DDA 和 ARP 在这些未见的组合语境下，视觉处理方面仍面临挑战。


To further assess the impact of atomic task design, we train RVT-2 on 6 long-horizon tasks (6 Long) from DeCoBench, comprising 4 original IL tasks and 2 cross-domain tasks. We then evaluate the model on the remaining 6 novel long-horizon tasks not seen during training (6 Novel), as well as on all 12 long-horizon tasks (12 All). As shown in Table III, compared to RVT-2 trained directly on the 6 long-horizon tasks, denoted as RVT-2 (6 Long training), the DeCo-based variant (RVT-2 + DeCo) achieves substantially better zero-shot generalization on the unseen 6 Novel tasks, improving the success rate by 53.89%. Even when evaluated across all 12 tasks, including those seen by RVT-2 (6 Long training), the DeCo-based model still yields an overall improvement of 14.31%. These results indicate that DeCo's atomic task training set more effectively supports skill acquisition in multi-task IL models and enhances generalization to novel compositional tasks.
为了进一步评估原子任务设计的影响，我们在 DeCoBench 的 6 个长程任务（6 Long）上训练了 RVT-2，其中包括 4 个原始 IL 任务和 2 个跨域任务。随后，我们在训练期间未见的其余 6 个新长程任务（6 Novel）以及全部 12 个长程任务（12 All）上对模型进行了评估。如表 III 所示，与直接在 6 个长程任务上训练的 RVT-2（RVT-2 (6 Long training)）相比，基于 DeCo 的变体（RVT-2 + DeCo）在未见的 6 个新任务上实现了显著更好的零样本泛化，成功率提高了 53.89%。即使在评估全部 12 个任务（包括 RVT-2 (6 Long training) 已见过的任务）时，基于 DeCo 的模型仍实现了 14.31% 的整体提升。这些结果表明，DeCo 的原子任务训练集能更有效地支持多任务 IL 模型的技能获取，并增强对新颖组合任务的泛化能力。

## C. Real-Robot Evaluations
## C. 真实机器人评估


Extensive experiments are conducted on a real-world robotic platform to validate the practical effectiveness of the DeCo framework, assessing its ability to generalize learned skills to novel instructions and scenarios. Table IV compares the zero-shot success rates of RVT-2 and RVT-2+DeCo, both trained on 6 atomic tasks, and evaluated on 9 novel long-horizon (N-L-H) tasks unseen during training. These N-L-H tasks require reasoning over task instructions and composing atomic skills to achieve desired outcomes. The results show that RVT-2 struggles with generalization, achieving a success rate of 0%. In contrast, RVT-2+DeCo achieves an average success rate of 53.33% on the N-L-H tasks, highlighting the practical effectiveness and generalization of DeCo in real-world settings.
我们在真实机器人平台上进行了大量实验，以验证 DeCo 框架的实际有效性，并评估其将习得技能泛化至新指令和新场景的能力。表 IV 比较了 RVT-2 和 RVT-2+DeCo 的零样本成功率，两者均在 6 个原子任务上进行训练，并在训练中未见的 9 个新型长程（N-L-H）任务上进行评估。这些 N-L-H 任务要求对任务指令进行推理，并组合原子技能以达成预期结果。结果表明，RVT-2 在泛化方面表现欠佳，成功率为 0%。相比之下，RVT-2+DeCo 在 N-L-H 任务上的平均成功率为 53.33%，凸显了 DeCo 在真实环境中的实际有效性与泛化能力。


To analyze system robustness, we introduce human intervention during real-world task execution and evaluate two perturbation scenarios. When the target object is displaced, the end-effector fails to reach the goal pose; since DeCo determines task completion via pose matching, the monitoring module detects the mismatch and halts execution, exposing the lack of a re-planning mechanism. In contrast, under object replacement or minor drawer perturbations, DeCo correctly identifies task completion and schedules subsequent skills as long as the goal pose is reached. These results show that DeCo is robust to perturbations preserving goal poses but cannot recover from significant object displacement. Related experiment videos are available on the project website.

## D. Ablation Studies
## D. 消融研究


Fig. 7 summarizes three ablation studies on DeCo's heuristic settings. Fig. 7(a) compares the generalization performance of three models using DeCo under half and full interaction settings. The results show that while DeCo enhances generalization in both cases, different models exhibit varying sensitivity to the granularity of physical interactions. Notably, full interaction sequences (open $\rightarrow$ closed $\rightarrow$ open) significantly improve DeCo's compositional generalization capability, with ARP+DeCo being most affected in the half interaction setting, while RVT-2+DeCo and 3DDA+DeCo show relatively stable performance. We believe that full interaction decomposition generates clear and consistent subtasks, providing stronger learning signals and enhancing generalization. In contrast, half interaction decomposition often results in overly fine-grained fragments, increasing task interference and hindering policy learning. While finer decomposition allows for greater flexibility, it also expands the task space and raises the risk of inconsistent subtask combinations. Additionally, full interaction decomposition aligns better with language instructions, as each decomposition usually corresponds to a complete command. This alignment is crucial for effectively bridging perception, action, and language in DeCo.
图 7 总结了关于 DeCo 启发式设置的三项消融研究。图 7(a) 比较了三种模型在半交互和全交互设置下使用 DeCo 的泛化性能。结果显示，尽管 DeCo 在两种情况下均增强了泛化能力，但不同模型对物理交互粒度的敏感度各异。值得注意的是，全交互序列（打开 $\rightarrow$ 关闭 $\rightarrow$ 打开）显著提升了 DeCo 的组合泛化能力，其中 ARP+DeCo 在半交互设置下受影响最大，而 RVT-2+DeCo 和 3DDA+DeCo 的表现相对稳定。我们认为，全交互分解生成了清晰且一致的子任务，提供了更强的学习信号并增强了泛化能力。相比之下，半交互分解往往导致过于细碎的片段，增加了任务干扰并阻碍了策略学习。虽然更细致的分解允许更大的灵活性，但也扩大了任务空间，并增加了子任务组合不一致的风险。此外，全交互分解与语言指令更契合，因为每次分解通常对应一个完整的指令。这种对齐对于在 DeCo 中有效连接感知、动作和语言至关重要。


The number of atomic task demonstrations for RVT-2+DeCo is varied, reporting the average success rates over 10 atomic tasks and 12 long-horizon tasks. As shown in Fig. 7(b), more demonstrations improve the performance of the IL model. Provided the RVT-2 model learns atomic skills to a sufficient degree, DeCo effectively composes these skills to achieve zero-shot generalization on long-horizon tasks. Moreover, improved atomic task performance directly correlates with enhanced generalization in DeCo. Fig. 7(c) shows that disabling the spatially-aware skill chaining module (Chaining Poses Num = 0) causes a significant performance drop. A failure case of the Put in and Close task shows a collision with the drawer due to poor transition planning, preventing successful grasping. In contrast, enabling the spatially-aware skill chaining module consistently improves task success, regardless of the number of poses. In DeCo, the chaining poses number is heuristically set to 6.
我们改变了 RVT-2+DeCo 的原子任务演示数量，并报告了 10 个原子任务和 12 个长程任务的平均成功率。如图 7(b) 所示，更多的演示提升了模仿学习（IL）模型的性能。只要 RVT-2 模型能充分学习原子技能，DeCo 就能有效地组合这些技能，从而在长程任务上实现零样本泛化。此外，原子任务性能的提升与 DeCo 泛化能力的增强直接相关。图 7(c) 显示，禁用空间感知技能链模块（链式位姿数量 = 0）会导致性能显著下降。在“放入并关闭”任务的一个失败案例中，由于过渡规划不佳，机械臂与抽屉发生碰撞，导致抓取失败。相反，启用空间感知技能链模块无论位姿数量如何，都能持续提高任务成功率。在 DeCo 中，链式位姿数量被启发式地设定为 6。

## VI. CONCLUSION AND DISCUSSION
## VI. 结论与讨论


This paper proposes DeCo, a model-agnostic framework enabling multi-task IL models to zero-shot generalize to novel compositional long-horizon 3D manipulation tasks. DeCo decomposes IL demonstrations into modular atomic tasks through physical interaction analysis, leverages VLMs for task planning, and uses a spatially-aware chaining module for collision-free transitions. Extensive evaluations in both simulation and real-world settings show that DeCo significantly improves the generalization of multi-task IL models.
本文提出了 DeCo，这是一个与模型无关的框架，使多任务 IL 模型能够零样本泛化至新型组合式长程 3D 操作任务。DeCo 通过物理交互分析将 IL 演示分解为模块化原子任务，利用 VLM 进行任务规划，并使用空间感知链式模块实现无碰撞过渡。在仿真和真实环境中的广泛评估表明，DeCo 显著提升了多任务 IL 模型的泛化能力。


Limitations arise from system dependencies. Our analysis identifies two failure modes: atomic task failures due to VLM visual-semantic grounding errors (e.g., over-planning), and long-horizon task failures from the base IL model's limited visual robustness in novel contexts. Additionally, the current decomposition is limited to claw-like end-effectors. Future work includes exploring tactile modalities for dexterous control and non-prehensile manipulation, as well as incorporating closed-loop re-planning and parameterized primitives to enhance robustness and scalability.
局限性源于系统依赖。我们的分析确定了两种失效模式：一是由于 VLM 视觉语义定位错误（如过度规划）导致的原子任务失效；二是由于基础 IL 模型在陌生场景下视觉鲁棒性不足导致的长期任务失效。此外，目前的分解方法仅限于爪式末端执行器。未来的工作包括探索用于灵巧控制和非抓取式操作的触觉模态，以及引入闭环重规划和参数化基元，以增强系统的鲁棒性和可扩展性。

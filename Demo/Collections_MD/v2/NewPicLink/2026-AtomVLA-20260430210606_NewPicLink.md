Abstract-Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models demonstrate remarkable potential for generalizable robotic manipulation. The execution of complex multi-step behaviors in VLA models can be improved by robust instruction grounding, a critical component for effective control. However, current paradigms predominantly rely on coarse, high-level task instructions during supervised fine-tuning. This instruction grounding gap leaves models without explicit intermediate guidance, leading to severe compounding errors in long-horizon tasks. Therefore, bridging this instruction gap and providing scalable post-training for VLA models is urgent. To tackle this problem, we propose AtomVLA, the first subtask-aware VLA framework integrated with a scalable offline post-training pipeline. Our framework leverages a large language model to decompose high-level demonstrations into fine-grained atomic subtasks. This approach utilizes a pretrained predictive world model to score candidate action chunks against subtask goals in the latent space, mitigating error accumulation while significantly improving long-horizon robustness. Furthermore, this approach enables highly efficient Group Relative Policy Optimization without the prohibitive expenses associated with online rollouts on physical robots. Extensive simulations validate that our AtomVLA maintains strong robustness under perturbations. When evaluated against fundamental baseline models, it achieves an average success rate of 97.0% on the LIBERO benchmark and 48.0% on the LIBERO-PRO benchmark. Finally, experiments conducted in the real world using the Galaxea R1 Lite platform confirm its broad applicability across diverse tasks, especially long-horizon tasks. All datasets, checkpoints, and code will be released to the public domain following the acceptance of this work for future research.
摘要——视觉-语言-动作（VLA）模型在通用机器人操作方面展现出巨大潜力。通过稳健的指令对齐（instruction grounding）——这是实现有效控制的关键要素——可以提升 VLA 模型执行复杂多步行为的能力。然而，当前的范式在监督微调阶段主要依赖粗略的高级任务指令。这种指令对齐的缺失导致模型缺乏明确的中间引导，从而在长程任务中产生严重的累积误差。因此，弥合这一指令鸿沟并为 VLA 模型提供可扩展的后训练方案迫在眉睫。为解决这一问题，我们提出了 AtomVLA，这是首个集成可扩展离线后训练流水线的子任务感知 VLA 框架。我们的框架利用大语言模型将高级演示分解为细粒度的原子子任务。该方法利用预训练的预测性世界模型在潜在空间中根据子任务目标对候选动作块进行评分，在减轻误差累积的同时显著提高了长程任务的鲁棒性。此外，该方法实现了高效的组相对策略优化（GRPO），无需承担物理机器人在线交互的高昂成本。大量仿真实验验证了 AtomVLA 在扰动下保持了强大的鲁棒性。在与基础基准模型的对比评估中，它在 LIBERO 基准测试上达到了 97.0% 的平均成功率，在 LIBERO-PRO 基准测试上达到了 48.0% 的平均成功率。最后，使用 Galaxea R1 Lite 平台进行的现实世界实验证实了其在多样化任务，尤其是长程任务中的广泛适用性。所有数据集、检查点和代码将在本工作被录用后向公众发布，以供后续研究使用。


## I. INTRODUCTION
## I. 引言


Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models have significantly advanced the development of embodied intelligence, offering a unified framework for end-to-end visuomotor control and complex instruction-following [1], [2], [3], [4]. By leveraging the reasoning capabilities of large-scale multi-modal models, they directly interpret natural language instructions into executable robot actions, demonstrating remarkable potential for generalizable robot manipulation. However, the transition from lab-scale demonstrations to robust long-horizon deployment is consistently constrained by several critical challenges. Specifically, current VLA architectures [5], [6] demand prohibitive computational resources and massive trajectory datasets for pre-training, making them difficult to scale in resource-constrained environments. Furthermore, the semantic-visual information asymmetry is widespread across these models, as high-dimensional visual tokens often dominate the latent space. As a result, the learned policy struggles to ground sparse linguistic instructions into sequential physical guidance, leading to compounding errors in multistep tasks. Finally, the high costs and safety risks of real-world interaction make online reinforcement learning (RL) impractical for most robotic platforms, leaving a gap between static imitation learning and dynamic policy refinement.
视觉-语言-动作（VLA）模型显著推动了具身智能的发展，为端到端视觉运动控制和复杂指令遵循提供了一个统一的框架 [1], [2], [3], [4]。通过利用大规模多模态模型的推理能力，它们能直接将自然语言指令解释为可执行的机器人动作，在通用机器人操作方面展现出巨大潜力。然而，从实验室规模的演示到稳健的长程部署，其过程始终受到几个关键挑战的制约。具体而言，当前的 VLA 架构 [5], [6] 在预训练阶段需要极高的计算资源和海量的轨迹数据集，使其难以在资源受限的环境中扩展。此外，语义与视觉信息的不对称性在这些模型中普遍存在，因为高维视觉标记往往主导了潜在空间。结果导致学习到的策略难以将稀疏的语言指令对齐到序列化的物理引导上，从而在多步任务中产生累积误差。最后，现实世界交互的高成本和安全风险使得在线强化学习（RL）对于大多数机器人平台而言并不切实际，这在静态模仿学习与动态策略优化之间留下了鸿沟。


To address these challenges, effort has been devoted to computational overhead optimization [7], [8], [9], semantic reasoning improvement [10], and the reduction of physical interaction costs through generative simulation [11], [12]. Specifically, LLaVA-VLA [7], SmolVLA [8] and TinyVLA [9] focuses on building lightweight models to significantly reduce parameter counts; CycleVLA [10] introduces cyclic mechanisms to enhance semantic comprehension; and RoboTwin [11], [12] generates extensive interaction data via simulation environments. Despite these significant advancements, existing methods often suffer from poor generalization in practical deployment due to their reliance on imitation learning. Furthermore, most current frameworks focus on reactive action generation, lacking the latent understanding and prediction of real-world dynamic evolution. This absence of modeling capability hinders the model from performing scalable offline policy optimization in the absence of costly online feedback.
为应对这些挑战，研究人员致力于优化计算开销 [7], [8], [9]、提升语义推理能力 [10]，并通过生成式仿真 [11], [12] 降低物理交互成本。具体而言，LLaVA-VLA [7]、SmolVLA [8] 和 TinyVLA [9] 专注于构建轻量化模型以显著减少参数量；CycleVLA [10] 引入循环机制以增强语义理解；而 RoboTwin [11], [12] 则通过仿真环境生成海量交互数据。尽管取得了这些重大进展，现有方法由于依赖模仿学习，在实际部署中往往泛化能力较差。此外，当前大多数框架侧重于反应式动作生成，缺乏对现实世界动态演变的潜在理解与预测。这种建模能力的缺失，阻碍了模型在缺乏昂贵在线反馈的情况下进行可扩展的离线策略优化。


In this paper, we propose AtomVLA, a novel, scalable, two-stage post-training framework for robust VLA models in long-horizon robotic tasks. Our contributions are threefold:
在本文中，我们提出了AtomVLA，这是一种新颖且可扩展的两阶段后训练框架，旨在为长程机器人任务提供稳健的VLA模型。我们的贡献主要体现在以下三个方面：


- AtomVLA first introduces a robust architecture that bridges the instruction-grounding gap by leveraging a large language model (LLM) to decompose complex, high-level demonstrations into fine-grained atomic subtasks. This decomposition provides explicit stage-wise guidance that aligns the policy's semantic understanding with its underlying action-chunking mechanism.
- AtomVLA首先引入了一种稳健的架构，通过利用大语言模型（LLM）将复杂的、高层级的演示分解为细粒度的原子子任务，从而弥合了指令对齐的鸿沟。这种分解提供了明确的阶段性指导，使策略的语义理解与其底层的动作分块机制保持一致。


- Furthermore, to eliminate reliance on costly real-world interactions and to bypass the hallucinations inherent in generative models, a predictive latent world model based on V-JEPA2 [13] is employed to rigorously evaluate candidate action trajectories against subtask goals directly in the latent space. Equipped with these latent transitions, the framework generates highly reliable reward signals for RL post-training, enabling efficient policy refinement without the computational overhead or visual artifacts of pixel-level synthesis.
- 此外，为消除对代价高昂的现实世界交互的依赖，并避免生成式模型固有的幻觉问题，采用基于 V-JEPA2 [13] 的预测性潜在世界模型，直接在潜在空间中根据子任务目标严格评估候选动作轨迹。借助这些潜在转换，该框架为强化学习后训练生成高度可靠的奖励信号，从而在无需像素级合成的计算开销或视觉伪影的情况下实现高效的策略优化。


- We demonstrate state-of-the-art performance, achieving a 97.0% success rate on the LIBERO benchmark alongside robust visual generalization with a 48.0% success rate on the LIBERO-PRO benchmark. Furthermore, we verify the physical reliability of the model on the Galaxea R1 Lite platform, specifically highlighting its success in complex long-horizon manipulation of deformable objects such as folding a T-shirt.
- 我们展示了最先进的性能，在LIBERO基准测试中达到了97.0%的成功率，并在LIBERO-PRO基准测试中展现了稳健的视觉泛化能力，成功率达48.0%。此外，我们在Galaxea R1 Lite平台上验证了该模型的物理可靠性，特别突出了其在折叠T恤等可变形物体复杂长程操作任务中的成功表现。


## II. RELATED WORK
## II. 相关工作


## A. Vision-Language-Action Models
## A. 视觉-语言-动作模型


To achieve better adaptability and efficacy, early works [14], [6] optimized Transformer architectures from the ground up, leveraging massive internet-sourced multimodal datasets alongside extensive robotic execution traces. Building upon this foundation, OpenVLA [3] released the first open source foundation model trained on large-scale public data. Subsequently, OpenHelix [15] proposed a dual system architecture for robotic manipulation. To improve deployment efficiency in practical environments, PD-VLA [16] and CEED-VLA [17] explored methods to accelerate the inference process. Furthermore, recent studies such as Spatial Forcing [18] and SpatialVLA [19] have investigated the incorporation of three dimensional spatial features. However, these approaches oriented toward three dimensional perception rely excessively on visual representations at the pure image level. Consequently, they exhibit significant limitations regarding the deep grounding of natural language instructions and the design of their overall training paradigms.
为了实现更好的适应性和功效，早期工作 [14]、[6] 从底层优化了 Transformer 架构，利用海量的互联网多模态数据集以及广泛的机器人执行轨迹。在此基础上，OpenVLA [3] 发布了首个在公开大规模数据上训练的开源基础模型。随后，OpenHelix [15] 提出了一种用于机器人操作的双系统架构。为了提高实际环境中的部署效率，PD-VLA [16] 和 CEED-VLA [17] 探索了加速推理过程的方法。此外，Spatial Forcing [18] 和 SpatialVLA [19] 等近期研究探讨了三维空间特征的融合。然而，这些面向三维感知的方案过度依赖纯图像层面的视觉表征。因此，它们在自然语言指令的深度对齐以及整体训练范式的设计方面表现出显著局限性。


## B. Reinforcement Learning for Post-Training
## B. 用于后训练的强化学习


Reinforcement learning post-training has emerged as a crucial paradigm to overcome the inherent limitations of supervised fine-tuning [20], [21], [22], [23], [24]. Because directly deploying online reinforcement learning on physical robots incurs prohibitive interaction costs [25], recent research increasingly favors adopting world models as virtual simulators. Numerous studies [26], [21], [27], [28] have explored this intersection by utilizing simulated environments to generate synthetic experiences for policy optimization. However, effectively fusing these two components remains a significant challenge. Standard generative world models typically synthesize future states at the pixel level. This mechanism inevitably leads to the accumulation of autoregressive errors and produces visual hallucinations during long sequence predictions [20]. To address these bottlenecks, we introduce a scalable post-training pipeline that combines a predictive latent world model (V-JEPA) with GRPO to score trajectories and refine policies. This yields a reliable reward signal and stable improvements on long-horizon tasks.
强化学习后训练已成为克服监督微调固有局限性的关键范式 [20], [21], [22], [23], [24]。由于在实体机器人上直接部署在线强化学习会产生高昂的交互成本 [25]，近期研究日益倾向于采用世界模型作为虚拟模拟器。大量研究 [26], [21], [27], [28] 通过利用模拟环境生成用于策略优化的合成经验，探索了这一交叉领域。然而，如何有效融合这两个组件仍是一项重大挑战。标准的生成式世界模型通常在像素层面合成未来状态。这种机制不可避免地导致自回归误差累积，并在长序列预测中产生视觉幻觉 [20]。为解决这些瓶颈，我们引入了一种可扩展的后训练流水线，将预测性潜在世界模型 (V-JEPA) 与 GRPO 相结合，用于轨迹评分和策略优化。这为长程任务提供了可靠的奖励信号和稳定的性能提升。

## A. Model Architecture
## A. 模型架构


VLM Backbone. AtomVLA uses the open-source multimodal Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct [29] as the VLM backbone and couples it with an action head. At time $t$ ,given a multiview visual observation ${O}_{t}$ ,high-level instruction ${I}_{t}$ ,and subtask instruction $S{I}_{t}$ ,the backbone encodes them into contextual token features
VLM 主干网络。AtomVLA 使用开源多模态模型 Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct [29] 作为 VLM 主干，并耦合一个动作头。在时间 $t$，给定多视角视觉观测 ${O}_{t}$、高层指令 ${I}_{t}$ 和子任务指令 $S{I}_{t}$，主干网络将其编码为上下文标记特征


$$
{\mathbf{H}}_{t} = {f}_{\mathrm{{VLM}}}\left( {{O}_{t},{I}_{t} + S{I}_{t}}\right) . \tag{1}
$$



which condition the action expert via cross-attention. The expert outputs an action chunk ${\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N} = \left\lbrack  {{\mathbf{a}}_{t},\ldots ,{\mathbf{a}}_{t + N}}\right\rbrack$ , where $N$ is the action horizon.
这些特征通过交叉注意力机制调节动作专家模型。专家模型输出一个动作块 ${\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N} = \left\lbrack  {{\mathbf{a}}_{t},\ldots ,{\mathbf{a}}_{t + N}}\right\rbrack$，其中 $N$ 为动作时域。


Action Head. We introduce an action head, implemented as a cross-attention Diffusion Transformer [30]. Specifically, the action expert serves as a flow-matching head that regresses an action sequence. Conditioned on ${\mathbf{H}}_{t}$ and ${S}_{t}$ ,the expert predicts a horizon- $N$ action chunk ${\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N}$ . Following a flow-matching formulation, we corrupt the ground-truth chunk with Gaussian noise: sample $\mathbf{\epsilon } \sim  \mathcal{N}\left( {0,\mathbf{I}}\right)$ and a continuous time $\tau  \in  \left( {0,1}\right)$ ,then form ${\mathbf{x}}_{\tau } = \left( {1 - \tau }\right) \mathbf{\epsilon } + \tau {\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N}$ . The expert is trained to regress the target velocity $\mathbf{v} = {\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N} - \mathbf{\epsilon }$ by predicting $\widehat{\mathbf{v}} = {\mathcal{A}}_{\phi }\left( {\mathbf{G}{\mathbf{x}}_{\tau },\tau ;{\mathbf{H}}_{t},{s}_{t}}\right)$ and minimizing
动作头。我们引入了一个动作头，实现为交叉注意力扩散 Transformer [30]。具体而言，动作专家充当流匹配头，对动作序列进行回归。在 ${\mathbf{H}}_{t}$ 和 ${S}_{t}$ 的条件下，专家预测一个时域为 $N$ 的动作块 ${\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N}$。遵循流匹配公式，我们用高斯噪声破坏真实动作块：采样 $\mathbf{\epsilon } \sim  \mathcal{N}\left( {0,\mathbf{I}}\right)$ 和连续时间 $\tau  \in  \left( {0,1}\right)$，然后构建 ${\mathbf{x}}_{\tau } = \left( {1 - \tau }\right) \mathbf{\epsilon } + \tau {\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N}$。专家通过预测 $\widehat{\mathbf{v}} = {\mathcal{A}}_{\phi }\left( {\mathbf{G}{\mathbf{x}}_{\tau },\tau ;{\mathbf{H}}_{t},{s}_{t}}\right)$ 并最小化损失来训练回归目标速度 $\mathbf{v} = {\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N} - \mathbf{\epsilon }$


$$
{\mathcal{L}}_{\mathrm{{FM}}} = {\mathbb{E}}_{\tau ,\mathbf{\epsilon }}{\begin{Vmatrix}{\mathcal{A}}_{\phi }\left( {\mathbf{x}}_{\tau },\tau ;{\mathbf{H}}_{t},{s}_{t}\right)  - \left( {\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N} - \mathbf{\epsilon }\right) \end{Vmatrix}}_{2}^{2}. \tag{2}
$$



At inference time,we initialize ${\mathbf{a}}^{\left( 0\right) } \sim  \mathcal{N}\left( {0,\mathbf{I}}\right)$ and iteratively refine it for $K$ steps using explicit Euler updates ${\mathbf{a}}^{\left( k + 1\right) } = {\mathbf{a}}^{\left( k\right) } + {\Delta t}{\mathcal{A}}_{\phi }\left( {{\mathbf{a}}^{\left( k\right) },{\tau }_{k};{\mathbf{H}}_{t},{s}_{t}}\right)$ ,with ${\Delta t} = 1/K$ , yielding the final action chunk.
在推理阶段，我们初始化 ${\mathbf{a}}^{\left( 0\right) } \sim  \mathcal{N}\left( {0,\mathbf{I}}\right)$ 并使用显式欧拉更新 ${\mathbf{a}}^{\left( k + 1\right) } = {\mathbf{a}}^{\left( k\right) } + {\Delta t}{\mathcal{A}}_{\phi }\left( {{\mathbf{a}}^{\left( k\right) },{\tau }_{k};{\mathbf{H}}_{t},{s}_{t}}\right)$ 对其进行 $K$ 步迭代细化，其中 ${\Delta t} = 1/K$，最终得到动作块。


Subtask Dataset. We use a large language model (LLM) to further decompose each demonstration trajectory and its high-level task instruction into fine-grained atomic subtasks, and to annotate each subtask with a natural language instruction and the start and end frames. Given the high-level task instruction, we provide the LLM with a sampled sequence of video frames and the task context, and ask it to segment the entire demonstration into a number of atomic subtasks. The LLM returns a JSON list of tuples in the format ${\left\{  \left( {\ell }_{i},{s}_{i},{e}_{i}\right) \right\}  }_{i = 1}^{M}$ ,where $i$ indexes subtasks, $M$ is the number of subtasks, ${\ell }_{i}$ is the subtask instruction,and ${s}_{i},{e}_{i}$ are the start and end frames. To ensure consistent granularity, we explicitly constrain the prompt so that the generated subtasks align with a small set of basic manipulation actions, rather than overly fine-grained intermediate process descriptions. For example, for the pick and place task, we standardize subtasks into action expressions such as Pick up [object], Place [object] on [target position], Open/Close [object], and Push [object]. Under this rule, a task is decomposed into 2-5 subtasks. Prompt details are provided in the supplementary material. In our implementation, we use GPT-40 as the LLM for fine-grained atomic subtask decomposition and annotation.

## B. Reward Modeling and RL Post-training
## B. 奖励建模与强化学习后训练


Motivation. While SFT on demonstrations equips the policy with a strong imitation capability, it often falls short on more challenging tasks and generalizes poorly. A common remedy is to further apply reinforcement learning post-training to improve long-horizon decision making and error recovery; however, collecting online RL rollouts in simulators or on real robots is costly and difficult to scale. We therefore seek a scalable offline post-training signal that encourages goal-directed progress at an appropriate temporal granularity and remains stable by anchoring learning to expert demonstrations. To this end, we treat subtask boundary frames as intermediate goals and leverage a pretrained world model to score candidate action chunks, enabling scalable RL post-training.
动机。虽然基于演示的监督微调（SFT）赋予了策略强大的模仿能力，但在更具挑战性的任务上往往表现不足，且泛化能力较差。一种常见的补救措施是应用强化学习后训练来提升长程决策和错误恢复能力；然而，在模拟器或真实机器人上收集在线强化学习轨迹成本高昂且难以扩展。因此，我们寻求一种可扩展的离线后训练信号，它能在适当的时间粒度上鼓励目标导向的进展，并通过锚定专家演示来保持学习的稳定性。为此，我们将子任务边界帧视为中间目标，并利用预训练的世界模型对候选动作块进行评分，从而实现可扩展的强化学习后训练。

This enables scoring candidate actions by comparing their predicted consequences against goal states in latent space.

## C. Training Pipeline
## C. 训练流程


As shown in Fig. 3, there are two major training stages: SFT Training. We perform supervised fine-tuning on robot demonstrations by jointly optimizing the Qwen3-VL backbone and the action expert. Given $\left( {{O}_{t},{I}_{t},{S}_{t}}\right)$ ,we supervise the predicted action chunk ${\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N}$ using the flow-matching loss in Eq. (2). We further reduce gradient variance by repeating each batch with independently sampled $\left( {\tau ,\mathbf{\epsilon }}\right)$ via the repeated diffusion steps and training with mixed precision.
如图 3 所示，训练分为两个主要阶段：SFT 训练。我们通过联合优化 Qwen3-VL 主干网络和动作专家，对机器人演示进行监督微调。给定 $\left( {{O}_{t},{I}_{t},{S}_{t}}\right)$，我们使用公式 (2) 中的流匹配损失来监督预测的动作块 ${\mathbf{a}}_{t : t + N}$。我们通过重复扩散步骤并采用混合精度训练，利用独立采样的 $\left( {\tau ,\mathbf{\epsilon }}\right)$ 重复每个批次，从而进一步降低梯度方差。


Post Training. Starting from the SFT checkpoint, we conduct offline GRPO post-training using the reward model defined in Sec. III-B. Concretely, we first sample states from the same offline demonstration dataset used in Stage I. Initialized from the Stage I checkpoint, the current policy then generates multiple candidate action chunks for each state. Next, we compute subgoal and final goal rewards with the V-JEPA2 [13] world model, and perform GRPO updates with a KL constraint to the frozen SFT reference policy. Unless otherwise specified, we mainly update the action expert parameters during post-training to preserve stable vision-language representations while improving long-horizon robustness.

Key Finding 1: Cross-embodiment large-scale pretraining is not necessary to achieve strong downstream performance. As shown in Table III, even without cross-embodiment large-scale pre-training, AtomVLA achieves an average success rate of 93% on the LIBERO benchmark [31]. Key Finding 6: AtomVLA demonstrates superior robustness and generalization in real-world robotic tasks. While AtomVLA and the baseline ${\pi }_{0}$ [6] exhibit comparable foundational capabilities under the ST setting, the critical advantage of our approach emerges under the GE setting. When subjected to spatial, visual, and instruction variations, the baseline performance degrades sharply to an average success rate of 29.2%. For instance, under the GE setting for the Fold T-shirt and Fold towel tasks, AtomVLA sustains success rates of ${25}\%$ and ${35}\%$ respectively,far exceeding the baseline. ## VI. CONCLUSION
## VI. 结论


In this paper, we propose AtomVLA to effectively address the instruction grounding gap and the inefficiency of offline reinforcement learning in horizon tasks. By introducing subtask decomposition driven by LLM, our training framework explicitly provides sequential guidance for complex robotic operations. Our experiments and analyses substantiate that our training paradigm, consisting of two stages, yields significant performance gains. Specifically, combining supervised fine-tuning with scalable offline GRPO guided by a predictive world model achieves remarkable results on both the LIBERO and LIBERO-PRO benchmarks. Furthermore, evaluations in the real world on the Galaxea R1 Lite platform highlight the substantial advantages of AtomVLA for tasks requiring prolonged execution sequences. This reliability is particularly evident in the challenging manipulation of deformable objects, such as folding a T-shirt. While our framework enables long-horizon tasks, adapting to highly dynamic environments remains challenging because the current system relies on static subtask boundaries generated by LLM. Future work will explore end-to-end subtask generation during execution and world models that can perceive uncertainty. Furthermore, we intend to systematically enhance the generalization capabilities of our approach to handle entirely unseen objects and novel semantic instructions.
本文提出了 AtomVLA，旨在有效解决长程任务中指令对齐的缺失以及离线强化学习效率低下的问题。通过引入由大语言模型（LLM）驱动的子任务分解，我们的训练框架为复杂的机器人操作提供了明确的序列引导。实验与分析证实，我们包含两个阶段的训练范式带来了显著的性能提升。具体而言，将监督微调与由预测性世界模型引导的可扩展离线 GRPO 相结合，在 LIBERO 和 LIBERO-PRO 基准测试中均取得了卓越成果。此外，在 Galaxea R1 Lite 平台上的真实世界评估凸显了 AtomVLA 在需要长序列执行任务中的巨大优势。这种可靠性在处理可变形物体（如折叠 T 恤）等具有挑战性的操作时表现尤为突出。尽管我们的框架支持长程任务，但由于当前系统依赖于 LLM 生成的静态子任务边界，适应高度动态的环境仍具挑战。未来的工作将探索执行过程中的端到端子任务生成以及能够感知不确定性的世界模型。此外，我们计划系统性地增强方法的泛化能力，以处理完全未见的物体和新颖的语义指令。

## D. Baseline.
## D. 基线模型。


The following baselines are utilized for comparative evaluation.
以下基线模型用于对比评估。


OpenVLA: A 7B-parameter VLA model that integrates a Llama 2 language backbone with a hybrid visual encoder fusing pretrained representations from DINOv2 and SigLIP. The model is pretrained on a large-scale collection of 970k real-world robot trajectories sourced from the Open X-Embodiment dataset.
OpenVLA：一款 7B 参数的 VLA 模型，集成了 Llama 2 语言主干与融合了 DINOv2 和 SigLIP 预训练表征的混合视觉编码器。该模型在源自 Open X-Embodiment 数据集的 97 万条大规模真实世界机器人轨迹上进行了预训练。


TraceVLA: A spatial-temporal enhanced VLA model that incorporates visual trace prompting to encode state-trajectories into the visual input. Built upon OpenVLA and fine-tuned on 150K robot manipulation trajectories.
TraceVLA：一款时空增强型 VLA 模型，引入视觉轨迹提示（visual trace prompting）将状态轨迹编码进视觉输入。它基于 OpenVLA 构建，并在 15 万条机器人操作轨迹上进行了微调。


Octo-base: A transformer-based generalist manipulation policy trained on approximately 800K trajectories from the Open X-Embodiment dataset. It supports language and goal-image conditioning and is designed to accommodate diverse sensor modalities and action spaces across multiple robotic platforms.
Octo-base：一款基于 Transformer 的通用操作策略模型，在 Open X-Embodiment 数据集的约 80 万条轨迹上训练而成。它支持语言和目标图像条件输入，旨在适配多种机器人平台下不同的传感器模态和动作空间。


SpatialVLA: A spatially-aware VLA model designed to enhance 3D understanding in robot manipulation. Pretrained on 1.1M real-world robot episodes, it introduces Ego3D Position Encoding to inject explicit 3D spatial information into visual observations and employs Adaptive Action Grids to discretize and represent spatial robot movements for cross-robot transfer. CoT-VLA: A 7B VLA model that incorporates explicit visual chain-of-thought reasoning for manipulation. It autoregressively predicts future visual frames as intermediate goals and then generates short action sequences to reach them, enabling temporal planning.
SpatialVLA：一款空间感知型 VLA 模型，旨在增强机器人操作中的 3D 理解能力。它在 110 万条真实世界机器人片段上预训练，引入 Ego3D 位置编码将显式 3D 空间信息注入视觉观测，并采用自适应动作网格（Adaptive Action Grids）对空间机器人运动进行离散化表示，以实现跨机器人迁移。CoT-VLA：一款 7B VLA 模型，结合了显式视觉思维链推理以辅助操作。它通过自回归方式预测未来视觉帧作为中间目标，随后生成短动作序列以达成目标，从而实现时间规划。


${\pi }_{0}$ : A VLA model that builds a flow-matching action architecture on top of a pretrained VLM to inherit large-scale semantic knowledge. It is trained on diverse datasets collected from multiple dexterous robotic platforms. The model supports direct language prompting and fine-tuning for new skills, demonstrating broad task coverage and cross-platform generalization.
${\pi }_{0}$：一款在预训练 VLM 基础上构建流匹配（flow-matching）动作架构的 VLA 模型，旨在继承大规模语义知识。它在从多个灵巧机器人平台收集的多样化数据集上进行训练。该模型支持直接语言提示及针对新技能的微调，展现出广泛的任务覆盖范围和跨平台泛化能力。


ThinkAct: A dual-system VLA framework that separates high-level reasoning from low-level control. It trains a multimodal LLM to generate embodied reasoning plans, which are reinforced using action-aligned visual rewards and compressed into a visual latent plan. This latent representation conditions a downstream action policy, enabling improved long-horizon planning, few-shot adaptation, and robust execution in complex embodied manipulation tasks.
ThinkAct：一款将高层推理与底层控制分离的双系统 VLA 框架。它训练多模态大语言模型生成具身推理计划，利用动作对齐的视觉奖励进行强化，并将其压缩为视觉潜在计划。该潜在表征作为下游动作策略的条件，实现了更优的长程规划、少样本适应以及复杂具身操作任务中的稳健执行。


NORA: A 3B-parameter VLA model designed for efficient real-time robotic manipulation. Built on the Qwen-2.5-VL- 3B backbone, it leverages strong visual-semantic understanding to improve action grounding while significantly reducing computational overhead compared to larger VLA models. It trained on 970K real-world robot demonstrations and equipped with the FAST+ tokenizer for efficient action sequence generation.
NORA：一款专为高效实时机器人操作设计的 3B 参数 VLA 模型。它基于 Qwen-2.5-VL-3B 主干，利用强大的视觉语义理解能力提升动作定位精度，同时相比大型 VLA 模型显著降低了计算开销。它在 97 万条真实世界机器人演示数据上训练，并配备 FAST+ 分词器以实现高效的动作序列生成。


NORA-1.5: An enhanced VLA model built upon the pre-trained NORA backbone, incorporating a flow-matching-based action expert to improve reliability and task performance. It uses reward-driven post-training with action-conditioned world models and deviation-from-ground-truth heuristics, enabling direct preference optimization for adaptation to new embodiments.
NORA-1.5：一款在预训练 NORA 主干基础上增强的 VLA 模型，引入了基于流匹配的动作专家以提升可靠性和任务性能。它使用基于奖励的后训练方法，结合动作条件世界模型和偏离真值启发式算法，实现了针对新具身形态适应的直接偏好优化。


MolmoAct: An Action Reasoning Model that integrates depth-aware perception, mid-level spatial planning, and low-level action prediction. The 7B-D variant achieves strong zero-shot, fine-tuned, and out-of-distribution performance across simulation and real-world tasks, surpassing prior VLAs.
MolmoAct：一款集成了深度感知、中层空间规划和底层动作预测的动作推理模型。其 7B-D 变体在仿真和真实世界任务中均实现了卓越的零样本、微调及分布外性能，超越了先前的 VLA 模型。


X-VLA: A flow-matching-based VLA model that uses soft-prompt embeddings to capture cross-embodiment variations in heterogeneous robotic datasets. Its 0.9B variant, X-VLA-0.9B, achieves state-of-the-art performance across simulations and real-world robots, enabling flexible dexterity, fast adaptation, and effective exploitation of diverse robotic platforms with minimal additional parameters.
X-VLA：一款基于流匹配的 VLA 模型，利用软提示嵌入（soft-prompt embeddings）捕捉异构机器人数据集中跨具身形态的差异。其 0.9B 变体 X-VLA-0.9B 在仿真和真实世界机器人任务中均达到了最先进水平，实现了灵活的灵巧操作、快速适应，并能以极少的额外参数有效利用多样化的机器人平台。

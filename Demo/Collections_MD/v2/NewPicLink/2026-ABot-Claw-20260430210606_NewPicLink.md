## Abstract
## 摘要


Current embodied intelligent systems still face a substantial gap between high-level reasoning and low-level physical execution in open-world environments. Although Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models provide strong perception and intuitive responses, their open-loop nature limits long-horizon performance. Agents incorporating System 2 cognitive mechanisms improve planning, but usually operate in closed sandboxes with predefined toolkits and limited real-system control. OpenClaw provides a localized runtime with full system privileges, but lacks the embodied control architecture required for long-duration, multi-robot execution. We therefore propose ABot-Claw, an embodied extension of OpenClaw that integrates: 1) a unified embodiment interface with capability-driven scheduling for heterogeneous robot coordination; 2) a visual-centric cross-embodiment multimodal memory for persistent context retention and grounded retrieval; and 3) a critic-based closed-loop feedback mechanism with a generalist reward model for online progress evaluation, local correction, and replanning. With a decoupled architecture spanning the OpenClaw layer, shared service layer, and robot embodiment layer, ABot-Claw enables real-world interaction, closes the loop from natural language intent to physical action, and supports progressively self-evolving robotic agents in open, dynamic environments.
当前的具身智能系统在开放世界环境中的高层推理与底层物理执行之间仍存在巨大鸿沟。尽管视觉-语言-动作（VLA）模型提供了强大的感知与直觉响应能力，但其开环特性限制了长程任务的表现。引入系统2认知机制的智能体虽提升了规划能力，却通常局限于预设工具包的封闭沙盒中，且缺乏对真实系统的控制力。OpenClaw虽提供了具备完整系统权限的本地运行时，却缺乏长周期、多机器人执行所需的具身控制架构。因此，我们提出了ABot-Claw，作为OpenClaw的具身扩展，它集成了：1）具备能力驱动调度功能的统一具身接口，用于异构机器人协作；2）以视觉为中心的跨具身多模态记忆，用于持久化上下文保留与落地检索；以及3）基于评论家的闭环反馈机制，利用通用奖励模型进行在线进度评估、局部修正与重规划。通过涵盖OpenClaw层、共享服务层与机器人具身层的解耦架构，ABot-Claw实现了真实世界的交互，打通了从自然语言意图到物理动作的闭环，并支持在动态开放环境中实现渐进式的机器人自进化。

## 1 Introduction
## 1 引言


Recent robotic intelligent systems increasingly adopt a dual-system architecture inspired by cognitive science, often referred to as the System 1-System 2 paradigm [16]. In this context, System 1 corresponds to fast, reactive modules that rely on learned priors to produce immediate responses. In contrast, System 2 represents a slower, deliberative process that supports structured reasoning, long-term planning, memory maintenance, and decision consistency across extended horizons. Current robotic intelligent systems commonly adopt architectures such as Vision-Language-Action (VLA) [2, 15, 22], Vision-Language Navigation (VLN) [3, 5, 25], or World Action Model (WAM) [17, 23], to enable fast perception and intuitive, reactive responses. These approaches demonstrate superior generalization in local tasks compared to traditional rule-based methods $\left\lbrack  {8,{14},{27}}\right\rbrack$ . However,such systems are fundamentally open-loop reactive agents,lacking explicit modeling of task states, environmental topology, and long-term memory. As a result, they struggle to support complex, long-horizon tasks that require continuous monitoring, dynamic adaptation, and coherent context maintenance. Simply enhancing the perceptual capabilities of System 1 cannot resolve the core limitations of missing memory, fragmented planning, and inconsistent execution [13].
受认知科学启发，近期的机器人智能系统日益采用“系统1-系统2”范式（System 1-System 2 paradigm）的双系统架构 [16]。其中，系统1对应依赖学习先验、能产生即时响应的快速反应模块；系统2则代表支持结构化推理、长期规划、记忆维护及跨长时程决策一致性的慢速审慎过程。当前机器人智能系统普遍采用视觉-语言-动作（VLA）[2, 15, 22]、视觉-语言导航（VLN）[3, 5, 25] 或世界动作模型（WAM）[17, 23] 等架构，以实现快速感知与直观的反应式响应。与传统基于规则的方法相比，这些方法在局部任务中展现出更优的泛化能力 $\left\lbrack  {8,{14},{27}}\right\rbrack$ 。然而，此类系统本质上是开环反应式智能体，缺乏对任务状态、环境拓扑及长期记忆的显式建模。因此，它们难以支持需要持续监控、动态适应及连贯上下文维护的复杂长时程任务。仅提升系统1的感知能力，无法解决记忆缺失、规划碎片化及执行不一致等核心局限 [13]。


To overcome this bottleneck, researchers have proposed agent frameworks with advanced cognitive functions to implement System 2 capabilities $\left\lbrack  {9,{18},{24}}\right\rbrack$ . These agents perform goal-driven reasoning through task decomposition, memory management, and self-reflection, while coordinating System 1 modules to execute concrete actions. This hierarchical integration of reasoning and reaction significantly improves behavioral organization and represents a critical step toward autonomous execution of complex tasks.
为克服这一瓶颈，研究人员提出了具备高级认知功能的智能体框架，以实现系统2的能力 $\left\lbrack  {9,{18},{24}}\right\rbrack$ 。这些智能体通过任务分解、记忆管理及自我反思进行目标驱动的推理，同时协调系统1模块执行具体动作。这种推理与反应的层级整合显著提升了行为组织能力，是迈向复杂任务自主执行的关键一步。


Nevertheless, most existing agents operate within closed sandboxes, relying on pre-registered toolkits and restricted to static pools of predefined functions [19]. When faced with novel actions such as greeting a guest through gesture or adapting to unexpected environmental changes, these systems often fail to generate solutions autonomously and must either abort or request human intervention [12]. At a more fundamental level, such agents lack direct control over the operating system. They are unable to read or write files, invoke local applications, or maintain persistent processes, which results in a disconnection from the physical environment [21]. Without the ability to interact meaningfully with real-world systems, they remain disembodied "brains in the air," unable to achieve true grounding in open-world environments [6].
然而，大多数现有智能体运行在封闭沙箱内，依赖预注册的工具包，且局限于预定义函数的静态池中 [19]。当面对诸如通过手势问候客人或适应意外环境变化等新颖动作时，这些系统往往无法自主生成解决方案，只能选择终止任务或请求人工干预 [12]。从更本质的层面看，此类智能体缺乏对操作系统的直接控制权。它们无法读写文件、调用本地应用或维持持久进程，导致其与物理环境脱节 [21]。由于无法与现实世界系统进行有效交互，它们仍是“悬浮的大脑”，无法在开放世界环境中实现真正的具身落地 [6]。

OpenClaw offers a key solution: as a self-hosted, locally running agent runtime, it possesses full system privileges, enabling shell command execution, GUI application control, event message listening, and unified integration across communication platforms such as WhatsApp, Telegram, and iMessage. With OpenClaw, AI agents can finally "take action" in the real world, achieving round-the-clock cross-platform autonomous operation. Crucially, OpenClaw supports natural language-driven dynamic skill evolution, allowing agents to generate code, validate logic, and deploy new functionalities autonomously, forming a closed-loop mechanism from intent understanding to capability realization. It is more than a communication channel; it is an infrastructure that endows AI with embodied presence.
OpenClaw提供了一个关键解决方案：作为一个自托管、本地运行的智能体运行时，它拥有完整的系统权限，支持Shell命令执行、GUI应用控制、事件消息监听，以及跨WhatsApp、Telegram和iMessage等通信平台的统一集成。有了OpenClaw，AI智能体终于能在现实世界中“采取行动”，实现全天候的跨平台自主运行。至关重要的是，OpenClaw支持由自然语言驱动的动态技能演进，允许智能体自主生成代码、验证逻辑并部署新功能，从而形成从意图理解到能力实现的闭环机制。它不仅是一个通信渠道，更是一种赋予AI具身存在感的基础设施。


While OpenClaw provides a powerful execution foundation that greatly enhances the flexibility and scalability of the perception-decision-action loop, it still lacks a corresponding high-level control architecture. As a result, it cannot natively support complex, long-duration, multi-device collaborative embodied tasks. The original framework does not clearly delineate the roles between System 1 and System 2, making sustained decomposition, monitoring, and correction of high-level intentions difficult. Its reliance on textual logs fragments visual, linguistic, and state information into multimodal silos, limiting contextual coherence [11]. Furthermore, the system lacks mechanisms for long-term state tracking and closed-loop feedback, making it prone to cascading failures under environmental perturbations. Additionally, integrating heterogeneous devices requires tight-coupled configurations, constraining dynamic collaboration and parallel execution.
尽管OpenClaw提供了一个强大的执行基础，极大地增强了感知-决策-动作循环的灵活性与可扩展性，但它仍缺乏相应的高级控制架构。因此，它无法原生支持复杂的、长时程的、多设备协作的具身任务。原始框架未明确界定系统1与系统2的角色，使得对高级意图的持续分解、监控与修正变得困难。其对文本日志的依赖将视觉、语言及状态信息碎片化为多模态孤岛，限制了上下文的连贯性 [11]。此外，该系统缺乏长期状态跟踪与闭环反馈机制，在环境扰动下极易引发级联故障。同时，集成异构设备需要紧耦合配置，限制了动态协作与并行执行的能力。


In response, We propose ABot-Claw, built upon OpenClaw as the foundational runtime engine, to integrate three core technical innovations. First, it introduces a unified embodiment interface and dynamic multi-agent scheduling mechanism that connect heterogeneous robots through a shared skill layer, enabling capability-aware routing, parallel execution, and cross-embodiment collaboration. Second, it incorporates a visual-centric multimodal memory that stores object observations, place anchors, keyframes, and semantic visual representations in a shared memory space, allowing the runtime to retrieve persistent world context across time, space, and robot embodiments. Third, it equips execution with a critic-based closed-loop feedback module, instantiated with a generalist reward model [20, 26], to evaluate task progress online and support completion detection, local correction, or replanning under execution uncertainty. By tightly coupling high-level cognitive decision-making with low-level physical execution, ABot-Claw establishes an embodied intelligence framework capable of continuous learning, adaptation, and evolution in complex and dynamic environments, marking a significant step toward truly autonomous, general-purpose robotic systems.
为此，我们提出了 ABot-Claw，它以 OpenClaw 为基础运行时引擎，集成了三项核心技术创新。首先，它引入了统一的具身接口和动态多智能体调度机制，通过共享技能层连接异构机器人，实现能力感知路由、并行执行及跨具身协作。其次，它整合了以视觉为中心的多模态记忆，将物体观测、地点锚点、关键帧及语义视觉表征存储在共享内存空间中，使运行时能够跨时间、空间和机器人具身检索持久的世界上下文。第三，它为执行过程配备了基于评论家的闭环反馈模块，通过通用奖励模型 [20, 26] 实例化，以在线评估任务进度，并在执行不确定性下支持完成检测、局部修正或重新规划。通过将高层认知决策与底层物理执行紧密耦合，ABot-Claw 建立了一个能够在复杂动态环境中持续学习、适应和进化的具身智能框架，标志着向真正自主、通用的机器人系统迈出了重要一步。

### 2.1 Overview
### 2.1 概述


Our goal in ABot-Claw is not to build an standalone embodied agent framework from scratch. Instead, we extend the OpenClaw runtime, originally designed for high-level software operation and task orchestration, into a general-purpose embodied runtime for real-world environments. This shift raises three practical challenges. First, the runtime must organize and control heterogeneous robot embodiments with very different actuation and control interfaces. Second, it must maintain persistent spatiotemporal context so that the agent can localize, recall, and act upon observations collected over long horizons. Third, it must remain robust under execution uncertainty, where open-loop plans often fail due to perception noise, environment changes, or control drift.
我们在 ABot-Claw 中的目标并非从零构建一个独立的具身智能体框架。相反，我们将最初为高层软件操作和任务编排而设计的 OpenClaw 运行时，扩展为适用于现实环境的通用具身运行时。这一转变带来了三个实际挑战。首先，运行时必须组织和控制具有截然不同驱动和控制接口的异构机器人具身。其次，它必须维护持久的时空上下文，以便智能体能够定位、回溯并基于长期观测采取行动。第三，它必须在执行不确定性下保持鲁棒性，因为开环计划往往会因感知噪声、环境变化或控制漂移而失败。


To address these challenges, we build ABot-Claw around three closely related components, as shown in Figure 2. We first extend OpenClaw with a unified embodiment interface that connects multiple robot types and exposes their capabilities through a shared skill layer. We then introduce a visual-centric multimodal memory that stores place anchors, object observations, semantic images in a shared memory space, so that the agent can reason over grounded environmental context rather than only the current observation. Finally, we incorporate a critic-style feedback module based on a generalist reward model, which provides an explicit signal of task progress during execution and supports termination, local correction, or replanning when needed. Together, these components turn ABot-Claw into an embodied runtime that can coordinate multiple robots, retrieve persistent world context, and react more robustly to real-world uncertainty. In the rest of this section, we describe these three components in turn and then summarize how they interact during end-to-end execution.
为应对这些挑战，我们围绕三个紧密相关的组件构建了 ABot-Claw，如图 2 所示。我们首先通过统一的具身接口扩展了 OpenClaw，该接口连接多种机器人类型并通过共享技能层暴露其能力。随后，我们引入了以视觉为中心的各种模态记忆，将地点锚点、物体观测和语义图像存储在共享内存空间中，使智能体能够基于已落地的环境上下文进行推理，而非仅依赖当前观测。最后，我们结合了一个基于通用奖励模型的评论家式反馈模块，它在执行过程中提供明确的任务进度信号，并在需要时支持终止、局部修正或重新规划。这些组件共同将 ABot-Claw 转变为一个能够协调多个机器人、检索持久世界上下文并对现实世界不确定性做出更稳健反应的具身运行时。在本节的其余部分，我们将依次介绍这三个组件，并总结它们在端到端执行过程中的交互方式。

### 2.2 Heterogeneous Embodiment Integration
### 2.2 异构具身集成


We begin by extending OpenClaw beyond a single physical platform. Many real-world tasks naturally span multiple workspaces and require complementary capabilities such as mobility, manipulation, and embodied perception. This motivates our one-runtime, multiple-bodies design, in which a single decision-making runtime coordinates a distributed pool of heterogeneous robot embodiments.
我们首先将 OpenClaw 的应用范围从单一物理平台扩展开来。许多现实任务自然跨越多个工作空间，并需要移动性、操作性和具身感知等互补能力。这促成了我们“一个运行时，多个身体”的设计，即由单一决策运行时协调分布式异构机器人具身池。


Under this design, ABot-Claw is not tied to one robot in one location. It instead acts as a centralized embodied agent that can perceive and intervene across multiple regions at the same time. In practice, this expands the set of tasks the system can solve and increases its capacity for parallel execution.
在这种设计下，ABot-Claw 不再绑定于特定地点的特定机器人。相反，它充当一个中心化的具身智能体，能够同时感知并干预多个区域。在实践中，这扩展了系统可解决的任务集，并提高了其并行执行的能力。


Unified embodiment interface. A central challenge in heterogeneous embodiment integration is that different robots expose fundamentally different low-level interfaces. A manipulator may rely on Cartesian planning from a fixed base, a mobile platform may expose navigation primitives, and a humanoid may require whole-body motion control. If these differences are passed directly to the reasoning layer, high-level planning becomes tightly coupled to hardware details and quickly becomes difficult to maintain.
统一具身接口。异构具身集成的一个核心挑战在于，不同机器人暴露的底层接口存在根本差异。机械臂可能依赖固定基座的笛卡尔规划，移动平台可能暴露导航原语，而人形机器人可能需要全身运动控制。如果这些差异直接传递给推理层，高层规划将与硬件细节紧密耦合，并迅速变得难以维护。


To avoid this, we introduce a unified embodiment interface between the OpenClaw runtime and the underlying robots. Concretely, we provide ROS-based adapters for different robot types, and map their native functions into a shared set of callable skills. These skills capture intent-level actions such as navigation, observation, inspection, and manipulation primitives, while leaving the embodiment-specific execution to the corresponding local controller or model service. This design allows the high-level agent to issue actions through a common interface without reasoning directly about robot-specific command formats.
为避免这种情况，我们在 OpenClaw 运行时与底层机器人之间引入了统一的具身接口。具体而言，我们为不同类型的机器人提供了基于 ROS 的适配器，并将它们的原生功能映射为一组共享的可调用技能。这些技能捕获了导航、观测、检查和操作原语等意图级动作，同时将具身特定的执行留给相应的本地控制器或模型服务。这种设计允许高层智能体通过通用接口发布动作，而无需直接推理机器人特定的指令格式。


Centralized runtime with multi-body execution. At runtime, ABot-Claw maintains a dynamic pool of connected physical devices and acts as a coordination center. It tracks the availability, task progress, and basic status of each robot, and uses this shared state to organize execution across embodiments.
集中式运行时与多体执行。在运行时，ABot-Claw 维护一个动态连接的物理设备池，并充当协调中心。它跟踪每个机器人的可用性、任务进度及基本状态，并利用这一共享状态来组织跨载体的执行。


A practical benefit of this design is that a user request can be decomposed and dispatched across multiple robots when the subtasks are compatible with parallel execution. For example, when asked to prepare a reception environment, the runtime may send a mobile robot to inspect the corridor, assign another embodiment to approach the entrance area, and command a tabletop arm to arrange objects on a desk. Each robot executes the assigned subtask through its own local controller or service stack, while reporting execution status back to the shared runtime.
该设计的一个实际优势在于，当子任务支持并行执行时，用户请求可被分解并分发给多个机器人。例如，在准备接待环境时，运行时可能会派遣一台移动机器人检查走廊，指派另一个载体接近入口区域，并命令桌面机械臂整理桌面物品。每个机器人通过其本地控制器或服务栈执行分配的子任务，同时将执行状态反馈回共享运行时。


We note that ABot-Claw does not replace embodiment-side control. Low-level motion control, safety handling, and hardware-specific exception management remain on the robot side. Our contribution here is a runtime-level abstraction that allows OpenClaw to invoke and coordinate heterogeneous embodiments through a common task interface.
我们指出，ABot-Claw 并不取代载体侧的控制。底层运动控制、安全处理及硬件特定的异常管理仍保留在机器人侧。我们在此的贡献在于提供了一种运行时级别的抽象，使 OpenClaw 能够通过通用的任务接口调用并协调异构载体。


Task routing and cross-embodiment collaboration. The value of multi-body embodiment is not limited to parallel execution of independent subtasks. In many practical scenarios, effective task completion requires handoff and cooperation across embodiments. In ABot-Claw, all robots operate as cooperative nodes that share both the same global task objective and the same environment memory, which allows the runtime to perform intent-aware routing and dynamic reassignment when a subtask exceeds the capability of a single robot or when cooperation yields better efficiency.
任务路由与跨载体协作。多体载体的价值不仅限于独立子任务的并行执行。在许多实际场景中，有效的任务完成需要载体间的交接与合作。在 ABot-Claw 中，所有机器人作为协作节点运行，共享相同的全局任务目标和环境记忆，这使得运行时能够在子任务超出单个机器人能力或协作能带来更高效率时，执行意图感知的路由与动态重分配。


Our task allocation strategy jointly considers four factors: capability, location, load, and priority. Capability-based assignment matches task requirements to robot morphology, location-based assignment reduces travel time by favoring nearby robots, load-based assignment avoids overloading individual embodiments, and priority-based assignment ensures urgent requests are dispatched to available robots first. For instance, in an object transfer task, a mobile robot may deliver an object to a workstation, after which the runtime hands over the object pose and execution context to a fixed manipulator for precise placement or assembly. This centralized-brain, multi-body relay pattern allows ABot-Claw to solve compound spatial tasks that are difficult or inefficient for any single robot morphology alone.
我们的任务分配策略综合考虑了四个因素：能力、位置、负载和优先级。基于能力的分配将任务需求与机器人形态相匹配，基于位置的分配通过优先选择邻近机器人来减少行程时间，基于负载的分配避免单个载体过载，而基于优先级的分配确保紧急请求优先分发给可用机器人。例如，在物体转移任务中，移动机器人可将物体运送到工作站，随后运行时将物体位姿和执行上下文移交给固定机械臂进行精确放置或组装。这种“集中大脑、多体接力”的模式使 ABot-Claw 能够解决仅靠单一机器人形态难以完成或效率低下的复合空间任务。


While embodiment integration allows ABot-Claw to act through multiple bodies, these bodies still require a shared understanding of the environment. We therefore next introduce a memory system that aggregates observations across robots into a persistent, queryable, and action-ready world context.
虽然载体集成使 ABot-Claw 能够通过多个主体行动，但这些主体仍需对环境有共享的理解。因此，我们接下来引入一种记忆系统，将各机器人的观测结果聚合为持久、可查询且可直接用于行动的世界上下文。


### 2.3 Visual-Centric Cross-Embodiment Multimodal Memory
### 2.3 以视觉为中心的跨载体多模态记忆


Embodied execution requires more than instantaneous perception. A robot acting in the physical world must be able to remember where objects were previously seen, which places have already been explored, and what spatial relations are relevant to the current instruction. However, neither geometric maps nor text-only logs are sufficient on their own. Geometric maps offer precise localization but are poorly aligned with natural language, while textual summaries lose critical visual and spatial detail. This mismatch becomes more pronounced in open-world settings, where object categories, attributes, and relations are often long-tailed and difficult to compress into fixed symbolic labels.
具身执行需要的不仅仅是瞬时感知。在物理世界中行动的机器人必须能够记住物体之前出现的位置、哪些区域已被探索，以及哪些空间关系与当前指令相关。然而，几何地图或纯文本日志单独使用均不足够。几何地图提供精确的定位，但与自然语言的对齐效果较差；而文本摘要则丢失了关键的视觉和空间细节。这种不匹配在开放世界设置中更为显著，因为物体类别、属性和关系往往呈现长尾分布，难以压缩为固定的符号标签。

To address this gap, we build a visual-centric multimodal memory that stores observations from different robots in a shared memory space. Rather than forcing all perception outputs into text, we preserve visual, semantic, and spatial information at a level that remains useful for later retrieval and execution. This memory serves as the grounding knowledge between high-level reasoning and physical execution: it gives the agent a persistent notion of where things are, what has been observed, and how the retrieved result can be translated into a concrete target for navigation or manipulation.
为解决这一差距，我们构建了一种以视觉为中心的多模态记忆，将来自不同机器人的观测结果存储在共享的记忆空间中。我们没有强行将所有感知输出转化为文本，而是保留了视觉、语义和空间信息，使其在后续检索和执行中依然有效。这种记忆充当了高层推理与物理执行之间的基础知识：它为智能体提供了关于物体位置、已观测内容以及如何将检索结果转化为导航或操作具体目标的持久认知。


Memory entities. We organize the memory around four types of entities, each corresponding to a different granularity of embodied context.
记忆实体。我们围绕四种类型的实体组织记忆，每种实体对应不同粒度的具身上下文。


Visual memory stores scene-level observations together with their semantic and spatiotemporal context. Each entry contains a visual embedding extracted by a vision-language encoder, enabling open-vocabulary retrieval over unconstrained observations. In parallel, the system maintains sparse keyframes selected from long visual streams according to information density and scene novelty [1]. Each keyframe is associated with timestamp and pose metadata, allowing the runtime to retrieve not only semantically relevant scenes but also informative visual snapshots for environment initialization, historical review, and viewpoint revisiting across robots. By combining semantic embeddings with keyframe-level structure, visual memory supports both language-driven search and efficient trajectory summarization.
视觉记忆将场景级别的观测与其语义和时空上下文一起存储。每个条目包含一个由视觉语言编码器提取的视觉嵌入，可对无约束的观测进行开放词汇检索。同时，系统会根据信息密度和场景新颖性从长视觉流中选择并维护稀疏关键帧 [1]。每个关键帧都关联着时间戳和位姿元数据，这使得运行时不仅能检索语义相关的场景，还能获取用于环境初始化、历史回顾以及跨机器人视点重访的信息丰富的视觉快照。通过将语义嵌入与关键帧级结构相结合，视觉记忆既支持基于语言的搜索，也支持高效的轨迹总结。


Object-centric memory anchors entities relevant to downstream interaction. Continuous visual detectors identify salient objects in the scene and store their category labels, observation timestamps, source robot identifiers, and associated spatial information like 3D poses. This memory is particularly useful for tasks such as grasping and placing, where the runtime needs object-level grounding and the ability to recover the last known observation of a target.
以对象为中心的记忆锚定与下游交互相关的实体。连续视觉检测器识别场景中的显著对象，并存储其类别标签、观测时间戳、源机器人标识符以及 3D 位姿等相关空间信息。这种记忆对于抓取和放置等任务尤为重要，因为运行时需要对象级的定位能力，并能恢复目标的最后已知观测状态。


Place-anchor memory represents semantically meaningful locations in the environment. Through automatic registration or user annotation, selected coordinates are associated with names such as kitchen, entrance, or sofa area. These anchors discretize continuous space into language-friendly nodes, making it easier for the planner to reason about destinations, neighborhoods, and task-relevant regions.
地点锚点记忆代表环境中具有语义意义的位置。通过自动注册或用户标注，选定的坐标被关联至如“厨房”、“入口”或“沙发区”等名称。这些锚点将连续空间离散化为语言友好的节点，使规划器更容易对目的地、邻近区域及任务相关区域进行推理。


Retrieval mechanisms. Because different memory entities capture different kinds of context, we support two complementary retrieval paradigms.
检索机制。由于不同的记忆实体捕获不同类型的上下文，我们支持两种互补的检索范式。


For image-semantic memory, we adopt latent-space cross-modal retrieval. A vision-language encoder maps both visual observations and textual queries into a shared embedding space, and retrieval is performed by nearest-neighbor search using cosine similarity. Which allows the agent to search memory directly from natural language, even when the query refers to compositions of attributes and relations that would be difficult to specify through predefined tags alone.
对于图像语义记忆，我们采用潜在空间跨模态检索。视觉-语言编码器将视觉观测和文本查询映射到共享嵌入空间，并通过余弦相似度进行最近邻搜索。这使得智能体能够直接通过自然语言搜索记忆，即使查询涉及难以通过预定义标签单独指定的属性和关系组合。


For object-centric and place-anchor memory, we use structured retrieval over discrete metadata such as object category, source robot, time window, and spatial constraints. In practice, these structured filters can be combined with semantic retrieval. For example, the runtime may first retrieve semantically relevant frames and then narrow the result to observations from a recent time window or from a particular area of the environment.
对于以对象为中心和地点锚点记忆，我们对对象类别、源机器人、时间窗口和空间约束等离散元数据进行结构化检索。在实践中，这些结构化过滤器可以与语义检索相结合。例如，运行时可以先检索语义相关的帧，然后将结果缩小到最近时间窗口或环境特定区域的观测范围内。


Navigable return protocol. A memory system is only useful for embodied execution if its outputs can be consumed directly by downstream controllers. To avoid burdening the language model with modality-specific parsing logic, we normalize all retrieval results into a unified navigable return protocol. The guiding principle is simple: multimodal input, spatialized output.
可导航返回协议。记忆系统只有在输出能被下游控制器直接使用时，对具身执行才有意义。为了避免给语言模型增加模态特定的解析逻辑负担，我们将所有检索结果归一化为统一的可导航返回协议。其指导原则很简单：多模态输入，空间化输出。


Regardless of whether a retrieval result originates from vector similarity over semantic frames or structured lookup over object detections, the memory module returns a standard action-ready representation, which includes semantic category, confidence score, visual evidence, and most importantly a stable 3D pose in the global coordinate frame. The runtime therefore can directly pass the returned pose into a navigation stack or motion planner for the selected robot embodiment.
无论检索结果源自语义帧的向量相似度还是对象检测的结构化查找，记忆模块都会返回一个标准的、可执行的表示，其中包括语义类别、置信度分数、视觉证据，以及最重要的——全局坐标系下的稳定 3D 位姿。因此，运行时可以直接将返回的位姿传递给所选机器人实体的导航栈或运动规划器。


Shared memory across embodiments. All connected robots contribute to and consume from the same memory space. This shared design reduces repeated exploration, since newly added robots can reuse observations already gathered by other embodiments. In practice, this improves efficiency in multi-robot settings and allows the runtime to accumulate environmental context over time, even when the active embodiment changes from one stage of the task to the next.
跨实体的共享记忆。所有连接的机器人共同贡献并使用同一记忆空间。这种共享设计减少了重复探索，因为新加入的机器人可以复用其他实体已收集的观测结果。在实践中，这提高了多机器人环境下的效率，并允许运行时随时间积累环境上下文，即使任务在不同阶段切换了活跃实体。


Once such a shared memory is in place, the runtime can reason over persistent environmental context rather than reacting only to immediate observations. However, memory and planning alone are still insufficient for robust real-world execution. The next component of ABot-Claw therefore introduces an explicit critic that evaluates ongoing progress and closes the loop between planning and action.
一旦建立了这种共享记忆，运行时便能基于持久的环境上下文进行推理，而不仅仅是对即时观测做出反应。然而，仅靠记忆和规划不足以实现稳健的现实世界执行。因此，ABot-Claw 的下一个组件引入了一个显式评估器（Critic），用于评估执行进度并闭合规划与行动之间的回路。


### 2.4 Closed-Loop Feedback with a Generalist Critic
### 2.4 基于通用评估器的闭环反馈


Even with heterogeneous embodiments and grounded memory, real-world execution remains uncertain. Objects may move, detections may fail, manipulators may drift, and a plan that looked valid at the start of a trajectory may stop being appropriate after only a few steps. To improve robustness, we equip ABot-Claw with an explicit critic module that evaluates task progress during execution and provides decision signals for intervention.
即使具备异构实体和定位记忆，现实世界的执行仍存在不确定性。物体可能会移动、检测可能失败、机械臂可能发生漂移，而轨迹开始时看起来有效的计划在执行几步后可能就不再适用。为了提高稳健性，我们为 ABot-Claw 配备了一个显式评估器模块，用于在执行过程中评估任务进度并提供干预决策信号。


State evaluation with a generalist reward model. We instantiate the critic with a generalist reward model that takes the task instruction together with the current observation and returns a scalar progress signal. Intuitively, this score reflects how well the current state aligns with the intended goal. Unlike a binary success detector, such a signal can be useful throughout execution, providing an additional source of evidence about whether the current behavior is making progress.
基于通用奖励模型的状态评估。我们通过一个通用奖励模型来实现评估器，该模型接收任务指令和当前观测，并返回一个标量进度信号。直观地说，该分数反映了当前状态与预期目标的契合程度。与二元成功检测器不同，这种信号在整个执行过程中都很有用，提供了关于当前行为是否取得进展的额外证据来源。


Adaptive correction and strategy switching. We use this critic signal to support three basic decisions during runtime. When the score exceeds a task-dependent threshold, the system marks the current subtask as complete and proceeds to the next stage. When the score remains below the completion threshold but still indicates improvement, the system keeps the current strategy and attempts local refinement, such as adjusting a target pose, updating the viewpoint, or repeating a short action sequence. When the score stagnates or drops significantly, the runtime treats the current strategy as ineffective and triggers replanning, potentially with help from the shared memory.
自适应校正与策略切换。我们利用该评估信号在运行时支持三个基本决策。当分数超过任务相关阈值时，系统标记当前子任务完成并进入下一阶段。当分数低于完成阈值但仍显示有所改善时，系统保持当前策略并尝试局部优化，例如调整目标位姿、更新视角或重复短动作序列。当分数停滞或显著下降时，运行时会将当前策略视为无效并触发重规划，必要时可借助共享记忆。


Closing the execution loop. The critic does not only improve immediate task robustness, it also enriches the system's long-term experience. We log execution traces together with critic scores and feed them back into the runtime as structured experience. Over time, this creates a closed loop from execution to evaluation and from evaluation to future decision-making. In this sense, ABot-Claw extends OpenClaw from a task orchestration framework into a real-world runtime with online supervision and self-correction.
闭合执行回路。评估器不仅提高了即时任务的稳健性，还丰富了系统的长期经验。我们将执行轨迹连同评估分数一起记录，并作为结构化经验反馈回运行时。从这个意义上说，ABot-Claw 将 OpenClaw 从一个任务编排框架扩展为一个具备在线监督和自我校正能力的现实世界运行时。


### 2.5 End-to-End Execution Flow
### 2.5 端到端执行流程


We now summarize how embodiment integration, memory, and feedback interact during runtime. Although the exact execution path depends on the task and selected robot, the overall behavior follows a common loop.
我们现在总结在运行时，具身集成、记忆和反馈是如何交互的。尽管具体的执行路径取决于任务和所选机器人，但整体行为遵循一个通用的循环。


Instruction grounding and embodiment selection. Given a user instruction, the OpenClaw runtime first interprets the task at a high level and selects a suitable embodiment or set of embodiments based on available skills, current status, and spatial context. If the task refers to previously observed places or objects, the runtime can query memory before acting, so that execution starts from grounded context rather than blind exploration.
指令对齐与具身选择。给定用户指令后，OpenClaw 运行时首先从高层级解析任务，并根据可用技能、当前状态和空间上下文选择合适的具身或具身组合。如果任务涉及先前观察到的地点或物体，运行时可以在行动前查询记忆，从而使执行从已对齐的上下文开始，而非盲目探索。


Memory-assisted action generation. Once the active embodiment is selected, the runtime invokes the corresponding skill or model service to generate executable actions. Depending on the task, this may include navigation, grasping, object search, or other embodied operations. At this stage, the memory layer may provide place anchors, object observations or semantic keyframe context that helps localize the target and reduce unnecessary search.
记忆辅助的动作生成。一旦选定活动的具身，运行时便调用相应的技能或模型服务来生成可执行动作。根据任务的不同，这可能包括导航、抓取、物体搜索或其他具身操作。在此阶段，记忆层可以提供地点锚点、物体观测或语义关键帧上下文，有助于定位目标并减少不必要的搜索。


Execution and progress evaluation. The selected robot then executes the action through its own control stack while the runtime monitors intermediate observations and status updates. During or after execution, the critic module evaluates task progress with respect to the original instruction and current observation, returning a signal that reflects whether the subtask is advancing toward completion.
执行与进度评估。选定的机器人通过其自身的控制栈执行动作，同时运行时监控中间观测结果和状态更新。在执行期间或执行后，评价器模块会根据原始指令和当前观测评估任务进度，返回反映子任务是否正向完成推进的信号。


Update, refinement, or replanning. Finally, the runtime uses this feedback to decide what to do next. Successful executions are written back into memory when appropriate. Partially successful attempts may trigger local refinement. Failed or stalled attempts may trigger a new memory query, a revised plan, or a different embodiment assignment. This closes the loop between environment grounding, physical execution, and high-level task organization.
更新、优化或重规划。最后，运行时利用此反馈决定下一步行动。成功的执行会在适当时写回记忆。部分成功的尝试可能会触发局部优化。失败或停滞的尝试可能会触发新的记忆查询、修订后的计划或不同的具身分配。这闭合了环境对齐、物理执行与高层级任务组织之间的循环。


Overall, ABot-Claw integrates heterogeneous robot access, visual-centric multimodal memory, and critic-based execution feedback into a unified embodied runtime. This design gives OpenClaw a practical path from software task orchestration to real-world embodied operation, while keeping the system modular enough to support different robot morphologies, perception services, and control backends.
总而言之，ABot-Claw 将异构机器人访问、视觉中心的多模态记忆以及基于评价器的执行反馈集成到一个统一的具身运行时中。这种设计为 OpenClaw 提供了一条从软件任务编排到现实世界具身操作的实用路径，同时保持了系统的模块化，以支持不同的机器人形态、感知服务和控制后端。

### 3.2 OpenClaw Layer
### 3.2 OpenClaw 层


The OpenClaw layer is designed as a unified interface for human-robot interaction and high-level decision making. Natural language instructions are parsed into structured task representations. Task context, skill abstractions, and device states are maintained within this layer. OpenClaw is not bound to any specific robotic platform. It functions as a general intelligent agent that abstracts and orchestrates heterogeneous robot and service resources as shown in Figure 4.
OpenClaw 层被设计为用于人机交互和高层级决策的统一接口。自然语言指令被解析为结构化的任务表示。任务上下文、技能抽象和设备状态均在此层维护。OpenClaw 不绑定于任何特定的机器人平台。它作为一个通用的智能代理，对异构机器人和资源服务进行抽象与编排，如图 4 所示。


Task execution is formulated as a mapping from structured task specifications to available resources. Tasks are decomposed into sequential sub-tasks when necessary. Resource allocation is determined based on task requirements. For instance, fixed-base manipulation tasks are assigned to robotic arms, while large-area perception tasks are assigned to mobile platforms. This mapping ensures consistent and fine-grained scheduling without relying on platform-specific logic.
任务执行被表述为从结构化任务规范到可用资源的映射。任务在必要时被分解为顺序子任务。资源分配根据任务需求确定。例如，固定基座操作任务被分配给机械臂，而大范围感知任务则分配给移动平台。这种映射确保了调度的一致性和细粒度，且无需依赖平台特定的逻辑。


A capability-driven scheduling mechanism is implemented. Each robot exposes a structured description of its capabilities. Task allocation is performed by matching required capabilities with available resources. Static binding to robot identities is avoided. OpenClaw supports two scheduling modes: tasks can be assigned explicitly to user-specified robots, or resources can be selected automatically. This design improves flexibility and robustness under resource variation and system heterogeneity. New robots can be integrated through capability registration without modifying the scheduling policy.
系统实现了基于能力的调度机制。每台机器人均提供其能力的结构化描述。任务分配通过匹配所需能力与可用资源来完成，避免了与机器人身份的静态绑定。OpenClaw 支持两种调度模式：任务可明确分配给用户指定的机器人，或由系统自动选择资源。该设计提升了在资源变动与系统异构环境下的灵活性与鲁棒性。新机器人可通过能力注册接入，无需修改调度策略。


Critically, OpenClaw outputs a fully executable Python script corresponding to the scheduled task. This script encodes all selected skills, resource bindings, and execution sequences. The generated Python file serves as the concrete interface between high-level task planning and robot-side execution, ensuring reproducibility and traceability of the planned operations. Simultaneously, the execution results are provided in real-time, and new executable code is output.
关键在于，OpenClaw 会输出与调度任务完全对应的可执行 Python 脚本。该脚本编码了所有选定的技能、资源绑定及执行序列。生成的 Python 文件作为高层任务规划与机器人端执行之间的具体接口，确保了规划操作的可复现性与可追溯性。同时，系统实时提供执行结果，并输出新的可执行代码。


System-level coordination is also maintained. Task commands are dispatched, and execution feedback is collected. Failure handling and task replanning are triggered when required. External services, such as perception and memory modules, are accessed through standardized interfaces. Execution strategies are updated based on returned results. As a result, OpenClaw functions as a high-level orchestration module, producing executable task representations that bridge planning and physical execution.
系统级协调也得以维持。任务指令被分发，执行反馈被收集。在需要时触发故障处理和任务重规划。感知和记忆模块等外部服务通过标准化接口进行访问。执行策略根据返回的结果进行更新。因此，OpenClaw 作为高层编排模块，生成连接规划与物理执行的可执行任务表示。


A low-coupling integration scheme is employed. New robots and service modules are incorporated through standardized interfaces and capability descriptions. The core scheduling mechanism and OpenClaw output remain unchanged, improving scalability and supporting long-term system evolution.
采用了低耦合集成方案。新的机器人和服务模块通过标准化接口和能力描述进行整合。核心调度机制和 OpenClaw 的输出保持不变，从而提高了可扩展性并支持系统的长期演进。


### 3.3 Shared Service Layer
### 3.3 共享服务层


The shared service layer is designed to host computationally intensive and highly reusable modules that are weakly coupled with specific robot embodiments. Key functionalities, including object detection [4], grasp perception [7], spatial memory, and task evaluation [26], are decoupled from both the robot layer and the OpenClaw layer. These functionalities are deployed as independent services. A unified interface is thus provided for capability access and extension.
共享服务层旨在承载计算密集且高度可复用的模块，这些模块与特定机器人实体的耦合较弱。包括目标检测 [4]、抓取感知 [7]、空间记忆和任务评估 [26] 在内的关键功能与机器人层和 OpenClaw 层解耦。这些功能被部署为独立服务，从而为能力访问和扩展提供了统一接口。


The shared service layer is organized into three categories. First, perception services are provided, including object detection, depth-assisted localization, and grasp candidate generation. These services support environment understanding and action priors. Second, memory services are implemented to maintain shared spatial-semantic representations. Objects, locations, and scene structures are stored in a persistent form. This design supports context recovery and long-horizon task execution. Third, evaluation services are included to assess task progress and completion. Structured feedback signals are generated to support policy adjustment and execution monitoring.
共享服务层分为三类。首先是感知服务，包括物体检测、深度辅助定位和抓取候选生成，这些服务支持环境理解和动作先验。其次是记忆服务，用于维护共享的空间语义表示，物体、位置和场景结构以持久化形式存储，该设计支持上下文恢复和长程任务执行。第三是评估服务，用于评估任务进度和完成情况，生成结构化反馈信号以支持策略调整和执行监控。


The service layer is further designed as a platform for model-level extension. Advanced models such as VLA , VLN, and WAM, can be integrated through standardized interfaces. These models are typically computationally expensive and require independent runtime environments. Their deployment in the service layer avoids intrusive modification of robot control pipelines. A unified access interface is maintained for both OpenClaw and robot embodiments. This design enables a smooth transition from modular functionality to model-driven capability evolution.
服务层还被设计为模型级扩展平台。VLA、VLN 和 WAM 等先进模型可以通过标准化接口集成。这些模型通常计算开销大且需要独立的运行环境，将其部署在服务层避免了对机器人控制流水线的侵入式修改。OpenClaw 和机器人实体均维持统一的访问接口，该设计实现了从模块化功能到模型驱动能力演进的平滑过渡。


Service-oriented deployment provides several engineering advantages. Computational resources, such as GPUs, can be centrally managed. Model deployment and version control are simplified. New models or algorithms can be incorporated through interface extension and service registration. No modification of the control logic or scheduling framework is required. This property supports rapid iteration and comparative evaluation of different methods.
面向服务的部署带来了多项工程优势。GPU 等计算资源可以集中管理，模型部署和版本控制得到简化。新模型或算法可以通过接口扩展和服务注册来整合，无需修改控制逻辑或调度框架。这一特性支持快速迭代和不同方法的对比评估。


The shared service layer also enables unified interaction across system components. OpenClaw can invoke services for task decomposition, environment understanding, and execution evaluation. Robot embodiments can access services during execution for online perception and feedback. Service outputs can be written back to shared memory or task context. A closed-loop interaction is thus established among OpenClaw, robot embodiments, and service modules.
共享服务层还实现了跨系统组件的统一交互。OpenClaw 可以调用服务进行任务分解、环境理解和执行评估。机器人实体在执行过程中可访问服务以获取在线感知和反馈。服务输出可写回共享内存或任务上下文。由此，OpenClaw、机器人实体和服务模块之间建立了闭环交互。


In multi-robot scenarios, the shared service layer provides a foundation for information sharing and capability reuse. Perception, memory, and evaluation services are shared across robots. Redundant deployment is avoided. Environment knowledge can be accumulated and reused. For example, observations collected by a mobile robot can be stored and later accessed by a manipulation robot. This mechanism supports the transition from parallel execution to collaborative operation.
在多机器人场景中，共享服务层为信息共享和能力复用提供了基础。感知、记忆和评估服务在机器人间共享，避免了冗余部署。环境知识得以积累和复用。例如，移动机器人收集的观测结果可以被存储，并随后由操作机器人访问。该机制支持从并行执行向协作操作的转变。


### 3.4 Robot Layer
### 3.4 机器人层


The robot embodiment execution layer maps high-level action semantics into executable commands for specific hardware platforms. A ROS-based interface is adopted to standardize communication [10]. This layer directly interfaces with physical systems and concentrates hardware-dependent components, including drivers, sensors, and control interfaces.
机器人具身执行层将高级动作语义映射为特定硬件平台的可执行命令。采用基于ROS的接口来规范通信 [10]。该层直接与物理系统对接，集中了依赖硬件的组件，包括驱动器、传感器和控制接口。


Robot-side functionalities are organized into shared and platform-specific modules. Shared modules define unified representations for execution flow, state feedback, and cross-layer communication. Platform-specific modules implement driver adaptation, topic mapping, and sensor integration. Embodiment heterogeneity is confined to these modules, while upper-layer interfaces remain unchanged. This design isolates hardware variation without introducing excessive abstraction.
机器人侧功能被组织为共享模块和平台特定模块。共享模块定义了执行流、状态反馈和跨层通信的统一表示。平台特定模块实现驱动适配、话题映射和传感器集成。机器人异构性被限制在这些模块内，而上层接口保持不变。该设计在不引入过度抽象的前提下隔离了硬件差异。


ROS communication is used to achieve interface consistency across different robot. Although implementations differ, their interaction patterns are reduced to a set of stable topic structures. For each robot, sensory inputs and robot states are expressed through standardized topics. System migration across platforms is achieved by adjusting configuration and topic mapping only. Upper-layer logic is not modified. Cross-embodied generalization is thus achieved under weak interface constraints.
ROS 通信被用于实现不同机器人间的接口一致性。尽管实现方式各异，但它们的交互模式被简化为一组稳定的主题结构。对于每个机器人，感官输入和机器人状态通过标准化主题表达。通过仅调整配置和主题映射即可实现跨平台系统迁移，无需修改上层逻辑。从而在弱接口约束下实现了跨实体泛化。


Low-level control is not standardized across robot types. Instead, structural and functional differences are explicitly maintained within this layer. Each robot embodies specific physical capabilities, such as precise manipulation, human-centered interaction, or mobility over uneven terrain. Consequently, this layer serves as the concrete instantiation of embodied abilities. High-level scheduling in OpenClaw operates on these capability-defined execution units rather than on generic or abstract devices.
底层控制并未在不同机器人类型间标准化。相反，结构和功能差异被明确保留在该层内。每个机器人具备特定的物理能力，如精密操作、以人为中心的交互或在不平坦地形上的移动性。因此，该层作为具身能力的具体实例化。OpenClaw 中的高层调度基于这些由能力定义的执行单元运行，而非基于通用或抽象设备。


This layer also forms the execution interface of the system loop. High-level commands are received, and execution feedback is returned. Service-layer modules are invoked when additional perception or planning is required. Intermediate results are propagated to support task continuation. As a result, high-level task representations are consistently grounded into physical execution.
该层还构成了系统循环的执行接口。接收高层指令并返回执行反馈。当需要额外的感知或规划时，调用服务层模块。中间结果被传播以支持任务持续。因此，高层任务表示被一致地落地为物理执行。

最后一个示例重点展示使用四足机器人进行访客接待与引导。任务是在电梯口迎接访客并将其护送至会议室，如图 9 所示。与之前的任务相比，该场景强调了形态选择、敏捷导航、人员检测以及在更长空间范围内的持续引导。


ABot-Claw selects the quadruped as the most suitable embodiment because the task primarily requires mobility and person-guiding behavior rather than manipulation. The robot is dispatched to the elevator area, where it searches for and identifies the arriving visitor. After detection, it initiates the guidance behavior and leads the visitor along the planned route to the meeting room while the runtime monitors task progress. This demonstration highlights that embodiment selection can be conditioned on task structure and that the same runtime can coordinate reception-style behaviors on a mobile legged platform.
ABot-Claw 选择四足机器人作为最合适的形态，因为该任务主要需要移动性和引导行为，而非操作能力。机器人被派遣至电梯区域，搜索并识别到达的访客。检测到访客后，它启动引导行为，沿着规划路线将访客带至会议室，同时运行时系统会监控任务进度。该演示突显了形态选择可以基于任务结构进行调整，且同一运行时系统能够在移动足式平台上协调接待类行为。

## 5 Conclusion
## 5 结论


In this report, ABot-Claw was presented as an embodied intelligence framework that extends the OpenClaw runtime into a general foundation for real-world robotic execution. To reduce the gap between high-level reasoning and physical interaction, three key components were integrated into the framework: 1) critic-based closed-loop feedback mechanism for online closed-loop feedback and policy adjustment, 2) a visual-centric multimodal memory for cross-modal context retention, and 3) an elastic multi-agent scheduling architecture based on dynamic routing tables. By adopting a decoupled engineering design, heterogeneous embodiment interfaces were standardized, which enabled coordinated deployment across diverse robotic platforms, including robotic arms, humanoids, and quadrupeds.
在本报告中，我们介绍了 ABot-Claw，这是一个将 OpenClaw 运行时扩展为现实世界机器人执行通用基础的具身智能框架。为缩小高层推理与物理交互之间的差距，框架集成了三个关键组件：1）用于在线闭环反馈和策略调整的基于评价器的闭环反馈机制；2）用于跨模态上下文保留的视觉中心多模态内存；3）基于动态路由表的弹性多智能体调度架构。通过采用解耦的工程设计，标准化了异构形态接口，从而实现了在包括机械臂、人形机器人和四足机器人在内的多种机器人平台上的协同部署。

图 9 四足机器人引导访客接待演示。根据在电梯口迎接访客并将其引导至会议室的指令，系统选择四足机器人，将其派遣至电梯口，检测访客并将其引导至目的地。


Experimental results showed that ABot-Claw can autonomously execute complex manipulation and search tasks. Robust performance was demonstrated under partial observability, and ambiguous semantic instructions were successfully grounded into executable actions. These results validate the effectiveness and flexibility of the proposed framework in open-world robotic settings.
实验结果表明，ABot-Claw 能够自主执行复杂的操作和搜索任务。系统在部分可观测环境下表现出稳健的性能，并成功将模糊的语义指令转化为可执行动作。这些结果验证了所提框架在开放世界机器人场景中的有效性和灵活性。


Overall, ABot-Claw provides a scalable infrastructure for general-purpose embodied agents. It offers a practical step toward robotic systems that are more persistent, cooperative, and adaptive in real-world environments.
总而言之，ABot-Claw 为通用具身智能体提供了可扩展的基础设施。它为实现更具持久性、协作性和适应性的现实环境机器人系统迈出了务实的一步。

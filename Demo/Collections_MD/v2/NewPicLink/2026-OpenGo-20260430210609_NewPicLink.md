## Abstract
## 摘要


Adaptation to complex tasks and multiple scenarios remains a significant challenge for a single robot agent. The ability to acquire organize, and switch between a wide range of skills in real time, particularly in dynamic environments, has become a fundamental requirement for embodied intelligence. We introduce OpenGo, an OpenClaw-powered embodied robotic dog capable of switching skills in real time according to the scene and task instructions. Specifically, the agent is equipped with (1) a customizable skill library with easy skill import and autonomous skill validation, (2) a dispatcher that selects and invokes different skills according to task prompts or language instructions, and (3) a self-learning framework that fine-tunes skills based on task completion and human feedback. We deploy the agent in Unitree's Go2 robotic dog and validate its capabilities in self-checking and switching of skills autonomously. In addition, by integrating Feishu-platform communication, we enable natural-language guidance and human feedback, allowing inexperienced users to control the robotic dog through simple instructions.
对于单一机器人智能体而言，适应复杂任务和多场景环境仍是一项重大挑战。在动态环境中实时获取、组织并切换多种技能，已成为具身智能的基本要求。我们推出了 OpenGo，这是一款由 OpenClaw 驱动的具身机器狗，能够根据场景和任务指令实时切换技能。具体而言，该智能体配备了：(1) 一个支持便捷导入与自主验证的可定制技能库；(2) 一个根据任务提示或语言指令选择并调用不同技能的调度器；(3) 一个基于任务完成情况和人类反馈对技能进行微调的自学习框架。我们将该智能体部署在宇树（Unitree）Go2 机器狗上，并验证了其自主检查和切换技能的能力。此外，通过集成飞书平台通信，我们实现了自然语言引导和人类反馈，使非专业用户也能通过简单指令控制机器狗。


## 1 Introduction
## 1 引言


Robust quadruped robot tasks in diverse environments remain a fundamental challenge. Robots must simultaneously deal with rapidly changing environments, indoor or outdoor spaces, sensory degradation, and safety behavioral limitations. Even strong reinforcement-learning-based quadruped controllers that show zero-shot transfer on rough outdoor terrain still face significant difficulty in harder conditions where perception is unreliable, such as snow, plants, water, fog, reflective surfaces, or partial occlusion. [1] In practice, this is why recent high-performance navigation systems for quadruped robots still incorporate multiple obstacle or terrain-aware skills, such as walking, jumping, going up and down stairs, climbing, and crouching, rather than relying on a single general strategy. [2,3] This trend suggests a key limitation for embodied agents: while a general skill may work in nominal settings, its re-usability often degrades sharply in extreme environments, where success depends on explicit awareness of scene geometry, safety margins, and the robot's executable capability set. [4, 5]
在多样环境中执行稳健的四足机器人任务仍是一项根本性挑战。机器人必须同时应对快速变化的环境、室内或室外空间、感官性能下降以及安全行为限制。即使是那些在户外崎岖地形上能实现零样本迁移的强大的基于强化学习的四足控制器，在感知不可靠的更恶劣条件下（如雪、植物、水、雾、反光表面或部分遮挡）仍面临巨大困难。[1] 实际上，这就是为什么近期高性能的四足机器人导航系统仍会整合多种感知障碍物或地形的技能，如行走、跳跃、上下楼梯、攀爬和蹲伏，而非依赖单一通用策略。[2,3] 这一趋势表明具身智能体存在一个关键局限：虽然通用技能在正常环境中可能有效，但其在极端环境中的可复用性往往会急剧下降，因为成功与否取决于对场景几何结构、安全裕度以及机器人可执行能力集的明确认知。[4, 5]


At the same time, embodied intelligence requires a robot to unite perception, semantic grounding, planning, and action under high-level human intent. At the same time, embodied intelligence requires a robot to pair perception, semantic grounding, planning, and action under high-level human intent. Recent large pretrained models have made this increasingly feasible by transferring semantic knowledge learned from internet-scale language and vision corpora into robot decision-making. [6] PaLM-E shows that a single embodied multimodal model can transfer knowledge from visual-language domains to embodied reasoning and robot planning. [7] RT-2 further demonstrates that vision-language-action models can transfer web knowledge directly into robotic control, improving generalization and semantic reasoning. [8] SayCan shows that large language models can be grounded by a library of pretrained skills and affordance/value estimates, enabling long-horizon task execution on real robots. [9, 10] In summary, these results strongly support the feasibility of using LLMs as high-level decision makers in embodied systems, provided that semantic reasoning is tightly coupled to embodiment-aware action grounding.
同时，具身智能要求机器人在人类高层意图下将感知、语义基础、规划和行动统一起来。同时，具身智能要求机器人在人类高层意图下将感知、语义基础、规划和行动进行匹配。最近的大型预训练模型通过将从互联网规模的语言和视觉语料库中学到的语义知识转移到机器人决策中，使这一点越来越可行。[6] PaLM - E 表明，单一的具身多模态模型可以将知识从视觉 - 语言领域转移到具身推理和机器人规划中。[7] RT - 2 进一步证明，视觉 - 语言 - 行动模型可以将网络知识直接转移到机器人控制中，提高泛化能力和语义推理能力。[8] SayCan 表明，大语言模型可以通过预训练技能库以及可供性/价值估计来进行基础构建，从而使真实机器人能够执行长期任务。[9, 10] 总之，这些结果有力地支持了在具身系统中使用大语言模型作为高层决策者的可行性，前提是语义推理要与具身感知的行动基础紧密结合。

在模拟或强化学习环境中，大语言模型驱动的智能体已经展现出令人印象深刻的决策能力。Voyager 使用自动课程、基于代码的技能库和迭代提示，在《我的世界》游戏中实现了开放式的终身技能获取。Eureka 使用 GPT - 4 合成奖励代码，在一系列广泛的强化学习任务中表现优于人工设计的奖励。[11] 更广泛地说，先前的研究表明，语言模型可以为具身智能体生成可执行的计划，并通过环境反馈改进这些计划。[12] 然而，将这种范式从模拟环境转移到物理机器人上要困难得多。正如 SayCan 所指出的，无约束的语言模型可能会生成一个在语言上合理，但对于特定环境中的特定机器人来说不可行的计划。[9] 在机器人技术中，这就是幻觉的实际表现形式：无效的技能调用、不安全的参数分配，或忽略具身性和场景约束的行为。[13] 这正是为什么在实际机器人部署中，直接端到端地依赖自由形式的大语言模型输出仍然很困难的原因。


To address this issue, we present OpenGo, an OpenClaw-powered embodied robotic dog pipeline in which controllability is anchored in the skill layer rather than in unconstrained language generation. OpenGo maintains a customization skill library, and the role of the LLM is deliberately restricted to two high-level functions: selecting the most appropriate skill for the current scene and task, and setting a bounded set of skill hyperparameter according to task prompts, natural-language guidance, and feedback. By design, this reduces the impact of hallucination, because the final behavior must be instantiated through predefined, robot-compatible, and self-checkable skills. In this way, OpenGo preserves the semantic flexibility of language-guided decision making while improving robustness, interpret-ability. This pipeline has been examined on a real Unitree Go2 platform.
为了解决这一问题，我们提出了 OpenGo，这是一个基于 OpenClaw 的具身机器狗流水线。其可控性锚定在技能层，而非不受约束的语言生成。OpenGo 维护着一个可定制的技能库，LLM 的作用被刻意限制在两个高层功能上：为当前场景和任务选择最合适的技能，并根据任务提示、自然语言指导和反馈设置一组有限的技能超参数。这种设计减少了幻觉的影响，因为最终行为必须通过预定义的、与机器人兼容且可自检的技能来实现。通过这种方式，OpenGo 在保持语言引导决策的语义灵活性的同时，提高了鲁棒性和可解释性。该流水线已在真实的宇树 Go2 平台上进行了验证。


## 2 Related work
## 2 相关工作


### 2.1 LLM-driven decision making
### 2.1 大模型驱动的决策制定


Recent work has shown that large language models can serve as effective high-level controllers for embodied systems. Code as Policies uses language models to generate executable robot policy code that calls perception modules and control units, enabling reactive and waypoint-based behaviors on real robots. [14] ProgPrompt further improves executability by constraining the language model with program-like specifications of available actions and objects, so that task plans remain consistent with the robot's capabilities and the current scene. [15] VIMA demonstrates that a transformer-based agent can solve a broad family of manipulation tasks from multimodal prompts, suggesting that language-like prompting can unify task specification across text, images, and demonstrations. [16] Compared with direct low-level action generation, these approaches collectively indicate that LLMs are most reliable when they operate over structured abstractions-such as APIs, plans, or skills-rather than raw motor commands.
近期研究表明，大语言模型可作为具身系统有效的高级控制器。《代码即策略》（Code as Policies）利用语言模型生成可执行的机器人策略代码，该代码调用感知模块和控制单元，使真实机器人能够实现反应式和基于路点的行为。[14] 《程序提示》（ProgPrompt）通过用可用动作和对象的类程序规范约束语言模型，进一步提高了可执行性，从而使任务计划与机器人的能力和当前场景保持一致。[15] 《视觉交互多模态代理》（VIMA）表明，基于变换器的代理可以根据多模态提示解决广泛的操作任务，这表明类语言提示可以统一文本、图像和演示中的任务规范。[16] 与直接生成低级动作相比，这些方法共同表明，大语言模型在处理结构化抽象（如应用程序编程接口、计划或技能）而非原始运动命令时最为可靠。

### 2.2 Skill libraries and embodied agents.
### 2.2 技能库与具身智能体


Within embodied intelligence, the notions of skill library and agent have become increasingly important as a way to bridge semantic reasoning and physical execution. A skill library represents behavior as a reusable set of callable modules, often parameterized and composable, while the agent is responsible for selecting, sequencing, and adapting those skills according to task context. This pipeline is attractive because it improves modularity, interpretability, reusability, and controllability compared to monolithic end-to-end policies.
在具身智能领域，技能库和智能体的概念作为连接语义推理与物理执行的桥梁，变得愈发重要。技能库将行为表示为一组可调用的、通常经过参数化且可组合的模块；而智能体则负责根据任务上下文选择、排序并调整这些技能。这种流水线具有吸引力，因为它与端到端的整体式策略相比，提高了模块化程度、可解释性、可重用性和可控性。


Voyager is a representative example: it maintains a growing library of executable skills and reuses them to solve unseen tasks in an open-ended environment. [17] More recently, BOSS (Bootstrap Your Own Skills) learns to solve new long-horizon tasks by expanding a language-specified skill library with minimal supervision and then reusing those acquired skills in new environments. [18] Together, these works suggest that a skill-library-based embodied agent provides a practical middle ground between symbolic planning and low-level control, especially when the system must remain extensible, interpretable, and robust under changing task demands. These approaches improve semantic controllability, but they also reveal a common limitation: high-level reasoning remains dependent on either carefully designed symbolic representations, fixed low-level skill set, or a constrained scene., which leads to open-loop plans fail when the robot enters unexpected states.
Voyager是一个典型示例：它维护着一个不断增长的可执行技能库，并在开放式环境中复用这些技能来解决未知任务。[17] 最近，BOSS（自举自身技能）通过在最少监督的情况下扩展一个语言指定的技能库来学习解决新的长期任务，然后在新环境中复用这些习得的技能。[18] 总之，这些研究表明，基于技能库的具身智能体在符号规划和低级控制之间提供了一个实用的中间方案，特别是当系统必须在不断变化的任务需求下保持可扩展性、可解释性和鲁棒性时。这些方法提高了语义可控性，但也暴露出一个共同的局限性：高级推理仍然依赖于精心设计的符号表示、固定的低级技能集或受限的场景，这导致当机器人进入意外状态时，开环规划会失败。

### 2.3 High-level control for quadruped robots.
### 2.3 四足机器人的高层控制


Recent quadruped research has explored several routes toward high-level motion control, each with distinct strengths and limitations. One line of work focuses on perception-aware locomotion: for example, robust perceptive locomotion integrates external and internal sensing to achieve fast and stable traversal in challenging outdoor environments, but its scope remains largely locomotion-centric rather than instruction-centric. [2] Another way uses hierarchical multi-skill control. ANYmal Parkour trains multiple specialized locomotion skills such as walking, jumping, climbing, and crouching - and then employs a high-level policy to switch among them according to the scenario, having strong agility but also relying on a predefined repertoire of obstacle-aware skills. [4] Robot Parkour Learning distills diverse parkour behaviors into a single end-to-end vision-based policy, reducing hierarchy design and enabling autonomous skill selection, but the system still faces practical issues such as perception delay and extreme motor demand. [5]
近期的四足机器人研究探索了几种实现高级运动控制的途径，每种途径都有其独特的优势和局限性。其中一类研究聚焦于感知感知型运动：例如，鲁棒感知运动将外部和内部传感相结合，以在具有挑战性的户外环境中实现快速稳定的移动，但其范围在很大程度上仍以运动为中心，而非以指令为中心。[2] 另一种方法是使用分层多技能控制。ANYmal Parkour 训练多种专门的运动技能，如行走、跳跃、攀爬和蹲伏，然后采用高级策略根据场景在这些技能之间进行切换，具有很强的灵活性，但也依赖于预先定义的、具备障碍物感知能力的技能库。[4] 《机器人跑酷学习》将各种跑酷行为提炼为单一的基于端到端视觉的策略，减少了层级设计并实现了自主技能选择，但该系统仍面临感知延迟和极高的运动需求等实际问题。[5]


## 3 Method
## 3 方法


We propose OpenGo, an embodied quadruped agent built on top of OpenClaw for robust task execution across diverse scenarios. As shown in Fig. 1, OpenGo is designed around three tightly coupled components: a customizable skill library, an LLM-based dispatcher, and a self-learning framework driven by execution outcomes and human feedback. The key design principle of OpenGo is to preserve the semantic flexibility of large language models while constraining their decision space to a set of validated robot skills. Instead of allowing the LLM to directly generate low-level robot actions, OpenGo restricts it to selecting skills from a structured library and assigning bounded hyperparame-ters under task and scene constraints. This design improves safety, controllability, and deployability on a real quadruped platform.
我们提出了 OpenGo，这是一个基于 OpenClaw 构建的具身四足智能体，旨在实现跨多种场景的鲁棒任务执行。如图 1 所示，OpenGo 的设计围绕三个紧密耦合的组件展开：可定制技能库、基于大语言模型（LLM）的调度器，以及由执行结果和人类反馈驱动的自学习框架。OpenGo 的核心设计原则是在保留大语言模型语义灵活性的同时，将其决策空间限制在一组经过验证的机器人技能内。OpenGo 不允许 LLM 直接生成底层机器人动作，而是限制其从结构化库中选择技能，并在任务和场景约束下分配有界的超参数。这种设计提升了在真实四足平台上的安全性、可控性和可部署性。


Given a task description $T$ ,a human instruction $I$ ,and a perceived scenario $S$ ,the system dispatcher generates a skill execution plan $\prod \left( {{k}_{1},{\theta }_{1}}\right) ,\left( {{k}_{2},{\theta }_{2}}\right) ,\ldots ,\left( {{k}_{n},{\theta }_{n}}\right)$ where ${k}_{i}$ denotes a callable skill from the library and ${\theta }_{i}$ denotes its task-dependent parameters. During execution,OpenGo monitors robot states and collects completion signals, error logs, and user feedback, which are then used to improve future dispatching and skill usage. In the following, we describe the three core components of the system.
给定任务描述 $T$、人类指令 $I$ 以及感知到的场景 $S$，系统调度器会生成一个技能执行计划 $\prod \left( {{k}_{1},{\theta }_{1}}\right) ,\left( {{k}_{2},{\theta }_{2}}\right) ,\ldots ,\left( {{k}_{n},{\theta }_{n}}\right)$，其中 ${k}_{i}$ 表示库中可调用的技能，${\theta }_{i}$ 表示其与任务相关的参数。在执行过程中，OpenGo 会监控机器人状态并收集完成信号、错误日志和用户反馈，随后利用这些信息改进后续的调度和技能使用。接下来，我们将详细介绍该系统的三个核心组件。


### 3.1 Customizable Skill Library
### 3.1 可定制技能库


The skill library is the foundation of OpenGo. Its purpose is to provide a structured, reusable, and controllable action space for the quadruped robot. Each skill corresponds to an executable robot capability, such as locomotion, posture adjustment, obstacle negotiation, navigation, or task-specific interaction. Rather than treating all robot behavior as an end-to-end policy, OpenGo decomposes execution into a set of callable skill modules that can be explicitly managed, validated, and reused.
技能库是 OpenGo 的基础。其目的是为四足机器人提供一个结构化、可重用且可控的动作空间。每项技能对应一种可执行的机器人能力，例如运动、姿态调整、障碍物处理、导航或特定任务交互。OpenGo 没有将所有机器人行为视为端到端策略，而是将执行过程分解为一组可明确管理、验证和重用的可调用技能模块。


As illustrated in Fig. 2, each skill is represented by a structured template containing five fields: skill head, parameters, constraints, function, and prompts. The skill head defines the skill identifier and its semantic label. The parameters specify adjustable hyperparameters for task adaptation, such as target distance, speed, duration, turning angle, or environment-dependent thresholds. The constraints define preconditions and safety boundaries for execution, such as terrain requirements, valid robot states, actuator limits, or forbidden transitions. The function contains the actual implementation of the skill and is kept fixed once validated, ensuring consistent and robot-compatible execution. The prompts provide textual descriptions or usage instructions that help the dispatcher understand when and how the skill should be invoked.
如图 2 所示，每项技能由一个包含五个字段的结构化模板表示：技能头（skill head）、参数（parameters）、约束（constraints）、函数（function）和提示（prompts）。技能头定义了技能标识符及其语义标签。参数指定了用于任务适配的可调超参数，如目标距离、速度、持续时间、转向角度或依赖于环境的阈值。约束定义了执行的前提条件和安全边界，如地形要求、有效的机器人状态、执行器限制或禁止的转换。函数包含技能的实际实现，一旦验证即保持固定，确保执行的一致性和机器人兼容性。提示提供了文本描述或使用说明，帮助调度器理解何时以及如何调用该技能。


A central feature of our library design is the separation between fixed execution function and adjustable task configuration. In particular, the function is immutable during online execution, while the parameters remain tunable within predefined bounds. This separation prevents the LLM from modifying low-level control behavior directly, while still allowing the system to adapt to task requirements at the semantic level. As a result, OpenGo maintains a controllable interface between high-level language reasoning and low-level robot execution.
我们库设计的一个核心特征是固定执行函数与可调任务配置的分离。具体而言，函数在在线执行期间是不可变的，而参数则可在预定义的范围内进行调整。这种分离防止了 LLM 直接修改底层控制行为，同时仍允许系统在语义层面适应任务需求。因此，OpenGo 在高层语言推理与底层机器人执行之间维持了一个可控的接口。


The skill library is also customizable and extensible. When a new skill is introduced, it does not enter the library immediately. Instead, it passes through a multi-stage import process consisting of: (1) code review, to verify interface compatibility, parameter definitions, and safety assumptions; and (2) simulation-based validation, to test executability and robustness before deployment. Only skills that pass these checks are admitted into the library. This process allows OpenGo to continuously expand its capability set while preserving reliability. In this sense, the library functions not only as an action repository, but also as a safety- and consistency-enforcing abstraction layer for embodied decision making.
技能库同时具备可定制性和可扩展性。当引入一项新技能时，它不会立即进入库中。相反，它必须经过一个多阶段的导入流程，包括：(1) 代码审查，以验证接口兼容性、参数定义和安全假设；(2) 基于仿真的验证，以在部署前测试可执行性和鲁棒性。只有通过这些检查的技能才会被准入库中。这一过程使 OpenGo 能够在保持可靠性的同时持续扩展其能力集。从这个意义上讲，该库不仅是一个动作存储库，更是具身决策中用于强制执行安全性和一致性的抽象层。


### 3.2 The Dispatcher
### 3.2 调度器


The dispatcher is responsible for translating high-level human intent into an executable skill sequence. As shown in Fig. 3, it receives three main categories of input: (1) a task description, which specifies the overall goal; (2) human instructions, which may refine preferences, order, or operating details; and (3) the current scenario state, inferred from the perception and classification modules. Based on these inputs, the dispatcher calls the LLM to generate a structured execution plan over the skill library.
调度器负责将人类的高层意图转化为可执行的技能序列。如图3所示，它接收三类主要输入：(1) 任务描述，明确总体目标；(2) 人类指令，用于细化偏好、顺序或操作细节；(3) 当前场景状态，由感知和分类模块推断得出。基于这些输入，调度器调用大语言模型（LLM）在技能库之上生成结构化的执行计划。


Unlike unconstrained language-based robot control, the OpenGo dispatcher does not permit the LLM to output arbitrary actions. Instead, the LLM operates only over a predefined and validated skill set. Concretely, its role is restricted to two functions:
与不受约束的基于语言的机器人控制不同，OpenGo调度器不允许LLM输出任意动作。相反，LLM仅在预定义且经过验证的技能集内运行。具体而言，其作用仅限于以下两项功能：


1. skill selection, i.e., choosing which skill or sequence of skills should be executed under the current task and scenario;
1. 技能选择，即在当前任务和场景下选择应执行的技能或技能序列；


2. parameter assignment, i.e., setting bounded skill hyperparameters according to the task requirement and environmental context.
2. 参数分配，即根据任务需求和环境背景设置受限的技能超参数。


Formally,given task $T$ ,instruction $I$ ,and scene $S$ ,the dispatcher computes
形式上，给定任务 $T$、指令 $I$ 和场景 $S$，调度器计算得出


$$
\prod  = {f}_{LLM}\left( {T,I,S,K}\right)
$$



where $K$ denotes the skill library and $\prod$ is an ordered sequence of skill-parameter pairs. The output is therefore not a motor command stream, but a symbolic-to-structured plan grounded in validated robot skills.
其中 $K$ 表示技能库，$\prod$ 是技能-参数对的有序序列。因此，输出的不是电机指令流，而是基于已验证机器人技能的符号到结构化计划。


To further reduce hallucination and infeasible outputs, we impose several constraints on dispatching. First, skill candidates are filtered by skill constraints and current perception scenarios before being presented to the LLM. Second, each parameter is restricted to a valid range defined in the corresponding skill template. Third, all selected skills are checked against current execution context through the Memory/State Check module. This module maintains recent execution history, monitors whether the robot is in a valid state for the next step, and provides failure or completion feedback for dynamic replanning.
为了进一步减少幻觉和不可行的输出，我们对调度施加了多项约束。首先，技能候选在呈现给LLM之前，会先经过技能约束和当前感知场景的过滤。其次，每个参数都被限制在相应技能模板定义的有效范围内。第三，所有选定的技能都会通过“记忆/状态检查”模块与当前执行上下文进行核对。该模块维护最近的执行历史，监控机器人是否处于执行下一步的有效状态，并为动态重规划提供失败或完成反馈。

### 3.3 Self-Learning Framework
### 3.3 自学习框架


The third component of OpenGo is a self-learning framework that improves skill usage over time through execution outcomes and human interaction. The goal of this module is not to let the robot freely rewrite its own control code online, but to refine the way skills are selected, parameterized, and reused based on observed success and failure.
OpenGo的第三个组件是一个自学习框架，它通过执行结果和人类交互随时间改进技能的使用。该模块的目标不是让机器人在线自由重写其控制代码，而是根据观察到的成功与失败，优化技能的选择、参数化和重用方式。


During task execution, OpenGo continuously records three types of feedback: (1) task completion signals, indicating whether the goal or subgoal has been achieved; (2) error logs, including failed skill calls, invalid transitions, interrupted execution, or abnormal robot states; and (3) human feedback, which is delivered through the communication platform and may include corrections, preferences, or explicit guidance. This feedback is routed back to the dispatcher and memory module, forming a closed-loop execution pipeline.
在任务执行过程中，OpenGo持续记录三类反馈：(1) 任务完成信号，指示目标或子目标是否已实现；(2) 错误日志，包括失败的技能调用、无效转换、执行中断或机器人异常状态；(3) 人类反馈，通过通信平台传递，可能包含修正、偏好或明确的指导。这些反馈被路由回调度器和记忆模块，形成闭环执行流水线。


The self-learning framework uses these signals at two levels. At the dispatch level, it updates the preference for skill selection under specific scenarios. For example, if one skill consistently fails under a given terrain class while an alternative skill succeeds, the system increases the priority of the successful skill for similar future conditions. At the parameter level, it adjusts default hyperparameter settings within the valid range defined by the skill template. For instance, step length, turning radius, or execution duration may be tuned based on repeated success or failure patterns. In this way, OpenGo gradually improves task completion without violating the structural constraints of the skill library.
自学习框架在两个层面上使用这些信号。在调度层面，它更新特定场景下的技能选择偏好。例如，如果某个技能在特定地形类别下持续失败，而另一个技能成功，系统会提高该成功技能在未来类似条件下的优先级。在参数层面，它在技能模板定义的有效范围内调整默认超参数设置。例如，步长、转弯半径或执行持续时间可以根据重复出现的成功或失败模式进行微调。通过这种方式，OpenGo在不违反技能库结构约束的前提下，逐步提高了任务完成度。


Human feedback plays an especially important role when task ambiguity cannot be resolved from perception alone. Since the system is connected to a communication platform such as Feishu, nonexpert users can provide natural-language instructions during or after execution, such as requesting a different behavior, confirming completion, or correcting an undesirable plan. These user signals are converted into additional supervisory information for future dispatching. This allows OpenGo to incorporate human preferences without requiring direct teleoperation or manual policy engineering.
当仅凭感知无法解决任务歧义时，人类反馈发挥着尤为重要的作用。由于系统连接到飞书等通信平台，非专业用户可以在执行期间或之后提供自然语言指令，例如请求不同的行为、确认完成或纠正不理想的计划。这些用户信号被转换为额外的监督信息，用于未来的调度。这使得OpenGo能够在无需直接遥控或手动策略工程的情况下，整合人类偏好。


Overall, the self-learning framework enables OpenGo to move beyond static task execution. By combining completion signals, execution logs, and human feedback, the system can iteratively improve its decision quality while preserving a safe and interpretable skill structure. This makes OpenGo particularly suitable for practical embodied deployment, where robot behavior must remain both adaptable and controllable over long-term use.
总的来说，自学习框架使OpenGo能够超越静态的任务执行。通过结合完成信号、执行日志和人类反馈，系统可以在保持安全且可解释的技能结构的同时，迭代提升其决策质量。这使得OpenGo特别适用于实际的具身部署，即机器人的行为在长期使用中必须保持适应性和可控性。

### 4.3 System Latency Analysis
### 4.3 系统延迟分析


To quantitatively evaluate the responsiveness of the OpenGo framework, we conduct a system-level latency analysis, decomposing the response time into two scenarios: (1) single-skill execution and (2) multi-skill composition.
为了定量评估 OpenGo 框架的响应能力，我们进行了系统级延迟分析，将响应时间分解为两种场景：（1）单技能执行和（2）多技能组合。


Single-Skill Latency. We measure the response time from the moment a user instruction is issued to the initiation of the corresponding robot action. As illustrated in 5, the latency shows a clear distinction between the first invocation and subsequent executions of the same skill. Specifically, initial execution incurs a significantly higher delay due to skill loading and initialization overhead, including LLM parsing, skill retrieval, and parameter instantiation. In contrast, subsequent executions benefit from cached or preloaded skill representations, resulting in reduced response times.
单技能延迟。我们测量从用户发出指令到机器人开始相应动作的响应时间。如图 5 所示，延迟在首次调用与后续执行同一技能时表现出明显差异。具体而言，由于技能加载和初始化开销（包括 LLM 解析、技能检索和参数实例化），首次执行会产生显著较高的延迟。相比之下，后续执行受益于缓存或预加载的技能表示，从而缩短了响应时间。


Furthermore, we observe that latency increases with the number of input parameters associated with a skill. This is primarily due to the increased complexity in parsing, validation, and binding of parameters within the LLM-based dispatcher, as well as additional overhead in the underlying control interface.
此外，我们观察到延迟会随着技能相关输入参数数量的增加而增加。这主要是由于基于 LLM 的调度器在参数解析、验证和绑定方面的复杂性增加，以及底层控制接口的额外开销所致。


Multi-Skill Composition Latency. We also evaluate scenarios that involve sequences of multiple skills. As shown in 6, the total response time does not scale linearly with the number of skills, indicating that the system introduces additional coordination overhead beyond simple accumulation of individual execution times. This includes inter-skill scheduling, state transitions, and dependency resolution.
多技能组合延迟。我们还评估了涉及多个技能序列的场景。如图 6 所示，总响应时间并不随技能数量呈线性增长，这表明系统在简单的个体执行时间累加之外，引入了额外的协调开销。这包括技能间调度、状态转换和依赖关系解析。


Despite this, a general trend of increasing latency is observed as the number of composed skills grows. Similarly to the single-skill case, the complexity of the parameter of each instruction also plays a critical role in determining the overall response time. Tasks requiring more parameters or more complex configurations tend to incur higher latency.
尽管如此，随着组合技能数量的增加，总体上仍呈现出延迟增加的趋势。与单技能情况类似，每条指令参数的复杂性在决定整体响应时间方面也起着关键作用。需要更多参数或更复杂配置的任务往往会产生更高的延迟。


Discussion. These findings highlight two key system characteristics: (1) the presence of a "cold-start" latency associated with skill initialization, and (2) the impact of parameter complexity on execution efficiency. From a system design perspective, these results suggest that performance can be improved through skill preloading, caching mechanisms, and more efficient parameter parsing strategies. Additionally, optimizing the execution pipeline for multi-skill coordination is critical for enabling smooth long-horizon task execution.
讨论。这些发现突显了两个关键的系统特性：(1) 与技能初始化相关的“冷启动”延迟，以及 (2) 参数复杂度对执行效率的影响。从系统设计角度来看，这些结果表明可以通过技能预加载、缓存机制以及更高效的参数解析策略来提升性能。此外，优化多技能协作的执行流水线对于实现流畅的长程任务执行至关重要。

## 5 Limitations
## 5 局限性


Despite the effectiveness of OpenGo in enabling structured and controllable embodied decision making, several limitations remain in the current system.
尽管 OpenGo 在实现结构化且可控的具身决策方面表现有效，但当前系统仍存在一些局限性。


First, the response latency of the large language model introduces a noticeable delay in the decision pipeline. Specifically, the process from natural language understanding to skill retrieval and final generation of the execution sequence requires a non-negligible initialization time. This latency limits the responsiveness of the system, particularly in time-sensitive or dynamic environments where rapid decision making is critical. The issue becomes more pronounced when tasks require frequent replanning or iterative interaction with the user.
首先，大语言模型的响应延迟在决策流水线中引入了明显的滞后。具体而言，从自然语言理解到技能检索，再到最终生成执行序列的过程，需要不可忽视的初始化时间。这种延迟限制了系统的响应速度，特别是在需要快速决策的时间敏感型或动态环境中。当任务需要频繁重规划或与用户进行迭代交互时，这一问题尤为突出。


Second, the execution latency within OpenClaw affects the temporal continuity of robot behavior. Each skill invocation incurs a delay, and transitions between consecutive skills introduce additional gaps. As a result, the overall motion of the quadruped robot may appear fragmented rather than smooth and continuous. This limitation is especially evident in tasks that require fine-grained coordination or fluid motion, such as dynamic navigation or obstacle negotiation.
其次，OpenClaw 内部的执行延迟影响了机器人行为的时间连续性。每次调用技能都会产生延迟，且连续技能之间的转换会引入额外的间隙。因此，四足机器人的整体运动可能显得碎片化，而非平滑连续。这一局限性在需要精细协调或流畅运动的任务（如动态导航或避障）中表现得尤为明显。


## 6 Summary
## 6 总结


In this work, we present OpenGo, an embodied quadruped agent that integrates large language model reasoning with a structured and controllable skill-based execution framework. By introducing a customizable skill library, an LLM-based dispatcher, and a self-learning framework grounded in execution feedback and human interaction, OpenGo provides a practical approach to bridging high-level semantic understanding and low-level robot control.
在本文中，我们提出了 OpenGo，这是一个将大语言模型推理与结构化、可控的基于技能的执行框架相结合的具身四足智能体。通过引入可定制的技能库、基于 LLM 的调度器以及基于执行反馈和人类交互的自学习框架，OpenGo 为连接高层语义理解与底层机器人控制提供了一种实用的方法。


A key contribution of this work lies in constraining the role of the LLM to skill selection and parameter tuning, rather than direct generation of low-level actions. This design significantly improves the safety, interpretability, and deployability of LLM-driven embodied systems. Through deployment on the Unitree Go2 quadruped robot, we demonstrate that the proposed framework can support real-world task execution, skill switching, and natural-language-based human-robot interaction via a communication platform.
本工作的一个关键贡献在于将 LLM 的作用限制在技能选择和参数调整上，而非直接生成底层动作。这种设计显著提高了 LLM 驱动的具身系统的安全性、可解释性和可部署性。通过在宇树 Go2 四足机器人上的部署，我们证明了所提出的框架能够支持现实世界的任务执行、技能切换，以及通过通信平台进行的基于自然语言的人机交互。


While current limitations remain in system latency and execution continuity, the overall framework establishes a scalable foundation for extending embodied intelligence systems toward more complex, dynamic, and interactive environments. Future improvements in skill composition, real-time control, and adaptive learning are expected to further enhance the robustness and generalization capability of the system.
尽管目前在系统延迟和执行连续性方面仍存在局限，但该整体框架为将具身智能系统扩展至更复杂、动态和交互式的环境奠定了可扩展的基础。未来在技能组合、实时控制和自适应学习方面的改进，有望进一步增强系统的稳健性和泛化能力。


In summary, OpenGo highlights a feasible and effective pathway for combining large language models with structured robotic skill systems, moving toward more reliable and accessible embodied AI.
总之，OpenGo 突显了一条将大语言模型与结构化机器人技能系统相结合的可行且有效的途径，向着更可靠、更易用的具身智能迈进。
